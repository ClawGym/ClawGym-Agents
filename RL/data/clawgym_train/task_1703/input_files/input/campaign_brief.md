# Campaign Brief: Indonesia–Japan Cultural Ties (May)

Objective
- Build awareness among Indonesian young professionals and students (18–35) about shared history and modern collaboration between Indonesia and Japan.
- Drive sign-ups to a bilingual webinar series on culture, education, and innovation.

Audience
- Primary: Indonesian students and early-career professionals interested in culture, education, and technology.
- Secondary: Educators and community organizers engaged in cultural exchange.

Key Messages
- Mutual respect, pembelajaran bersama (learning together), and innovation rooted in heritage.
- Celebrate friendship through arts, education, business collaboration, and community.

Sensitivity & Risk Notes
- Avoid sensationalizing WWII-era trauma; frame milestones as reconciliation and forward-looking partnership.
- Use images and wording that respect both flags and national symbols; avoid militaristic iconography.
- Avoid stereotypes; represent diverse people and contexts.

Channels (see timeline.yaml for cadence)
- Instagram (visual storytelling, short captions, bilingual BI-first with occasional romaji Japanese terms)
- LinkedIn (professional angle: education, business, innovation)

Language & Tone
- Bahasa Indonesia first; occasional Japanese terms in romaji for friendliness.
- Warm, respectful, informative, and culturally mindful.

KPIs
- Engagement rate target: Instagram 5%+, LinkedIn 3%+
- Webinar sign-ups goal: 300 total

Call to Action
- Ajak audiens mendaftar webinar (register for the webinar) at the end of relevant posts.

Constraints
- Schedule posts on preferred days from timeline.yaml.
- Only plan within the campaign window in timeline.yaml; stick to the specified channels.
