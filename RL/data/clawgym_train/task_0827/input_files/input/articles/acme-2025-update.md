# Acme Corp 2025 Strategy & Operations Update

- Publication date: 2025-03-15  
- Authors: Corporate Strategy & Operations Team  
- Audience: Investors, partners, and employees

## Executive Summary

Acme Corp enters 2025 with a sharpened focus on resilient operations and customer-centric monetization. We are expanding our connected devices and analytics platform while transitioning key product lines to a usage-based pricing model to align costs with customer value delivered over time.

Global logistics remain volatile due to geopolitical tensions and extreme weather. To address this, we are accelerating nearshoring, dual/multi-sourcing, and digital visibility across our supply network. Our goal is to reduce lead time variability by 30% and maintain service continuity against disruptions.

## 2024 Performance Snapshot

- Revenue: $2.1B (+8% YoY); Connected services ARR: $210M (+22% YoY)
- On-time-in-full (OTIF): 93% (up from 90%)
- Average component lead time: 14 weeks (down from 18 weeks), with continued volatility in semiconductors

## 2025 Strategic Priorities

1. Scale the Acme IoT Platform and analytics add‑ons.
2. Transition 30% of eligible SKUs to a usage-based pricing model by Q4 2025.
3. Increase supply chain resilience through nearshoring, dual sourcing, and cyber hardening.
4. Improve working capital efficiency while preserving strategic safety stock for critical components.

## Monetization Shift: Usage-Based Pricing

We will move select connected hardware + software bundles to usage-based pricing (UBP), aligning price to metered outcomes (e.g., runtime hours, processed units, or sensor events). Anticipated benefits:

- Lowers customer upfront capex and reduces friction in pilots (“land and expand” motion).
- Improves lifetime value via telemetry-driven upsell (analytics tiers, premium SLAs).
- Smooths revenue recognition and enhances retention through value-linked pricing.

Considerations:

- Requires stronger device telemetry, entitlement management, and SLA enforcement.
- Heightens importance of reliable spare-parts logistics and maintenance responsiveness.

## Supply Chain Outlook and Resilience Actions

### Top Supply Chain Risks Observed for 2025

1. Geopolitical route disruptions (e.g., Suez/Red Sea) increasing transit times and insurance costs.
2. Semiconductor constraints in 28nm microcontrollers and power management ICs.
3. Tier‑2 supplier insolvency risk in plastics and fasteners segments as interest rates normalize.
4. Cyberattacks targeting logistics IT (TMS/WMS) and supplier OT environments.
5. Extreme weather (heatwaves, floods, storms) driving unplanned factory and port shutdowns.

### Mitigation Program (2025)

- Nearshoring: Shift assembly of select SKUs to Mexico and Eastern Europe; target 25% of volume by Q3.
- Dual/Multi‑Sourcing: Increase dual‑sourced critical BOM lines from 48% to 65% by Q4.
- Strategic Safety Stock: Maintain 2–4 weeks of buffer for microcontrollers, with dynamic policies tied to risk scores.
- Logistics Redundancy: Pre‑book capacity on alternative ocean and rail routes; scenario-based playbooks for rapid re‑routing.
- Cyber Hardening: Zero‑trust segmentation for plant OT networks; supplier cyber assessments and minimum controls for Tier‑1/Tier‑2.
- Financial Health Monitoring: Quarterly credit risk scans of sub‑tier suppliers; early‑warning indicators integrated into S&OP.
- End‑to‑End Visibility: Expand track‑and‑trace and ETA prediction; supplier portal for ASNs, constraints, and exception alerts.

## Digital & Data

- Deploy a supply chain control tower with predictive ETAs, weather/geopolitical overlays, and automated alerting.
- Integrate risk signals into S&OP/SIOP so demand/supply plans reflect disruption probabilities.

## ESG & Compliance

- Increase recycled content in enclosures.
- Expand Scope 3 supplier engagement with climate risk disclosures and severe-weather contingency plans.

## 2025 Financial Guidance (Non‑GAAP)

- Revenue growth: 7%–10%; Connected services ARR growth: 18%–22%.
- Gross margin: 44%–46% (UBP mix impact offset by services margin expansion).
- Capex focused on nearshoring and digital supply chain capabilities.
