Riverton Ledger Digital House Style

Voice and Tone
- Objective news reporting for a general audience.
- Clear, active sentences. Avoid jargon; define terms briefly when needed.
- Present multiple perspectives fairly. Attribute facts and opinions.

Story Structure
- Use a strong lead hook (question/statistic/scene/conflict). First sentence must be 25 words or fewer.
- Include core information (who, what, where, when, why) within the first 100 words.
- Use H2 subheadings approximately every 150–200 words; use H3s to break down complex sections.
- Paragraphs average 2–3 sentences; avoid blocks longer than 5 sentences.
- Include at least three direct quotes, distributed across the story. Vary quote length.
- Attribution format: "…," said Name, title/organization.
- Cite facts to source IDs in brackets, e.g., [S1], [S3]. Include a final “Sources” section mapping IDs to source titles.

Verification and Sources
- Prioritize primary sources (press releases, ordinances, official memos, transcripts) for at least 60% of referenced material.
- Use expert sources (academics, technical briefs, nonprofit leaders) for 20–30%.
- Limit secondary sources (journalism or summaries) to a maximum of 20%.
- Apply a three-layer fact-check: source credibility, information accuracy via cross-checks, and context integrity for quotes.

Digital Conventions
- Write descriptive H2/H3 headers using natural language.
- Integrate SEO keywords naturally (no stuffing). Use the primary keyword 1–2% of total word count.
- Do not include external links beyond the provided sources. Use source IDs only.
- Use American English, but the city name and organization titles should remain as provided.

Copy Editing
- Use AP-style capitalization for titles and organizations.
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above; always use numerals for percentages.
- Time and dates: Month Day, Year (e.g., October 15, 2026).
- Acronyms on first reference should be spelled out with acronym in parentheses if reused.
