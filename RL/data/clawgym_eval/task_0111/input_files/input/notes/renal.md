# Renal
NYC clinic case: hyperkalemia scenario review

Q: Indications for emergent dialysis (AEIOU)?
A: Acidosis (refractory), Electrolytes (severe hyperkalemia), Ingestion (toxins), Overload (pulmonary edema), Uremia (pericarditis, encephalopathy).

Q: Effect of aldosterone on nephron?
A: Increases Na+ reabsorption and K+/H+ secretion in the collecting duct principal and intercalated cells.

Q: Nephrotic vs nephritic key differences?
A: Nephrotic: proteinuria >3.5 g/day, edema, hyperlipidemia; Nephritic: hematuria, RBC casts, HTN, mild proteinuria.
