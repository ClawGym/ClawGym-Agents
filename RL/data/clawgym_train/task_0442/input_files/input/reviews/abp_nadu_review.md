# ABP Nadu Review — MindMaze

[p1] MindMaze bills itself as a psychological thriller but often reads more like a routine crime drama. The opening sequences set expectations the film never quite meets.

[p2] The final twist feels cheap and unearned, arriving with little setup and less impact.

[p3] Protagonists appear thinly drawn, and their motivations shift to serve the plot rather than arise organically.

[p4] Cinematography leans on moody compositions that overshadow substance and slow the pace to a crawl.

[p5] Several supposed reveals hinge on weak logic; a few transitions suggest outright plot holes.

[p6] While we try to avoid spoilers, it’s worth noting a mid-film reveal that undermines the stakes set early on.

[p7] Despite some craft, the film lands at a middling score, buoyed more by style than storytelling.

[p8] MindMaze ultimately leaves little to ponder after the credits, its ambitions outpacing execution.
