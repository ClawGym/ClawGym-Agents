Lifecycle Greenhouse Gas Emissions of Urban Bus Propulsion Technologies: A Meta-analysis

Authors: K. Alvarez, P. Singh, L. Trinh

Journal: Journal of Urban Transport Systems (2024)

DOI: 10.0000/juts.2024.045

Abstract

We synthesize 68 studies comparing lifecycle greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions of diesel and battery-electric transit buses. Across diverse grid mixes and duty cycles, the median lifecycle reduction for battery-electric buses (BEBs) relative to conventional diesel is approximately 30% (interquartile range: 22%–41%). For regions with electricity mixes comparable to Metrovale (circa 2024 estimated at ~45% renewable and low coal utilization), modeled lifecycle reductions cluster between 32% and 38%, provided batteries are manufactured under contemporary supply chains and amortized over typical service life. Sensitivity analyses show reductions persist even when battery manufacturing emissions are included; net outcomes rarely reverse sign unless grids are highly carbon-intensive.

Methods

We included peer-reviewed studies and government technical reports with transparent system boundaries (vehicle manufacturing, battery manufacturing, electricity generation, fuel production, vehicle operation). Random-effects models were applied to estimate pooled effects, with subgroup analyses by grid carbon intensity and battery chemistry.

Key Findings

  • Median BEB lifecycle GHG reduction vs. diesel: ~30%.
  • Expected reduction for ~45% renewable grids: 32%–38%.
  • Inclusion of battery manufacturing and replacement does not typically negate net reductions under mid-to-low carbon grids.
  • Reported outliers showing higher BEB emissions generally correspond to coal-dominant grids or atypical system boundaries.
