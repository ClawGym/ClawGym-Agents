Dear friends at the youth club,

I hope this finds you well. I am writing from my old garden in Dümbüllü where I have been watching the weather since my children were small and the apricot tree was just a stick. I kept two thermometers nailed to the same post, side by side, and I wrote numbers in a notebook and later into a small computer file my nephew helped me make. I would like to share a little summary with you, but I tend to ramble and I’m not sure what parts matter for young scientists these days. I want to know if the two thermometers agree most days, and if there is some drift over time that a simple line can show. If you can help me put it plainly and briefly, I will be grateful. I don’t want to overwhelm anyone, only to pass along a careful habit from an old heart who still remembers the first snow of 1979 like it was yesterday. Please make it short enough to read at your meeting, and gentle in tone. Thank you kindly.
