# Editorial Brief — Rooftop Roots

Theme:
- A city rooftop becomes a living canvas where neighbors cultivate plants, friendship, and second chances.

Tone & Style:
- Warm, grounded, contemporary slice-of-life with gentle optimism and subtle humor.
- Keep language accessible and inviting; avoid cynicism. Celebrate small wins and quiet perseverance.

Motifs:
- Gardening metaphors (roots, grafting, pruning, seedling care)
- Harbor weather and light (fog lifting, salt air, gull calls)
- Sensory details (soil texture, herb aromas, humming vents, distant harbor bells)

Narrative Goals:
- Deliver continuity across episodes with evolving relationships and a persistent project: restoring the Marigold rooftop garden.
- Introduce and manage hooks (Mystery, Foreshadowing, Conflict, Secret, Enigma, Crisis). Track creation and resolution by ID (H-###).
- Resolve at least one major hook by the final episode; leave at most three open.

Episode Cadence (guide, not a straitjacket):
1) Episode 1 — Arrival & spark: introduce characters, rooftop’s potential, first open hooks.
2) Episode 2 — Obstacles: permits, structural concerns, clashing visions; deepen a hook.
3) Episode 3 — Mid-turn: unlock a resource or truth; close one hook; complicate another.
4) Episode 4 — Rally: near-term goal such as a “Harbor Day” preview; stakes grow.
5) Episode 5 — Festival & closure: public showcase; resolve key threads; end on earned hope.

Continuity & Graph:
- Characters evolve gradually; note per-episode updates (skills learned, trust earned).
- Scenes rotate among rooftop, cafe “Quiet Tide,” and the community center; include occasional streetscapes.
- Hooks: create sparingly, maintain status, and close with intention. Aim to end with no more than three open.

Planned Initial Hooks (IDs reserved; refine during writing):
- H-001 (Mystery): The locked greenhouse on the far side of the roof—who has the key and what’s inside?
- H-002 (Conflict): The building owner fears the roof isn’t safe enough to handle raised beds.
- H-003 (Secret): Maya’s previous community garden proposal failed—why, and how does that shape her choices?
- H-004 (Foreshadowing): A rumor of a city micro-grant for green roofs; criteria unknown.

Resolution Intent:
- Close H-001 by or before Episode 3 (key found; contents revealed).
- Close H-004 in Episode 5 (grant decision).
- Ensure at least one hook resolves in the finale; keep open hooks ≤ 3 at the end.

Content & Conduct:
- All-ages friendly: no graphic or violent content; no strong profanity.
- Inclusive, respectful depictions of community and background.
- Keep prose in English.

Structural Requirements (for each episode):
- Include a “### Plot Summary” (≤ 120 words).
- Include a “### Complete Content” section with at least one markdown table that has a “Scene” column and uses pipes.
- Add “### Character Updates,” “### Hook Handling,” and “### Emotional Curve.”
- Maintain consistent hook IDs (H-001, H-002, …).

Quality Gates:
1) AI Review (six dimensions: Plot Coherence, Character Consistency, Hook Handling, Pacing Control, Emotional Curve, Innovation). Score out of 10; retry until ≥ 7 or max retries reached.
2) Human Approval (simulate as approved if AI passes).

Finale Expectations:
- Public moment (e.g., Harbor Day rooftop opening).
- Closure for at least one long-running mystery or conflict.
- A forward-looking image (e.g., seedlings set for autumn).
