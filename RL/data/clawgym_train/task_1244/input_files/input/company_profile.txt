Company: NimbusHR
Industry: B2B SaaS (HR technology)
Headcount: 210 (R&D 95, GTM 70, G&A 45)
Primary Markets: North America (88% of revenue), EMEA (10%), Other (2%)

Product Lines:
- Core HRIS (employee records, time-off, onboarding)
- Payroll (US multi-state payroll with tax filings and payments)
- Performance & OKRs (reviews, 1:1s, goals)
- People Analytics (dashboards and reporting)

Go-To-Market:
- Customer tiers: Enterprise (>$250k ACV), Mid-Market ($25k–$250k ACV), SMB ($2k–$25k ACV)
- Channels: Direct AEs (enterprise/mid-market), Certified partners (accountants, HR consultants), Self-serve (SMB)
- Contract structure: ~70% annual prepaid, ~20% multi-year with 8–12% discounts, ~10% monthly

Pricing Model:
- Per-employee-per-month base price for Core HRIS
- Add-on modules priced per employee (Payroll, Performance & OKRs, People Analytics)
- Usage drivers: active employees, payroll runs, analytics query volumes

Cost Structure Notes:
- Payroll COGS include payment rails (ACH fees, returns), tax service provider fees, and fraud loss reserves; these can spike during tax season and during bank fee changes.
- Support costs are more sensitive in SMB due to onboarding effort and payroll tax changes.
- Cloud infrastructure and data warehouse costs scale primarily with People Analytics and Core HRIS usage.

Strategic Context:
- Competitive set: Rippling, Gusto (SMB), Paycor (mid-market), BambooHR
- Differentiators: Native integrations to 60+ payroll and accounting systems, pre-built compliance packs
- Risks: Partner-led deals with deeper discounts, SMB support load, and payment-rail fee volatility in Payroll

- Use 2026 SaaS benchmarks for comparison (Gross Margin 70–85%, Operating Margin 15–30%, Net Margin 10–25% for mature; growth-stage operating margin can be -20% to +10%).
