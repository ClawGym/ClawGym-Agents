# Traffic of Forms — Draft Review (West Pier Gallery)

The exhibition "Traffic of Forms" at West Pier Gallery attempts to corral unruly publics into the language of tradition. The staging, with its stiff pageantry, resists the contemporary urge to blur art and life. When performers stepped into the plaza, their interventions brushed the sanctity of spectatorship with an uncomfortable informality that, in my view, bordered on discourtesy.

Still, the morning’s sequence carried enough compositional rigor to withstand the crowd’s milling and chatter. The scores were readable; the intentions—however brazenly public—were clear.
