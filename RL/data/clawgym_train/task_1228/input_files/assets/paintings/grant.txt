Duncan Grant’s bathers often propose the pool as a studio: a lucid basin for choreographing bodies and light. The paint handles flesh as a set of articulate planes, and the water becomes a companion medium—changing the rules of contour and reflection. If Bell cultivates the ethical quiet of interiors, Grant stages a lucid sociality: limbs echo, shadows interlock, and the ambient color is ceremonious without being stiff. The figures are at ease not because they are careless but because the composition permits them to be gathered into a shared grammar. The surface moves between shimmer and anchor, as if the painter wanted to hold a conversation between fleeting sensation and the endurance of form.
