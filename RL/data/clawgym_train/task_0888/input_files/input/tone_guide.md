Mid-Century Ad Voice Quick Guide

Core voice:
- Crisp, confident, and witty. Lead with the benefit.
- Short, musical clauses. A wink without snark.
- Concrete, sensory words (e.g., counters, mirrors, coffee, neon).

Avoid (anachronisms & modern jargon):
- synergy, pivot, A/B test, disrupt, content strategy, social media/socials, metrics, bandwidth, onboarding, influencer, startup, iterate (in the tech sense), feedback loop, sticky (in a marketing sense)

Prefer (period flavor):
- Madison Avenue, copy desk, radio spot, department store counter, counter girl, press run, mock-up, proof, layout, billboard, typewriter, soda fountain, supper club

Romance tone:
- Keep warmth and subtext; let gestures carry feeling. Avoid coarse slang.
- Let affection surface in light metaphors; no heavy-handed declarations mid-pitch.

Dialogue tips:
- Natural contractions; no corporate run-ons.
- One benefit per line; keep taglines tight.

Taglines:
- Favor vivid, 5–9 word lines that promise a benefit. If a slogan already fits and avoids the banned terms, preserve it.
