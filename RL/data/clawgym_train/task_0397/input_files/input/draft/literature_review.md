# Literature Review: Natural Law since 1990

This review surveys major developments in natural law theory and related debates. Foundational jurisprudential discussions such as Fuller [ref:NL007] are historically important, and classical accounts (e.g., Aquinas [ref:NL008]) frame contemporary disputes. Some constitutional theory critiques (e.g., Sorrell [ref:NL012]) challenge the scope of natural law in public reason.

Recent work explores moral knowledge in natural law [ref:NL004], but the selection below needs updating to prioritize post-1990 scholarship directly on natural law. A few citations here may be outdated or incomplete, including [ref:NL000].

## Thematic Overview

- Practical reason and basic goods frameworks remain central.
- Debates on epistemology and moral knowledge continue.
- Applications to constitutionalism and political theory appear in adjacent literature.
