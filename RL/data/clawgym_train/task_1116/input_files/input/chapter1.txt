Lila stared at the tiny dragon egg glowing on her windowsill. It ticked like a clock and smelled faintly of cinnamon. She wanted to keep it a secret, but when it wiggled and rolled across the desk, bumped her pencil case, and almost fell to the floor, she yelped and her brother rushed in to see what was going on. He thought it was a weird rock until it chirped. They both froze. The egg cracked open with a soft sigh, and a snout peeked out, shiny and blue as a marble. Lila whispered, hello, and the tiny dragon squeaked, sneezed a sparkle, and sneezed again even louder while flapping its damp wings wildly today. It smelled like toast. Their cat, Muffin, crept in to investigate, but the dragon sneezed a tiny puff of smoke that made the cat blink in surprise and back out of the room without a single meow.
