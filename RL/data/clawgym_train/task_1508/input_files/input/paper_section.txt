We introduce AdaShift, an optimizer that augments adaptive moment methods with a lightweight curvature modulation term. Given gradients $g_t$, we compute first and second moments $m_t=\beta m_{t-1}+(1-\beta)g_t$ and $v_t=\gamma v_{t-1}+(1-\gamma)g_t^2$, and update parameters by $\theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta\,\frac{m_t}{\sqrt{v_t}+\epsilon}\,(1+\alpha\Delta_t)$, where $\Delta_t$ estimates local curvature via two finite-difference probes every 50 steps. The probes add $<1\%$ compute overhead and are skipped when gradient variance is low. On ImageNet-1k with ViT-B/16 using batch size 1024, learning rate $3\times10^{-4}$, weight decay $0.05$, drop\_path $0.1$, and a cosine schedule for 300 epochs, AdaShift reaches $80.1\%$ top-1 accuracy versus $78.6\%$ for AdamW \cite{loshchilov2019adamw} under identical seeds (5). The $95\%$ confidence interval is $[79.8,80.4]$, and convergence improves with a $12\%$ reduction in the area under the loss curve. Wall-clock training completes in $0.89\times$ the time of AdamW despite $0.7\%$ overhead, yielding $1.12\times$ effective speedup. On CIFAR-100 with ResNet-50 \cite{he2016deep}, AdaShift obtains $84.7\%$ versus $83.2\%$. Ablations show that removing $\Delta_t$ removes most gains, while reducing probe frequency modestly lowers accuracy but preserves stability. Our implementation is integrated into model\_v1 on PyTorch 2.2, with scripts that reproduce results. For language modeling on WikiText-103, perplexity improves from $14.2$ to $13.6$ with unchanged regularization. We follow standard baselines \cite{kingma2014adam,dosovitskiy2020vit} and report mean$\pm$std over 5 runs, with $95\%$ bootstrap intervals. For R\&D reproducibility, all seeds and hyperparameters are in Appendix~A.
