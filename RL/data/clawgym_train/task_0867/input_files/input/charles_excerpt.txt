On the first day of kindergarten, Laurie came home with a swaggering air and a voice that sounded very grown-up.
He announced that he had renounced his baby clothes and would wear only jeans.
The next day he said Charles was fresh and answered the teacher insolently.
By Friday there was a ruckus in class, and Charles had to stand in a corner.
On Monday, Laurie reported a reformation: Charles had been helpful and polite, at least for a day.
But by Tuesday, the swaggering stories returned.
