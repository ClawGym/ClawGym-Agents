Brand Voice Guidelines and Compliance Rules

Overview
Use clear, benefit-led language tailored to each platform. Be truthful, specific, and respectful. Avoid clickbait and any phrasing that could be misleading or non-compliant.

Brand Voice by Brand
- BrewBright
  - Tone: Warm, craft-forward, sustainable
  - Style: Descriptive sensory cues (aroma, roast, origin), concise benefits, community-minded impact
- StrideFlow
  - Tone: Energetic, performance-focused, down-to-earth
  - Style: Plain-language performance claims anchored in testing, everyday athlete inclusivity
- TaskPilot
  - Tone: Helpful, concise, confident but not hypey
  - Style: Outcome-oriented, simple verbs, time-savings and clarity

Compliance Rules
Rule 1 — Banned Phrases
Do not use any of the following phrases (case-insensitive):
- "100% guaranteed"
- "miracle cure"
- "best ever"
- "get rich quick"
- "no risk"
- "instant results"
- "risk-free"
- "overnight results"

Rule 2 — Capitalization
Do not use excessive capitalization. Avoid ALL CAPS for words longer than 3 letters, and avoid shouting-like styling.

Rule 3 — Misleading or Exaggerated Claims
Do not promise outcomes that are unprovable or unrealistic. Use qualified, supportable statements (e.g., “in internal testing,” “up to X based on…”) and avoid superlatives without context.

Rule 4 — Health-Related Claims
No disease treatment, prevention, or weight-loss claims. Do not imply medical or therapeutic benefit.

Rule 5 — Google Ads Headlines Punctuation
Do not use exclamation marks in Google headlines. Keep headlines professional and clear.

Rule 6 — Google Ads Short Headlines
For each product, include at least two Google headlines that are 30 characters or fewer.

Rule 7 — Facebook/Meta Personal Attributes
Do not assert or imply sensitive personal attributes (e.g., health status, financial status, race, religion). Avoid calling out individuals based on such attributes.

Rule 8 — Offer Clarity
State offers clearly and accurately. If a discount or trial is mentioned, include key terms (e.g., “first order,” “14-day free trial”). No hidden conditions.

Rule 9 — Competitor and Superiority Claims
Do not name competitors or claim superiority without substantiation. Prefer neutral, benefit-led comparisons or none at all.

Rule 10 — Sensitive Content and Body Imagery
Avoid sensational or demeaning content. If using before/after concepts, do not depict body or health transformations; focus on product, workflow, or environment changes.

Rule 11 — Brand Voice Consistency
Match the tone and style for each brand as defined above. Avoid slang that clashes with brand voice. Keep language inclusive and respectful.

Rule 12 — Privacy and Data Claims
Do not imply that user data is sold or shared improperly. Avoid absolute privacy claims; describe practices factually if referenced.

Platform Notes
- Facebook/Instagram: Avoid implying sensitive attributes about the audience. Ensure claims are supportable and respectful.
- Google: Headlines must be clear, concise, and free of exclamation marks. Include at least two headlines at or under 30 characters per product.
- General: No clickbait, no misleading urgency. Be specific, accurate, and transparent.
