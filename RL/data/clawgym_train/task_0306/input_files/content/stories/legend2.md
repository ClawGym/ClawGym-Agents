title: The Silent Bell

When the storm of 1723 fell upon the valley, the tower bell would not ring.
The keeper tugged the rope until his palms bled, but the iron tongue stayed still.
Only at dawn, when the wind gentled, did a single toll carry over the fields—said to mark a vow broken and a debt come due.
