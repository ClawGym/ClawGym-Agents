# Crawlspace Moisture Control Guidelines (Local Reference)

## G1: Target Conditions
- Aim to keep crawlspace relative humidity (RH) under 60% to reduce mold and condensation risk.
- Monitor RH daily; consistent levels over 60% indicate elevated moisture.

## G2: Red Flags and Triggers
- RH \u2265 70% for 3 or more consecutive days: High-priority trigger for immediate dehumidification and ground moisture control.
- Standing water or damp soil: Investigate drainage, downspouts, and consider sump pump or perimeter drainage.
- Musty odor: Likely microbial growth; assess ventilation and surfaces for moisture.
- Unsealed foundation vents letting in humid outdoor air: Consider sealing and conditioning the crawlspace.

## G3: Remediation Options and Prioritization
- Vapor barrier: Install a minimum 6-mil polyethylene ground cover, overlap seams, and seal edges to walls/piers (high priority if damp soil or RH triggers).
- Dehumidification: Install a crawlspace-rated dehumidifier sized to the area; set to maintain RH < 60%.
- Vent sealing and air sealing: Close foundation vents and seal penetrations to limit humid air intrusion.
- Drainage improvements: Extend downspouts away from foundation; add perimeter drain or sump pump if water intrusion persists.

## G4: Monitoring and Follow-up
- Keep logging daily RH and temperature after remediation.
- Reassess after 2 weeks; if RH remains \u2265 60%, adjust dehumidifier setpoint or add measures.
