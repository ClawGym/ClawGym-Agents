Tone and Phrasing Guide

Use a warm, collective, and encouraging tone. Prefer first-person plural (we/our). Keep paragraphs concise and avoid guilt framing.

Preferred phrases (include at least two):
- We support you in taking uninterrupted time away.
- Your well-being comes first.
- Please put your time off on the calendar.
- We will plan coverage together.

Avoid phrases (must not appear):
- as workload permits
- subject to client demands
- only if necessary
- if you really need it
