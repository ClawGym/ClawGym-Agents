# Chapter 1: Origins of the Edit

### Scene 1: Lab demo
In the teaching lab, the protagonist demonstrates CRISPR editing using a viral vector to deliver the nuclease. They prepare reaction mixes and set up PCR to confirm insertions, walking students through the controls. The instructor mentions off-target effects and reminds everyone to check guide RNA specificity. The CRISPR demo sparks questions about efficiency, and the team runs a second PCR to validate the band pattern. While the viral vector transduction looks smooth, concerns about off-target edits linger in the discussion, and a brief sidebar covers error rates and how sequencing can catch mistakes.

### Scene 2: Ethics debate
At a community forum, a heated discussion erupts over germline editing and the societal risks of a gene drive designed to collapse invasive pests. A bioethicist argues that germline changes pass to future generations, while an ecologist worries that a gene drive could spread beyond the target region. A clinician brings up mitochondrial replacement therapy and the limited scope of such interventions. The audience returns to the topic of germline edits and whether regulation should distinguish between therapy and enhancement.

### Scene 3: Clinic consult
During a fertility consult, the couple looks over a karyotype with their clinician. The conversation pivots to whether CRISPR-based somatic therapies could help a relative with a blood disorder. The clinician explains that the karyotype offers a broad view of chromosomal changes but not single-nucleotide variants, and underscores that heritable germline changes are not part of this visit. A follow-up is scheduled after additional lab work.
