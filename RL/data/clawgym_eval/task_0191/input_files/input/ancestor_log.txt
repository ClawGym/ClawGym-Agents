Logbook excerpts of Nathaniel Archer, Able Seaman, 1847

[1847-03-12] QUOTE: "The wind freshened by dusk; green water rolled over the fo'c'sle."
[1847-04-02] QUOTE: "We limped into Valparaíso harbor with one topsail to our name."
[1847-05-19] QUOTE: "A long swell ran from the west all afternoon."
[1847-06-07] QUOTE: "Took on fresh water and salt beef in a friendly port."
[1847-07-18] QUOTE: "Soundings kept us honest as we felt our way into the channel at dawn."
[1847-08-23] QUOTE: "A sudden squall veered the wind abeam and had us reefed in minutes."
[1847-09-14] QUOTE: "We rode out a hard storm off Cape Horn; timbers groaned but held."
[1847-10-01] QUOTE: "Lanterns flickered along the outer harbor; the crew fell silent."
