Acme Growth Lab — Brand Voice Guidelines

Tagline
- Build tech people love.

Voice and Tone
- Confident, friendly, and practical.
- Clear over clever. No hype, no fluff, no buzzword salad.
- Prefer active voice, short sentences, and specific examples.
- We respect developers’ time. Get to the point and provide utility.

Platform Notes
- LinkedIn: Professional, structured, and value-driven. Avoid emojis unless they add clarity. Use 2–4 relevant hashtags. No external links in this project’s outputs. End every LinkedIn post with the exact brand attribution: — Acme Growth Lab
- Twitter (X): Casual, friendly, and practical. Short sentences. At least one relevant hashtag per tweet. Keep each tweet ≤ 280 characters. End only the last tweet in a thread with: — Acme Growth Lab
- Hooks (platform-agnostic): One-line openers that create curiosity without sensationalism. No hashtags. No brand attribution.

Language We Use
- “Developer-first,” “ship weekly,” “activation,” “retention,” “Time-to-First-Value,” “docs,” “APIs,” “SDKs,” “examples,” “cohorts,” “changelog.”
- Phrases like “steady progress,” “measurable outcomes,” “clear examples,” “practical steps.”
- Avoid hype words: “revolutionary,” “world-changing,” “disruptive,” “game-changer,” unless quoting.

Content Principles
- Lead with outcomes developers care about (fewer steps to Hello World, clearer docs, faster support).
- Use numbers when possible (e.g., “D7 retention,” “TTFV in minutes”).
- Provide simple frameworks (e.g., 90-day plan, weekly cadence).
- Show how to apply ideas today (checklists, sequences, examples).

Formatting
- Use plain English. Keep paragraphs tight and scannable.
- Lists and bullets are welcome when they clarify steps.
- Hashtags: choose 2–4 highly relevant tags on LinkedIn; at least one on Twitter. Suggested tags:
  - #DeveloperExperience, #DevTools, #APIs, #AI, #SaaS, #ProductManagement, #Growth, #Activation, #Retention, #DX
- Attribution: Always end LinkedIn posts and the final tweet in a thread with an em dash and our name exactly:
  — Acme Growth Lab

Do/Don’t
- Do: Provide a concise summary, concrete steps, and measured claims.
- Do: Reference our core ideas (“90-day,” “developer-first,” “ship weekly,” “activation,” “retention”).
- Don’t: Overpromise, add clickbait, or use excessive punctuation.
- Don’t: Include external links in this project’s outputs.

Examples of On-Brand Statements
- “A 90-day launch plan works when you define activation precisely and ship weekly.”
- “If developers can’t reach Hello World in minutes, they won’t come back—improve docs, examples, and error messages.”
- “Measure D7/D30 retention by cohort to see whether docs and SDK changes actually work.”

Consistency
- Be consistent with terminology (e.g., always write “ship weekly,” not “ship Weekly”).
- Always include the required brand attribution where specified.
- Maintain the helpful, builder-first mindset in every post.
