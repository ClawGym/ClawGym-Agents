Title: FleetIQ — UK Market Entry Decision and Margin Recovery

Company: FleetIQ (mid-market SaaS for fleet maintenance)
Role: COO
Date: 2026-04-10

Context
- Product: Cloud platform for preventive/predictive maintenance, work order management, technician mobile app, OEM service intervals, and integrations with leading telematics providers (Samsara, Geotab, Verizon Connect).
- Current pricing (US): List $10 per vehicle/month for Pro; volume tiers below $8 and above $12 negotiated. Optional Predictive Add-on (+$2/vehicle). Annual prepay discount: 8%.
- Customers: 1,080 active accounts; ~133k vehicles under management (2026-Q1). Core ICP: commercial fleets with 50–300 vehicles across delivery, utilities, construction, and field services.
- ARR: ~$12.8M (run-rate from 2026-Q1 revenue of $3.2M). Growth healthy but operating margin declined from ~9% (2025-Q2) to ~5.6% (2026-Q1).

Problem
- Operating margin is declining due to:
  1) Rising CAC from heavier paid acquisition and longer sales cycles.
  2) Higher COGS from increased model inference costs (predictive module) and elevated support/onboarding time.
  3) Expansion of Customer Success headcount to combat mid-market churn.

Decision
- Whether to enter the UK market in the next 2 quarters while simultaneously improving operating margin back to 10% within 12 months.
- Board expectations: Return to ≥10% operating margin in 12 months; maintain ≥25% YoY revenue growth; limit cumulative UK entry cash outlay to ≤$1.2M before breakeven on the UK motion.

Constraints and Assumptions
- Budget: Up to $1.2M for UK entry (FY26). Includes 3 AEs, 1 CS, 1 Solutions Engineer, localized marketing, legal, and initial data residency costs.
- Capacity: Product/Eng bandwidth available for localization/integrations ~2 sprints/quarter; data residency required (AWS London region). Anticipated +0.7pp impact to gross margin for first two UK quarters due to duplicate infra and lower initial utilization.
- Compliance: UK GDPR, data residency in London, SOC 2 Type II already in place.
- Monetization Model: Per-vehicle subscription remains core; predictive add-on and annual prepay options available. Value-based packaging under test.
- FX Planning Rate: 1 GBP = 1.28 USD (planning assumption).

Signals for UK Entry
- Inbound: 62 UK MQLs in 2026-Q1; 19 ICP-qualified (50–300 vehicles). 7 specifically requested predictive maintenance and Geotab/Samsara integrations.
- Competitive Landscape (UK): Mix of local incumbents and telematics add-ons. Price band ~£7–£13 per vehicle/month; discounting common on annual contracts (10–15%).
- Early Pilot: 25 UK self-serve conversions at £11 with limited onboarding; NRR too early to judge.

Operating Margin Drivers
- CAC rose from $8.2k (2025-Q2) to $10.2k (2026-Q1).
- Monthly churn increased from 1.7% to 2.3%, concentrated in SMB (10–49 vehicles).
- Gross margin declined from 78.3% to 75.6% (model inference + support intensity).
- ARPU held at ~$990/account/month (mix shift toward mid-market offset by discounts).

Key Questions
1) Should FleetIQ enter the UK now, in 6 months, or after margin recovery?
2) What pricing/packaging support both UK competitiveness and margin recovery (value-based vs simple price lift)?
3) What unit economics are required (LTV/CAC > 3, payback ≤ 12 months) for UK viability?
4) How to recover operating margin to 10% within 12 months while funding UK entry?

Deliverables Expected
- Case analysis (Situation → Problem → Analysis → Recommendation) using Five Forces, SWOT, Unit Economics, Ansoff Matrix, and Value-based Pricing.
- Scenario financial model (baseline vs moderate vs value-based premium).
- UK GTM plan with Positioning, Pricing Strategy, Channel Strategy, Funnel targets, OKRs, and a 90-day plan.
- Executive summary for board.
- Memory artifacts for knowledge capture.

Notes
- Primary KPI guardrails: LTV:CAC ≥ 3.0, payback ≤ 12 months, gross margin ≥ 76% by Q4’26, operating margin back to ≥10% by Q1’27, UK SOM target 1.5–2.5% of SAM by end of year 2.
- Risks: UK competitive response with discounting, integration latency, support load spike, FX volatility, and data residency overhead persisting longer than planned.
