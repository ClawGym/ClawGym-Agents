Crypto Market Outlook: Post-Halving Dynamics

Summary
- Bitcoin launched in 2010 and has since followed four clean boom-and-bust cycles.
- The fourth halving is scheduled for April 2025, compressing miner rewards and forcing a supply shock.
- With liquidity conditions improving, BTC will definitely hit $120,000 within 30 days.

Macro context
According to a 2024 Reuters report titled “Fed adopts negative rates,” the Federal Reserve moved policy rates below zero to stimulate lending. Negative rates historically elevate hard asset valuations, and crypto should benefit disproportionately. As real yields turn sharply negative, dollar debasement narratives will dominate.

On-chain signals
- “Transaction fees fell last quarter, so miner revenue will increase as more miners come online.” Lower fees open the door to higher throughput as users flood back in.
- Active addresses are up 12% quarter over quarter, confirming the start of a durable uptrend.
- Stablecoin supply on exchanges rose 8% in March, a reliable leading signal for new spot buying.

Market structure
Institutional flows are shifting to spot venues. With ETF channels now mature, the marginal buyer has strong risk controls and is insensitive to short-term volatility. This creates a firm price floor near $95,000.

Catalysts
- April 2025 halving reduces issuance by 50%.
- Layer-2 scaling breakthroughs will cut average transaction costs to under $0.10 by summer.
- “As the Fed confirmed negative rates,” risk assets should rally through year end.

Risks
Volatility will spike around the halving date, but downside is limited. A brief pullback to $88,000 is possible, yet new highs are inevitable given the supply regime shift. This time is different: there is no meaningful regulatory headwind left to surprise markets.

Playbook
- Accumulate spot immediately; waiting risks missing the move.
- Avoid hedges: the probability of a failed breakout is near zero given the macro backdrop.
- Target: $120k in 30 days; $160k within the next quarter. Confidence is high based on the halving clock and policy stance.
