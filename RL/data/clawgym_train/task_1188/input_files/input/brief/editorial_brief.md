Editorial Brief: Interpreting Ancient Myths

Project goal
We aim to commission a comprehensive, accessible study that explains how ancient myths have been interpreted and understood within their original contexts and in later readings. The book should synthesize methods clearly for serious general readers.

Audience
Serious general nonfiction readers; advanced undergraduates and curious lay readers. Clarity and readability are important.

Hard constraints
- Manuscript due within 10 months of contract.
- Single-volume manuscript of 300–450 pages, with illustrative figures permitted.

Priorities (in order of importance)
1) Schedule adherence (10-month manuscript deadline is binding).
2) Audience accessibility and clarity of method explanation.
3) Cross-cultural breadth (desirable if achievable without jeopardizing the schedule).

Decision rule
Select the candidate whose proposal satisfies the hard 10-month deadline. If both satisfy the deadline, prefer accessibility and then cross-cultural breadth.

Tone for correspondence
Professional, concise, appreciative, and clear about next steps.

Editor
Miriam Hale, Senior Nonfiction Editor
