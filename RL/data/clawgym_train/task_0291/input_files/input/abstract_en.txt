We present an intuitive derivation of symmetry breaking in a scalar field theory that keeps the mathematics transparent while emphasizing creative physical pictures. Starting from the Lagrangian L = T - V with a quartic potential, we show how infinitesimal deformations pick out a preferred direction without imposing it by hand. The Hamiltonian perspective clarifies stability and fluctuations across phase space, and we compare how different gauge choices reveal complementary insights in an SU(2) toy model. In the path integral, we identify which saddle-point contributions dominate and how the vacuum is selected dynamically. Along the way we isolate the minimal assumptions needed for the argument to hold, and we note where dimensional analysis and scaling give the correct qualitative behavior before any heavy computation. Our goal is to sharpen intuition without sacrificing rigor; where possible we keep ℏ explicit to track the classical limit.
