Editorial Voice and Style
- Voice: Warm, insightful, and conversational. Write like a seasoned critic who loves films but respects reader time.
- Tense: Prefer present tense for plot description and analysis.
- Focus: Emphasize themes, craft, performances, and how the film feels to watch. Avoid plot dumps.
- Clarity: Short, active sentences; trim jargon unless defined in context.
- Length: 180–250 words for the review body (not counting headline, outline, hook, or CTA).

Spoiler Policy
- Absolutely no major spoilers. Only set up the premise and discuss tone, themes, and craft.
- Never reveal late twists, identity of culprits, or precise outcomes of climactic scenes.
- If referencing late-film elements, keep them abstract and consequence-free.
- Begin every review with a line starting exactly with: Spoiler-safe:
  - Example: Spoiler-safe: This review avoids late-plot reveals and major twists.

Required Formatting (per film)
1) Review text (structure and order):
   - Line 1: Spoiler-safe: <one brief assurance sentence>.
   - Line 2: Hook (1–2 sentences) that frames why the film matters now.
   - Line 3+: Review body bringing together perspective on themes, performances, direction, cinematography, sound/original score, pacing, and overall takeaway. Keep between 180–250 words total (including the Spoiler-safe line and hook).
2) Headline: 6–12 words; natural, no clickbait; include a keyword only if it fits.
3) Outline: Exactly 5 bullet points; each point should be 6–14 words and cover premise, craft, performances, tone/themes, and verdict.
4) Hook: 1–2 sentences (must match the one under the Spoiler-safe line).
5) CTA: One sentence inviting readers to react, share, or recommend related titles.
6) Hashtags: 8–12 items; all must start with #; mix film-specific and thematic tags; avoid duplicates across one film; use lowercase words separated by nothing (no spaces).

SEO and Keyword Usage
- Use the provided keyword list across the set of reviews and headlines; distribute naturally (do not stuff).
- Each keyword must appear at least once across the combined review_text and headline fields for all films.
- Prefer semantically appropriate placement: e.g., “cinematography” when discussing visuals; “original score” in sound section; “sci-fi thriller” for tone/genre; “character-driven” for performance/arc emphasis; “streaming release” only when referencing distribution context.

Tone and Professionalism
- Use American English and clean punctuation.
- Do not copy the notes verbatim; transform into polished prose with your own phrasing.
- Keep comparisons to other films minimal and non-derivative.
- Respect names and titles; verify spellings from notes.csv.
- Avoid hedging phrases like “maybe” or “kind of” unless for rhetorical effect.

Quality Checklist Before Finalizing
- Spoiler-safe line present and exact prefix used.
- Hook is 1–2 sentences; CTA is exactly one sentence.
- Review word count is 180–250 words total.
- Headline length 6–12 words, reads naturally.
- Outline contains exactly 5 bullets.
- 8–12 hashtags, all start with #, relevant and varied.
- At least one of the provided keywords used appropriately in each review set.
- Maintain respectful, constructive, and reader-first tone.
