Project: Westfield Public Library Renovation
Obligee/Owner: City of Westfield Public Works
Delivery Method: Design-Bid-Build (single-prime general contractor)

Bond Requirements:
- Bid Bond: 5 percent of the bid price. Use AIA A310 or equivalent. Penal sum form acceptable. The bid bond must be included with the bid submission.
- Performance Bond: 100 percent of the contract price. Use AIA A312 (2010 or later) or an equivalent form acceptable to the City.
- Payment Bond: 100 percent of the contract price. Use AIA A312 (2010 or later) or an equivalent form acceptable to the City.
- Surety Qualifications: Surety must be listed on the U.S. Treasury Department Circular 570 and licensed to do business in the project jurisdiction. Attorneys-in-fact must provide a valid digital or wet-signed power of attorney.
- Electronic Bonds: Electronic surety bonds are accepted via Surety2000 or as a digitally executed PDF with verifiable signatures and power of attorney.
- Substitutions: Subcontractor default insurance is not accepted as a substitute for the required payment bond.

Bid Deadline:
- Date: 2026-05-20
- Late bids, or bids without the required bid bond, will be rejected as non-responsive.

Claims and Notice Windows (expected forms based on AIA A312 concepts and City preferences):
- Performance Bond:
  - Owner will provide written notice of contractor default to the contractor and surety after giving any required cure opportunity stated in the contract.
  - Owner agrees to make remaining contract balance available to the surety for completion.
  - Surety is expected to acknowledge the claim promptly and begin its investigation within a short period. The City’s preference is acknowledgement within 7 calendar days and a position letter within 30 calendar days after receipt of sufficient documentation.
  - Available surety responses may include: financing the contractor, tendering a completion contractor, undertaking performance itself, or denying the claim with reasons.
- Payment Bond:
  - First-tier claimants with a direct contract with the general contractor generally do not need to give a preliminary notice before filing a claim, but must document last furnishing and amount due.
  - Second-tier claimants (subs or suppliers without a direct contract with the general contractor) must give written notice to the contractor and surety within 90 days from last furnishing of labor or materials.
  - Suits on the payment bond may not be filed before 90 days from last furnishing and must be filed within 1 year from last furnishing, consistent with common public works requirements. No mechanics’ liens are allowed on this public project, so the payment bond is the primary remedy for unpaid claimants.

Documentation Expectations (owner and surety preferences):
- Schedule Management: Maintain a current CPM schedule with monthly updates, critical path identification, and recovery plans as needed.
- Progress Documentation: Daily reports, photo logs, and quality control records should be kept current and made available upon request.
- Payment Documentation: Monthly pay applications must include sworn statements, conditional and unconditional lien waivers as applicable, updated subcontractor and supplier lists with contact information, and evidence of payment flowing downstream.
- Change Management: Written change directives, signed change orders, cost backup, and schedule impact analyses are required for all changes.
- Compliance: Prevailing wage compliance and certified payrolls are required; submit weekly payroll reports to the owner’s representative.
- Safety: Maintain a site-specific safety plan and incident logs; report incidents promptly per contract requirements.
- Communication: The City prefers a pre-claim coordination meeting prior to any formal declaration of default and encourages early notice to the surety when performance concerns arise.

Owner Preferences and Administrative Notes:
- Bond forms should not include rider language that materially alters the owner’s rights without prior written approval.
- The City prefers local points of contact for the surety, clear escalation paths, and a single email address for claims notices.
- For performance bond claims, the City’s preferred sequence is: notice of concerns, cure opportunity, default notice, surety involvement, and an agreed path to completion that minimizes service disruption to the library.
- Liquidated damages, if applicable under the contract, are enforceable per the General Conditions.
- Insurance requirements are separate from bonding requirements and do not replace the bonds.

Submission Checklist for Bonds:
