# Design Brief — Variable-Stiffness Liner Study

## Overview
We are developing a variable-stiffness socket liner that adapts to tissue load throughout the day. The socket liner aims to reduce peak pressures and improve donning comfort. The silicone sleeve concept (i.e., the socket liner) uses zoned materials to redistribute load. Our objective is to validate comfort, stability, and durability in a clinic-ready pilot.

This study will evaluate the silicone sleeve concept with repeatable protocols and instrumentation. ## User Needs
- Lower peak pressures over bony prominences without compromising suspension.
- Maintain proprioception and allow compatible signals for myoelectric sensors used in some upper limb systems.
- Simplify fitting adjustments while remaining compatible with current fabrication workflows.

## Test Plan
We will use pressure map instrumentation in the test socket to record peak and mean pressure during level walking and stairs. We will capture residual limb geometry with a 3D scan and repeat scans post-session to assess volume change. EMG sensors (where applicable) will be monitored to ensure signal quality is not degraded. A 3D scan will also be used to verify consistent donning between trials.

Protocol summary (v2.1):
- Fit participant with the liner and test socket.
- Collect baseline pressure map and EMG sensors data over a 10-minute walk.
- Swap stiffness zones per condition set and repeat.
- Document any discomfort and adjust as needed.

## Risks
- Excessive liner stiffness could increase peak pressures.
- Incompatibility with existing suspension systems.
- Signal interference with myoelectric sensors in mixed-use cases.
