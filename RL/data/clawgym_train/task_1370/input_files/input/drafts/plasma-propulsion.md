# Electric Propulsion and Plasma Plumes

Electric propulsion plasma devices—like Hall thrusters and ion engines—convert electrical power into directed momentum. The exhaust forms a plasma plume that can interact with spacecraft surfaces and instruments. Understanding electric propulsion plasma behavior helps engineers predict plume expansion, erosion, and electromagnetic emissions.

This overview connects basic plasma parameters with design trade-offs. We discuss how magnetic fields and discharge voltages shape performance, and why plume diagnostics (probes, spectroscopy) matter for validation. Future posts will link electric propulsion plasma insights to mission constraints and in-situ measurements.
