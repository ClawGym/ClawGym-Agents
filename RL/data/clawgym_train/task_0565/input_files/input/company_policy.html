Acme Corp Code of Conduct — Q2 2026 Update

Effective: June 1, 2026 • Owner: Compliance & Security

Summary of Changes:
  • Security: mandatory phishing drills; device encryption required; quarterly third‑party access reviews.
  • Data Handling: PII retention reduced to 18 months; stricter log redaction; clearer labeling for data classification.
  • Scope clarified for contractors and vendors handling customer data.

Scope

This company policy applies to all Acme Corp employees, contractors, interns, and third‑party vendors who access Acme systems or customer information. It supplements employment agreements and local regulations. Where conflicts arise, the stricter requirement governs.

Managers are responsible for reinforcing this code of conduct during onboarding, quarterly team reviews, and annual performance cycles.

Security

Access Control

  • Two‑factor authentication (2FA) is required for all production and admin accounts by June 30, 2026.
  • Password managers are mandatory; shared credentials must be rotated every 90 days.
  • Third‑party access is reviewed quarterly; dormant accounts are removed within 7 days.

Training & Reporting

  • Security awareness training is assigned quarterly; phishing simulations run monthly.
  • Suspected incidents must be reported within 1 hour to security@acme.example.
  • Remote work requires full‑disk encryption and screen‑lock timers ≤ 5 minutes.

Data Handling

Acme’s Data Handling standards protect customer privacy and ensure regulatory compliance (GDPR, CCPA). Key definitions: Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI) must be stored and processed with heightened safeguards.

Classification & Labeling

  • All documents and datasets must be labeled as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted.
  • Restricted data requires encrypted storage and audited access logs.

Retention & Minimization

  • PII retention is reduced to 18 months unless contractual or legal obligations require longer.
  • Collect only the minimum necessary attributes for a stated purpose; delete unused fields during quarterly reviews.

Storage, Transfer, & Logging

  • All transfers of Confidential or Restricted data must use TLS 1.2+ and approved key management.
  • Application logs must redact tokens, secrets, and full PII; store only hashed or truncated identifiers.

Data subject requests (access, correction, erasure) must be acknowledged within 72 hours and fulfilled within 30 days.

Professional Conduct

Employees must act ethically, avoid conflicts of interest, and report violations. Harassment and discrimination are strictly prohibited. This code of conduct is a living document and will be revisited each quarter.

Contact

Questions about this company policy or the Acme Corp Code of Conduct may be directed to the Compliance Office or Security Guild.
