# FlowFox — Friendly SaaS for freelancers and micro teams

Overview
- Product: Lightweight SaaS that handles clients, projects, invoices, and polite payment reminders in one place.
- Audience: Freelancers, solo creators, small agencies (2–10 people) across the Balkans and broader CEE.
- Promise: “Do your craft; FlowFox handles the admin.” Fast setup, zero clutter, and a warm tone.

Core Product Facts (for messaging)
- Onboarding: ~2 minutes from sign-up to first invoice.
- Trial: 14-day free trial, no credit card required.
- Pricing: From €9/month, cancel anytime.
- Integrations: Stripe, PayPal (optional).
- Privacy & Data: EU servers (Frankfurt, DE), GDPR-compliant, export anytime (PDF/CSV).
- Smart Reminders: Friendly, scheduled payment nudges that stop the moment an invoice is paid.
- Extras: Tags for clients/projects, quick filters, PDF export, dark mode.

Positioning
- FlowFox is not an all‑in‑one ERP. It’s intentionally simple and friendly. It saves time without forcing new workflows.
- Differentiators: humane tone, fast onboarding, gentle reminders that protect client relationships, and clear pricing.

Tone of Voice
- Casual, warm, and direct. Internet-native vibe without slang overload.
- Speak to one person (“you”), never corporate plural.
- No emojis and no hashtags.
- Use Latin script (online norm for the target market).
- Be practical (pain-point first, benefit immediately after).
- Expressive but not cheesy. Punchy sentence rhythm.

Style Do’s
- Lead with a relatable moment (“late invoice, busy day, tight deadline”).
- Keep sentences short-to-medium; natural flow.
- Sprinkle friendly interjections and softeners (in Serbian writing stage), but keep clarity first.
- Use necessary brand terms in English only when they’re product realities (SaaS, CRM, Stripe, PayPal).

Style Don’ts
- No corporate buzzwords (“end-to-end”, “synergy”, “digital transformation”).
- No formal address (“Vi”).
- No emojis or hashtags.
- No long feature dumps; always anchor a specific benefit.

Key Messages to Hit
1) Time back: Save 6+ hours/week by automating admin and follow-ups.
2) Frictionless start: 2-minute onboarding; import clients; first invoice fast.
3) Payment reminders that don’t burn bridges: friendly, respectful nudges you control.
4) Clear, fair pricing: from €9/month; trial without credit card; cancel anytime.
5) Trust & control: EU servers (Frankfurt), GDPR, easy export.

Brand Vocabulary (English allowed if necessary)
- Allowed: FlowFox, SaaS, CRM, Stripe, PayPal, PDF, CSV, dark mode.
- Prefer local equivalents for everything else.

Sample Angles (to inspire Serbian copy later)
- “Real-life freelancer moments” → how FlowFox quietly fixes them.
- “From sign-up to sent invoice in minutes” → momentum and ease.
- “Protect your client vibe” → polite reminders done for you.
- “Cost less than a daily coffee” → pricing relatability.
- “EU servers, GDPR, sleep easy” → reassurance and control.

Guardrails
- Consistent Latin script only.
- No emojis/hashtags.
- Keep each social post tight (target 180–220 characters when drafting).
- Avoid English unless it’s necessary product terminology.

Proof Points
- Early testers reported fewer unpaid invoices (directional: 20–35% improvement) after enabling reminders.
- Satisfaction comments emphasize “fast setup” and “no-nonsense UI.”

Sign-off Criteria for Posts
- Feels like a friendly human, not a corporate announcement.
- Clear value in the first line or two.
- Specific, verifiable product facts (onboarding time, trial terms, EU servers) used accurately.
