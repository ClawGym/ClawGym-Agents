Brand Voice and Tone — LeaseShield

Voice
- Second person: Speak to “you” and “your” at all times.
- Active voice: Clear verbs; short sentences.
- Confident and protective: You have their back.
- Plain English over legalese: Translate, don’t posture.

Style Rules
- Specific > vague: Use numbers, timeframes, and tangible outcomes.
- One primary CTA per view: “Get my free lease review”.
- Micro-copy under CTA reduces friction:
  - “Free in 24 hours. No credit card required.”
  - “Takes ~2 minutes to upload.”
  - “Cancel anytime.”

Words to Prefer
- “Get”, “Save”, “Protect”, “Add”, “Remove”, “Fix”
- “Cap your rent”, “Add 48-hour notice”, “Get your deposit back”

Words to Avoid
- “Disrupt”, “Revolutionize”, “Unique” (without specifics)
- “Submit” (use action+benefit instead)
- Jargon: minimize “heretofore”, “notwithstanding”; explain simply

Formatting Guidance
- Headlines: 6–12 words; outcome-first.
- Subhead: 15–25 words; explain the mechanism.
- Benefits: Bullets; lead with outcomes; include evidence when possible.
- Social proof near CTA: 1–2 testimonials or a stat block.
- Objection handling: Address cost, complexity, and landlord pushback directly.

CTA Button Copy
- Primary: “Get my free lease review”
- Keep consistent across page sections.
- Reinforcement: “Free in 24 hours. No credit card required.”

Proof Points (use responsibly)
- “Trusted by 5,200+ renters”
- “4.8★ from 1,240 reviews”
- “Average $612 saved per lease”

Compliance and Care
- Do not promise guaranteed outcomes.
- Emphasize guidance and increased likelihood, not certainty.
- Respect privacy: mention encryption and deletion on request.
