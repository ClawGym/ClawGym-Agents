Voice & Tone
- Tone: Professional, pragmatic, and optimistic. Sound like a seasoned operator who has shipped in remote teams.
- Audience: Busy leaders. Respect their time and intelligence.
- Person: Use second-person “you” throughout to make guidance actionable.
- Confidence: Evidence-based and specific; avoid hype.

Style Rules
- Pyramid Principle: State the main conclusion up front; then support it.
- Structure: Opening (hook, bridge, promise, preview) → Body (exactly 3 points: each with explanation, concrete example, actionable tip) → Closing (fresh summary, strong CTA, discussion prompt).
- Skimmability: Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences). Use subheadings and bold key phrases.
- Formatting: WeChat-friendly Markdown; no markdown tables.
- Emojis: Tasteful and minimal—3 to 6 total across the article. Recommended: ✅ 💡 ⏱️ 🧠 📌 🧭
- Title: Must start with “How to …”.
- Jargon: Prefer clear words over buzzwords. Allowed: “async collaboration”, “timeboxing”, “decision logs”. Avoid overused phrases like “synergy”, “robustify”, “bleeding edge”.
- Capitalization: FlowGrid (capitalize F and G). Features: Decision Log, Focus Guard, Meeting Health score.
- Numbers: Use numerals (e.g., 38%). Round only when summarizing; keep a specific data point from the case study.
- Brand Mentions: FlowGrid is an enabler, not the hero. Show outcomes and practices; keep product mentions helpful and light.

Content Do’s
- Do lead with a clear takeaway that reduces meetings without losing alignment.
- Do include NovaCore’s case (38% reduction) with concrete before/after.
- Do weave in relevant keywords naturally (aim for 5–8).
- Do give one practical tip per point that a reader can try this week.

Content Don’ts
- Don’t shame readers for current practices.
- Don’t promise results you can’t substantiate.
- Don’t overwhelm with features—show how the practice works in the real world.
- Don’t exceed emoji guidance or use novelty emojis that distract.

Call to Action (WeChat)
- End with a clear CTA inviting readers to Follow and Comment (and optionally Share).
- Include a Discussion prompt question to encourage real-world experiences and tactics.

Quality Bar
- Specifics over abstractions (numbers, examples, names).
- Actionability over theory.
- Friendly, not casual; concise, not curt.
