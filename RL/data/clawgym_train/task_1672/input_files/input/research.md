Use at least one of these numbers/examples in the draft.

## Internal Survey (Feb 2026)
- Sample: 212 solopreneurs running small email lists (500–10,000 subscribers)
- 73% paused their newsletter at least once in the last 90 days
- Top reasons for pausing:
  - 51% “no time”
  - 29% “no clear angle”
  - 11% perfectionism
  - 7% technical friction
- Median gap between issues after a pause: 21 days

## Case Study — Maya Chen (independent marketing consultant)
- Baseline: ~4 hours to draft one issue; missed 2 of 4 weeks in January
- Adopted a “brief → voice check → 45-minute sprint” workflow in February
- Results after 6 weeks:
  - Draft time down to ~90 minutes (−62%)
  - 6 consecutive issues shipped on schedule
  - Open rate improved from 31% to 44%
- Notes: She wrote the brief in 10 minutes the night before and used a single CTA.

## A/B Subject Line Test (March 2026, list size: 4,127)
- Variant A (curiosity gap): “Most AI agent setups fail by Friday” → 42% opens, 5.1% CTR
- Variant B (direct benefit): “Keep your agent alive past week one” → 34% opens, 3.8% CTR
- Takeaway: Curiosity gap outperformed direct benefit on both opens and clicks for this audience.

## Additional Observations
- A 10-minute pre-draft brief saved ~35% drafting time across 8 observed writing sessions (mixed industries).
- The “one CTA only” rule correlated with ~1.3× higher click-through in 3 small campaigns (n=3; directional, not definitive).
