Switchback — by Cassandra Flores\n\nAfter eight years away, Cass comes back to Borough Jct., the rail-choked district everyone else still calls The Borough. She planned to stay a week, just long enough to box up her late father’s maps and sell the old shop. Instead, she is pulled back into the lattice of alleyways, switchyards, and memories she had abandoned. Cassy thought she had outgrown the place. Then a handwritten note slips under the door: \"Your father kept a route he never charted. Find it before they do.\"\n\nCass had studied the way rails map a city’s secrets. In the past, she could decode every pattern but the ones that bound her to this neighborhood. Now she stumbles on a folder marked \"Proj. Hush\" and the last map her father sketched. It spirals through Borough Jct., looping past shuttered platforms and the cul-de-sac where her best friend vanished. It suggests there is a switch in the city’s story that no one ever threw. [TK]\n\nShe recruits two unlikely allies: a transit cop who knows when schedules lie, and a night-shift dispatcher who speaks in station codes. Together they retrace the lines on the map, one midnight ride at a time. As they follow the spiral, each waypoint unearths a debt her father took on to keep a corridor open for someone else. Cass discovers she can’t simply leave; she has to choose who the city will carry and who it will strand.\n\nWhen their search threatens a lucrative redevelopment plan, someone starts closing tracks—literally. Signals fail. A yard foreman goes missing. The anonymous notes turn into ultimatums. Cass realizes the uncharted route is not a place but a proof that the network can be bent to serve the people who live here, not just the ones who buy it. In the end, she must decide whether to publish the route and expose the rot, or burn the last of her father’s legacy to protect those who trusted him. The map is a mirror. When she reads it, she sees that Switchback isn’t about going home; it’s about choosing which way forward becomes possible.\n
