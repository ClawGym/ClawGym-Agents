# Acme Supplements — Q3 Acquisition Brief

Business model
- Direct-to-consumer (DTC) supplements with a flagship daily multivitamin and performance stack.
- Primary purchase path: paid social and search → quiz-based landing page → product detail → one-time purchase or subscribe & save (15% off).
- Fulfillment: in-house, 2–5 day shipping nationwide (US).

Audience
- 25–44, health-conscious, value on energy, focus, and recovery.
- Mix of first-time vitamin buyers and upgraders switching from grocery-store brands.

Pricing and unit economics
- Average order value (AOV): $58 (one-time); subscription AOV: $62 (first box with add-ons).
- Gross margin: 68% on one-time orders; 72% on subscription due to predictable forecasting.
- Contribution margin after variable costs (COGS, pick/pack, payment fees): ~55%.
- Blended return rate: < 2%.

LTV and retention
- 6-month LTV (blended across cohorts): $176.
- 9-month LTV: $220.
- 40% of new customers opt into subscription on first order; 2-month retention for subscribers is 78%.

Current performance
- Current blended CAC (last 30 days): $52 (too high vs payback goal).
- Payback target: ≤ 45 days at contribution margin level.
- Primary incremental channels: Meta and Google Search; TikTok and YouTube under-tested with promising early CTR but inconsistent CVR.

Geography and compliance
- United States only for Q3.
- Claims substantiated; ad copy requires soft-benefit language and avoids medical claims.

Operational notes
- Inventory secured for Q3 (no stockouts expected).
- Landing page speed: 2.4s on mobile; message match optimized for multivitamin angle.
- Analytics: server-side tracking enabled; post-iOS modeled conversions used on Meta.

Success definition for Q3
- Lower blended CAC to ≤ $45 while maintaining revenue growth.
- Hit 90-day new customer target with ≤ 45-day payback and ROAS ≥ 1.8 on prospecting.
