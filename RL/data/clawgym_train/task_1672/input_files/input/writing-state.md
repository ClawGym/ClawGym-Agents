# Writing State

## Voice Profile
- Tone: Direct, conversational, pragmatic. A little dry humor when useful. Zero hype.
- Sentence style: Short to medium sentences. Varied rhythm. Cut padding and throat-clearing.
- Vocabulary: Plain English. Use concrete examples and numbers. Industry terms okay: agent, workflow, pipeline, prompt.
- POV: First person singular. Address the reader as “you” when giving guidance.
- Things I never say: leverage, synergy, unlock your potential, it's important to note that, in today's fast-paced world, certainly!, great question!, game-changing, paradigm shift.
- Things I often say: “Here’s the thing:”, “Short version:”, “Worth noting:”, “Try this:”, “If you’re busy, do this first:”.

## Audiences
- Newsletter: Solopreneurs building AI-assisted businesses (tools, agents, systems). They’re time-poor and allergic to fluff.
- Email (cold): B2B, product-led, peer-to-peer. One ask. Under 150 words.
- Social (LinkedIn): Professional but human. One insight per post. No hashtags unless requested.
- Blog: Practitioners who want steps, not theory.

## Format Preferences
- Email subject lines: Under 50 characters. Curiosity over clickbait. Clear benefit or tension.
- CTA style: One CTA per piece. Specific action. No “click here.” No multiple asks.
- Paragraph length: 2–4 sentences for web content. Break up walls of text.
- Headers: Question-based or action-based. Never “Introduction.”

## Per-Format Tone Overrides
- LinkedIn: ~10% more formal than newsletter voice; keep it punchy.
- Twitter/X: Sharper, more direct; punchlines allowed.
- Cold email: Peer-to-peer; match their seniority.
- Newsletter: Warmest and most personal voice; concrete examples in each issue.

## Words I Never Use
- leverage, synergy, unlock, empower, journey, passion
- "it's important to note that"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "certainly!"
- "great question!"
- Any sentence that starts with “As you may know,”

## Subject Line Performance (from real sends)
- Curiosity-gap lines → avg ~42% open rate (best performer)
- Direct benefit → avg ~34%
- Question format → avg ~38%
- Avoid: pun-based — consistently under 30%

## Quality Notes
- Open strong: first 2–3 sentences must earn the scroll.
- Active voice: aim <15% passive constructions.
- One CTA only. Never two asks.
- Include at least one specific number or example in every issue.

## Series
- “The Real Config” — Monthly deep-dive
  - Format: 800–1,000 words; 3 sections + practical takeaway
  - Opening: Start with the problem, not the solution
  - Recurring element: “What I changed last month”
  - Issues published: 1–3; Next: 4 — “Minimal viable memory”
