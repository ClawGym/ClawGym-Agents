# Draft Bilateral Agreement on Security Information Sharing

## Preamble
Recognizing the shared interest in enhancing homeland security while respecting the rule of law, the Parties seek to facilitate the exchange of information to prevent, detect, and investigate serious transnational threats.

## Definitions
- "Information" means data relevant to preventing, detecting, investigating, or prosecuting serious transnational threats, including but not limited to Passenger Name Records (PNR), Advance Passenger Information (API), and watchlist data.
- "Processing" means any operation performed on Information, including collection, storage, analysis, and dissemination.

## Article 1: Data Sharing
1. Each Party shall share Information relevant to serious transnational threats, consistent with its domestic law and this Agreement.
2. The Parties may, in urgent circumstances, provide bulk datasets where appropriate to protect vital interests.
3. Information exchanges shall, where feasible, use mutually agreed technical standards and secure channels.

## Article 2: Information Retention
1. Each Party may retain Information for a reasonable period necessary to fulfill the purposes of this Agreement.
2. Retention periods should be reviewed periodically by the receiving Party.
3. Records may be retained as necessary to ensure continuity of investigations.

## Article 3: Reciprocity
1. The Parties will endeavor to ensure reciprocal benefits in Information exchange, subject to applicable law and feasibility.
2. Where automated access is provided to one Party, the other Party will consider equivalent access in good faith.

## Article 4: Oversight and Accountability
1. Each Party shall maintain internal oversight mechanisms concerning the processing of Information under this Agreement.
2. The Parties will provide periodic reports on implementation to a Joint Committee established under this Agreement.

## Article 5: Dispute Resolution
1. Any dispute arising from the interpretation or application of this Agreement shall be resolved through consultations within a reasonable time.
2. If consultations do not resolve the dispute, the matter may be referred to senior officials for consideration.

## Article 6: Verification and Implementation
1. Each Party may verify implementation through exchanges of best practices and, subject to consent, visits by technical experts.
2. A Party denying a verification request shall provide a justification to the requesting Party.

## Annex A: Safeguards
1. Processing shall reflect data minimization and proportionality.
2. Appropriate technical and organizational measures should be taken to protect Information.
3. The Parties will cooperate to correct inaccurate Information upon request.
