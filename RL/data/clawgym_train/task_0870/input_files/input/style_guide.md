Style Guide: Mamba Mentality Leadership Mini-Module

Purpose
- Equip readers with a clear, disciplined, and actionable interpretation of Mamba Mentality for leadership contexts.

Voice and Tone
- Voice: Direct, confident, instructive.
- Tone: Grounded, disciplined, and pragmatic; motivational without hype.
- Perspective: Speak to the reader as “you.” Use occasional “we” to frame shared practice, but keep it minimal.

Clarity and Structure
- Use short to medium paragraphs with clear topic sentences.
- Vary sentence length to maintain rhythm; avoid run-ons.
- Prefer concrete verbs and specific examples tied to the outlined moments.
- Introduce basketball terms only when necessary and explain them briefly if used.

Data and Accuracy
- Use only relevant facts about Kobe Bryant and Mamba Mentality.
- Explicitly name the 2009 opponent (Orlando Magic) and 2010 opponent (Boston Celtics) and state Kobe as Finals MVP in both.
- Include the 81-point game vs the Toronto Raptors on January 22, 2006, the Achilles free throws moment, and his 60-point farewell.
- Briefly explain #8 and #24 as era markers: #8 = youthful, explosive rise; #24 = mature, methodical leadership and 24/7 commitment.

Formatting Rules
- Title must be: Kobe Bryant: Mamba Mentality in Practice.
- Include a “## Takeaways” section with 5–7 bullet points using lines that start with “- ” (hyphen + space).
- Use numerals for game totals (e.g., 81-point, 60-point).
- When referencing jersey numbers, write them exactly as #8 and #24.

Quotations
- Use exactly three direct quotes from input/quotes.json.
- Each quote must be wrapped in double quotes, verbatim, with no alterations.
- Do not include any additional direct quotes.

Punctuation and Diction
- Avoid exclamation marks.
- Use em dashes sparingly and consistently.
- Maintain professional capitalization for “Finals MVP.”

Banned Words and Practices
- Do not compare Kobe to other players or discuss “GOAT” debates.
- Avoid: GOAT, greatest of all time, debate, versus another player, Mount Rushmore, legacy debate, hot take, clickbait.
- Avoid hyperbolic fillers: literally, unbelievable, insane, mind-blowing.
- Avoid empty clichés: at the end of the day, think outside the box, game changer (unless tied to a specific example).
- Avoid direct references to other players by name.
- Do not add more than three direct quotes.

Leadership Orientation
- Translate each principle into a leadership behavior (planning cadence, feedback loops, decision-making under pressure, recovery protocols, adaptation plans).
- Keep the focus on applicability: what leaders can do next.

Compliance Summary
- 900–1200 words.
- Exactly three direct quotes from quotes.json (double-quoted, verbatim).
- Include all required historical references and the jersey number explanation.
- Include “## Takeaways” with 5–7 bullets.
- No comparisons to other players. No extra quotes. No exclamation marks.
