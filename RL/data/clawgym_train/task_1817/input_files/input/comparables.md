# Comparables — Food Safety/Restaurant Ops (Indicative)

These are directional signals to benchmark SOM penetration and ACV. Figures triangulated from public press mentions, pricing pages, and operator interviews; treat as estimates for planning, not audited facts.

## Jolt
- What: Restaurant operations and food safety checklists for independents and small groups
- Footprint signal: 10,000+ locations served (public claims circa 2019–2022)
- Pricing observed: $79–$99 per location per month (see competitor_pricing.csv)
- Indicative ARR math: 10,000 × ~$90/mo × 12 ≈ ~$10.8M ARR (software only)
- Relevance: Strong independent footprint; validates Year 3 outcomes in high-velocity go-to-market

## Zenput (now part of CrunchTime)
- What: Operations execution and food safety for multi-unit restaurants and C-stores
- Footprint signal: 50,000–60,000+ locations (chain-heavy)
- Pricing observed: ~$120+ per location per month (enterprise packages vary)
- Indicative ARR math: 60,000 × $120 × 12 ≈ $86.4M (mostly enterprise/chain)
- Relevance: Category ceiling; less relevant to independent SAM but demonstrates category spend

## MeazureUp
- What: Digital audits and checklists for restaurants and retail
- Footprint signal: ~3,500–5,000 locations
- Pricing observed: ~$95 per location per month
- Indicative ARR math: 4,000 × $95 × 12 ≈ $4.6M
- Relevance: Mid-scale independent + small group penetration; good Year 2–3 benchmark

## ComplianceMate
- What: Food safety software with sensor integrations
- Footprint signal: ~5,000–7,000 locations (mix of independents/small chains)
- Pricing observed: ~$110 per location per month (software; sensors are extra)
- Indicative ARR math: 6,000 × $110 × 12 ≈ $7.9M (software only)
- Relevance: Validates ACV and attach dynamics for sensor-optional model

## Penetration benchmarks (to anchor SOM)
- New B2B SaaS in established category:
  - Year 1: 0.3–0.8% of SAM
  - Year 3: 3–7% of SAM
  - Basis: a16z/OpenView early-stage SaaS penetration patterns; triangulated with the above comparable footprints and timelines

Notes:
- Independent vs. chain mix heavily skews ACV and sales velocity. Jolt and MeazureUp are closer to our ICP; Zenput provides an upper bound for category spend but is chain-oriented.
- Where footnotes are not linkable, treat as directional and cross-check against your pipeline and conversion assumptions.
