# 7 Work-From-Home Tips for Busy Designers

Staying creative at home can be tricky. Here are practical habits I use daily to protect focus, keep energy up, and ship consistent work.

## 1) Protect your focus with Do Not Disturb mode
Silence your notifications during design sprints. Do Not Disturb mode removes the temptation to glance at chats and emails. Block time on your calendar, flip DND on, and let teammates know when you’ll be back online.

## 2) Batch creative sprints with the Pomodoro Technique and timeboxing
Use focused blocks to make progress without burning out. The Pomodoro Technique keeps sprints short and restorative, while timeboxing helps you commit a realistic amount of time to each task so it doesn’t expand endlessly.

## 3) Build a reusable mood board library
Collect references and color palettes you love. A mood board library speeds up exploration, keeps you inspired, and reduces decision fatigue.

## 4) Master keyboard shortcuts in Figma, Photoshop, and Illustrator
Memorize the shortcuts you use every day. Small time savings compound across hundreds of actions. Customize your cheat sheet and rehearse until it’s muscle memory.

## 5) Use a grid system from the start
A solid grid system makes layout decisions faster and more consistent. Align early so you don’t clean up alignment late.

## 6) Stand up: invest in a standing desk and an ergonomic chair
Alternate between sitting and standing to avoid stiffness. A standing desk and an ergonomic chair help you maintain posture and focus longer.

## 7) Commit to daily deep work
Schedule deep work when your energy peaks. Close extra tabs, set a clear goal, and protect the time like a meeting with yourself.
