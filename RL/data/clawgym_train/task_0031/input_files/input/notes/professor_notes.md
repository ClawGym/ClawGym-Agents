# Making the Most of College in Stockton: Faculty Notes

Stockton is a river city with a vibrant waterfront, a historic downtown, and neighborhoods that surround our campus. Students thrive when they connect coursework with place: walk the waterfront trail, attend neighborhood meetings, and learn local transit patterns. The RTD bus system and the U-Pass can open doors across the city without a car.

On campus, students benefit most when they build relationships: visit office hours early, join service-learning projects that match your interests, and use academic support centers before midterms. For basic needs, our Food Pantry reduces stress so you can focus on learning. For safety, travel with friends at night and use the Campus Night Escort when needed.

Guidance for tone and translation:
- Keep program and office names in English (e.g., "Writing Center", "RTD Bus U-Pass").
- Use clear, student-friendly language.
- Focus advice on Stockton context (transit, neighborhoods, campus-community connections).

Glossary terms (English):
- office hours
- service-learning
- food pantry
- first-generation student
- RTD bus
