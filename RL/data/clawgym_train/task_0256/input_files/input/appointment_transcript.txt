Title: Post-Visit Debrief with Dr. Lee
Date: 2026-04-01
Notes captured during a 30-minute virtual check-in.

[Topic: Intermittent Fasting]
- Dr. Lee: For most healthy adults, a 16:8 fasting pattern can be sustainable when meals in the eating window are balanced with adequate protein and fiber.
- Dr. Lee: Avoid dry fasting; water and electrolytes are important, and we discussed keeping hydration consistent across the day.

[Topic: Hydration]
- Dr. Lee: Aim for steady intake throughout the day. Signs like pale yellow urine and consistent energy are better signals than chasing a fixed gallon goal.
- Dr. Lee: During intense workouts or heavy sweating, an electrolyte drink can help; daily packets are not necessary for everyone.

[Topic: Sleep Hygiene]
- Dr. Lee: Keep bedtime and wake time consistent. Reduce screen exposure 60 minutes before bed and create a wind-down routine.
- Dr. Lee: A cool, dark bedroom and limiting caffeine after early afternoon support deeper sleep.

[Topic: Supplements Safety]
- Dr. Lee: Start low, go slow, and always check for interactions with medications.
- Dr. Lee: Magnesium glycinate may support sleep for some, while melatonin is best for short-term use. Greens powders can complement but do not replace whole foods.
