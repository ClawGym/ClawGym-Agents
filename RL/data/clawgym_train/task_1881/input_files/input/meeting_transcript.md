Community Mental Health Town Hall — May 14, 2026
Location: Northside Community Center

Attendees:
- Sen. Alex Rivera (Host)
- Dr. Maya Chen (Clinical Psychologist)
- Jordan Lee (School Counselor)
- Priya Natarajan (Community Organizer)
- Samir Patel (Veteran Advocate)
- Lena Ortiz (Parent)
- Maria Gomez (Policy Director)
- Tom Wright (Communications Manager)

Opening remarks
Alex Rivera: Thank you all for being here. As someone trained in psychology, I’ve seen firsthand how early intervention and supportive environments change trajectories. Tonight is about practical steps.

Topic 1: School-based services
Jordan Lee: We’re seeing more anxiety and school refusal. Students need accessible counseling, not waitlists.
Dr. Maya Chen: Early identification in schools matters. Screening plus brief CBT can stabilize kids quickly.
Alex Rivera: My own family benefitted from school counseling when we couldn't afford private therapy; it’s why I prioritize this.
Action: Draft bill language to fund 5 additional school counselors in District 14. Owner: Maria Gomez. Due: 2026-05-24.

Topic 2: Telehealth reimbursement
Priya Natarajan: Telehealth expanded access, but inconsistent reimbursement blocks continuity.
Alex Rivera: Cognitive-behavioral interventions translate well to telehealth; reimbursement parity is key to sustaining access.
Action: Schedule a meeting with the State Health Department to discuss telehealth parity. Owner: Alex Rivera. Due: 2026-05-20.

Topic 3: Veteran services
Samir Patel: Veterans wait weeks for intake. Delays worsen PTSD symptoms.
Alex Rivera: System delays undermine treatment adherence. We need timely access.
Action: Compile data on regional VA clinic wait times (last 12 months). Owner: Tom Wright. Due: 2026-05-22.

Topic 4: Public awareness
Lena Ortiz: Parents miss early signs; stigma stops us from asking for help.
Dr. Maya Chen: A bilingual flyer on anxiety signs and where to get help would normalize seeking care.
Alex Rivera: Normalizing help-seeking is a cornerstone of behavior change.
Action: Prepare a bilingual flyer (English/Spanish) on anxiety signs and local resources. Owner: Priya Natarajan. Due: 2026-05-25.

Topic 5: Clinic capacity
Priya Natarajan: Evening hours would help working families.
Dr. Maya Chen: Two pilot clinics could trial extended hours and measure no-show rates.
Alex Rivera: Structured pilots let us evaluate outcomes ethically and efficiently.
Action: Identify two pilot clinics willing to extend evening hours. Owner: Dr. Maya Chen. Due: 2026-05-27.

Closing
Alex Rivera: We have concrete steps. Thank you for your candor and partnership.
