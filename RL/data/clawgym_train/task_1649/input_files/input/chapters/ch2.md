# Chapter 2: Field Trials and Findings

### Scene 1: Field test
In a remote field station, engineers release mosquitoes carrying a gene drive and monitor population dynamics. The team logs unexpected behavior and debates whether off-target mutations could have reduced fitness. A senior scientist notes that off-target sites might accumulate silently. They consider switching delivery from a viral vector to an alternative approach in future tests. Debate continues as more data suggest the gene drive is spreading quickly, raising fresh questions about containment and off-target effects.

### Scene 2: Discovery
Back in the lab, the group runs PCR screens on dozens of colonies to verify edited loci. The gels look messy, prompting another round of PCR with adjusted annealing temperatures. Sequencing reads reveal mitochondrial DNA anomalies that might be artifacts. A junior researcher repeats CRISPR edits with a redesigned guide and logs more consistent bands. Another bench scientist flags that mitochondrial heteroplasmy could confound interpretation unless controls are tightened.
