# Scenario Brief: AI Assistants in Professional Services

Focal Question:
How will regulatory constraints and compute availability shape the adoption, ROI, and operating models for AI assistants in mid-market professional services firms by 2031?

Scope:
- Geography: North America and European Union
- Industry: Mid-market professional services (consulting, legal, accounting, research)
- Functions: Knowledge work (drafting, analysis, research synthesis, client correspondence)

Time Horizon:
2026–2031 (5-year horizon)

Context Notes:
- Emerging AI regulations in the EU and evolving guidance in the US create uncertainty around data residency, model transparency, and auditability.
- Compute and model access may tighten due to supply constraints or broaden via specialized/open-source models.
- Buyer priorities include risk mitigation, measurable productivity gains, and client trust.
- Procurement and IT concerns: data leakage, vendor lock-in, integration overhead, and compliance reporting.

Initial Driving Forces (for STEEP brainstorming):
- Social: Workforce acceptance, skills shift, client expectations for AI-augmented deliverables
- Technological: Model availability (proprietary vs open), retrieval and guardrail maturity, on-prem/edge options
- Economic: Cost of tokens/compute, pricing pressure in services, ROI thresholds
- Environmental: Data center energy and sustainability reporting pressures
- Political/Regulatory: EU AI Act implementation, US sectoral rules, cross-border data transfer constraints

Decision to Inform:
Investment and rollout strategy for AI assistants across practice areas, including whether to standardize on a single platform, adopt a hybrid approach, or delay until clarity improves.
