Transportation Guidance (Los Angeles realities)
- Car reality: LA is a car city. Most of us will drive. Budget extra time and bring a phone mount for safe navigation.
- 405 traffic: The 405 is heavily congested 4:00–7:00 PM. If you’re coming from the Westside/Valley, consider surface streets (Sepulveda, Sawtelle) or exit earlier to avoid chokepoints near the 10/405 interchange.
- 10 Freeway: East–west traffic also spikes 4:30–6:30 PM. Plan 1.5× Google Maps ETA during peak.
- Parking: Ivy Station garage usually offers paid parking with validation at on-site retailers; rates can change. Street parking is limited and time-restricted. Validate if you grab coffee/food, or use the garage to avoid tickets.
- Metro: The E Line serves Culver City directly (Culver City Station is at Ivy Station). It’s convenient if you’re already near the E Line corridor (Santa Monica–DTLA). However, first/last-mile can be inconvenient elsewhere, and trains can be infrequent evenings—check schedules and allow buffer time.
- Micromobility: Scooters/bikes are often available around Ivy Station, but availability fluctuates. Helmets encouraged, especially on Venice/Washington corridors.

Weather & June Gloom
- June Gloom: From May–July, coastal mornings can be overcast and cool. Even in Culver City, marine layer can make evenings breezy. Bring a light layer if we shift to a morning or late-evening meetup. Sun can break through midday, but temps drop quickly after sunset.

- Stay alert around the station area, especially after dark.
