Flowpilot Automation — 3-Day Social Sprint Brand Brief

Overview
We help operators and builders ship faster with safe, reliable AI agents that automate repetitive work. The sprint objective is to show real, practical wins from “agent life,” spark conversations, and drive qualified interest in demos/trials.

Audience
- Primary: Operations leaders, SaaS founders, RevOps, product/ops engineers
- Secondary: Indie hackers, no-code builders, creators scaling solo
- Mindset: Pragmatic, value clarity, allergic to hype, want proof and process

Value Proposition (Use this framing)
- Agents that augment teams (not replace people)
- Clear workflows, measurable outcomes, fewer handoffs
- Fast setup, safe by design, easy to iterate and scale

Brand Voice
- Clear, practical, confidence without hype
- Curious and helpful; invite dialogue over declarations
- Short sentences. Active verbs. Plain English. US spelling.
- Friendly, not flippant. Playful allowed, never snarky.
- “We” and “you” voice; avoid third-person corporatespeak.
- Data-minded: mention outcomes or processes when possible.
- Emoji: optional, max 1 relevant emoji per post; skip if it feels forced.
- Hashtags: purposeful only; avoid hashtag walls.

Tone Examples (feel free to adapt)
- “Agents take the repeat work. You keep the thinking.”
- “We ship small, test fast, scale what works.”
- “Automation is a system, not a stunt.”

Content Pillars for this Sprint
- Agent life & automation in the trenches (behind-the-scenes, playbooks)
- Practical tips and mini frameworks
- Outcome snapshots (time saved, errors avoided, speed gained)
- Conversation starters (questions, trade-offs, patterns to watch)

Hashtag Guidance
- Moltbook (required): Include at least one of these in every Moltbook post:
  - #AgentLife
  - #OpenClaw
- Secondary/optional tags (use sparingly, 0–1 max in addition to required tag):
  - #Automation, #AIinOps, #NoCode, #Ops
- Twitter: 0–1 hashtag max; plain-language posts often perform better.

Platform Nuance
- Moltbook: Native agent culture, playful but technical is welcome. Memes are fine if they point to a real ops insight. Required tag: #AgentLife or #OpenClaw.
- Twitter: Thread-openers should be crisp, self-contained, and under 280 characters. Make them expand-able later with proof or steps.

Style Constraints
- Avoid hard promises; frame as experiments, patterns, or results from specific contexts.
- Prefer specifics over generalities (e.g., “cut QA handoffs from 3 to 1” vs “improved quality”).
- Keep jargon minimal; define terms if used.

Banned words (case-insensitive; avoid all occurrences; substring match)
- “click here”
- “buy now”
- “limited time”
- “giveaway”
- “contest”
- “followback”
- “DM for details”
- “crypto”
- “NFT”
- “airdrop”
- “pump”
- “shill”
- “get rich”
- “secret sauce”
- “guaranteed”
- “100% free”
- “leak”
- “hack” (when used as “life hack” or implying exploits)
- “miracle”
- “magic”

Compliance & Risk Notes
- Don’t claim job replacement; we augment teams.
- No data scraping or TOS-violating language.
- No deceptive monetization or vague “growth hacks.”
- Attribute results to context; invite questions.

Examples of On-Brand Micro-Claims
- “Cut status pings by 60% with agent-led updates.”
- “Rolled out a triage agent: fewer handoffs, faster resolves.”
- “From backlog to shipped: agent-first QA checklist.”

Call to Action Style
- Soft CTAs: “Curious how we set this up? Reply and we’ll share the playbook.” / “Want the checklist? Say ‘PLAYBOOK’ and we’ll DM a summary.”
- No hard-sell CTAs, urgency, or gated promises.

Hashtag & Formatting Rules Recap
- Moltbook: Always include #AgentLife or #OpenClaw (at least one).
- Twitter: Under 280 characters. Keep it clean, 0–1 hashtag. Thread-openers should tease a clear angle (what/why/how).

If anything feels unclear, ask for clarification before posting.
