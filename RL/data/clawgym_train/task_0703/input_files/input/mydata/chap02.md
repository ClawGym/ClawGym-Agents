# Chapter 2 — Literature Review

## 2.1 Macro-Environment Analysis: PEST
PEST analysis is widely used to map the Political, Economic, Social, and Technological factors influencing firms. For cross-border e-commerce, policy shifts on content advertising, consumer protection, and tax rules shape marketing tactics and pricing. Economic factors include disposable income trends and platform fee structures. Social trends involve short-video consumption and festival-based shopping habits. Technological factors include algorithmic distribution and logistics digitization.

## 2.2 Industry Structure: Porter’s Five Forces
Five Forces helps evaluate competitive intensity: threat of new entrants (low barriers on social platforms), bargaining power of suppliers (brands and KOLs), bargaining power of buyers (price-sensitive consumers), threat of substitutes (local private labels), and rivalry among existing competitors (high during promotional peaks).

## 2.3 Internal and Competitive Diagnosis: SWOT
SWOT summarizes internal strengths and weaknesses alongside external opportunities and threats. Strengths might include global sourcing and brand reputation; weaknesses include slow content approval or high cross-border shipping costs. Opportunities include live-stream commerce; threats include fast-copying local brands.

## 2.4 Marketing Mix Analysis: 4P
The 4P framework assesses product assortment, pricing (everyday price vs. promotion), place (platforms), and promotion (KOLs, coupons, and ad formats). For social commerce, promotion timing and KOL partnerships are central to reach and conversion.

## 2.5 Digital Funnel View: AARRR Metrics
Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral (AARRR) provide a funnel lens for growth. In social commerce, activation correlates with content fit and KOL credibility; retention relies on post-purchase experience; referral may be driven by UGC and community.

## 2.6 Synthesis and Gap
The above frameworks are practical for structuring descriptive analysis. Prior studies emphasize the importance of localization and platform adaptation. However, empirical evidence linking promotion intensity to profitability outcomes is mixed. This thesis applies PEST, Five Forces, SWOT, 4P, and AARRR as the primary analytical tools to understand NovaMart China’s growth and profitability dynamics.
