# Reading Notes (Evidence-based)

- Responsive feeding: follow baby's hunger and fullness cues; avoid rigid scheduling that ignores cues.
- Safe sleep: back to sleep on a firm, flat surface; keep sleep space free of soft objects and loose bedding; room-share but do not bed-share.
- Tummy time: aim for at least 20–30 minutes total each day, split into short, supervised sessions.
- Introducing solids: around ~6 months when readiness cues appear (good head control, sits with support, interest in food); keep breastmilk/formula as primary nutrition.
- Bedtime routine: dim lights, quiet wind-down, consistent sequence; put baby down drowsy but awake to reduce sleep associations.
- Dream feed: optional trial; monitor whether it increases total overnight sleep without causing earlier wake-ups.
