East Danforth Market Context (Spring 2026)

Supply & Inventory
- Inventory for move-in-ready semi-detached homes within ~600m of subway stations (Coxwell–Main) remains low to moderate.
- New listings are down approximately 10–15% year-over-year in the immediate corridor, creating a mild supply squeeze for well-renovated products with parking.

Demand & Buyer Behavior
- Demand is strongest for 3-bedroom semis with parking and updated interiors within walking distance of TTC.
- Buyers show resistance above the ~$1.25M psychological threshold unless the home is larger, recently gut-renovated, or offers an income suite.
- Multiple-offer scenarios are still common when list prices are positioned to drive traffic (offer dates), especially on the first week.

Momentum & Pricing Trend
- Momentum has been modestly positive over the last 60–90 days; price per square foot is flat to slightly rising.
- Typical days on market (DOM) for well-priced semis: 7–14 days; overpriced listings can linger 20–30 days and require price cuts.
- Sale-to-list ratios often land 101–105% when underpricing strategy is used to concentrate demand, but appraisals can be tighter when sale prices stretch above recent comparable benchmarks.

Seasonality
- Spring (March–June) is the most liquid period; buyer activity and showing volume are higher, while summer tends to slow.
- Weather and school calendar effects suggest best traction before mid-June.

Strategy Notes
- Properties near subway stations with front-pad or laneway parking outperform similar stock without parking.
- Quiet streets away from busier corridors command a small premium.
- To maximize price while avoiding extended time on market, consider pricing at or slightly below fair market value with a defined offer date, professional staging, and a pre-list home inspection to reduce buyer friction.
