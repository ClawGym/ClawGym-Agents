# Lesson Outline: Heat-Induced Reactions

## Maillard Reaction
The Maillard reaction is a complex series of non-enzymatic browning reactions between amino acids and reducing sugars, accelerated at dry surface temperatures above ~140°C. It forms melanoidins and diverse flavor compounds.
Key variables:
- Surface dryness
- Temperature and time
- pH (alkaline speeds up)

## Caramelization
Caramelization is the pyrolysis of sugars at high heat, producing nutty, bitter, and sweet notes and brown color without amino acids.
Key variables:
- Sugar concentration
- Temperature profile

## Protein Denaturation and Coagulation
Proteins unfold (denature) and then form new bonds (coagulate), altering texture. This is temperature-dependent and often reversible only up to a point.
Example:
- Egg whites start coagulating around 62–65°C.
