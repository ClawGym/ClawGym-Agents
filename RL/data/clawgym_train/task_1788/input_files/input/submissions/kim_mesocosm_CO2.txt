Title: Mesocosm CO2 Enrichment and Carbonate Chemistry Responses in a Coastal Bloom
Author: J. Kim

This study examines how elevated pCO2 alters net community production and calcification during a spring phytoplankton bloom using enclosed coastal mesocosms. We adjusted carbonate chemistry to target ~400 and ~1000 µatm pCO2 and tracked dissolved inorganic carbon and alkalinity alongside chlorophyll over a 6-day bloom cycle. Tanks were mixed continuously and sampled daily for chemistry and biomass proxies. The enrichment produced notable shifts in carbonate saturation states and apparent production–calcification balance. Methods and sampling schedules are described at a high level here, and additional details will follow in the full draft. At this stage, we focus on demonstrating feasibility and general response patterns rather than full statistical treatment, and numerical summaries are not yet included. We plan to add explicit hypotheses and uncertainty analyses in the next iteration.
