This thesis examines NovaMart China, the cross-border consumer goods business of a global e-commerce retailer operating across Tmall, Douyin, and marketplace channels. From 2022 to 2024, NovaMart China’s gross merchandise volume (GMV) increased from RMB 1.2 billion to RMB 1.78 billion, while contribution margin declined from 18% to 12%. The central question is how to improve marketing efficiency and translate growth into sustainable profitability in China’s dynamic social commerce environment.

The study adopts a descriptive case approach and applies standard MBA frameworks—PEST, SWOT, 4P, and Porter’s Five Forces—to analyze the external macro environment, the competitive landscape, and the firm’s internal capabilities. Data derive from public industry reports, company dashboards, and campaign summaries. The analysis highlights regulatory sensitivity in content marketing, rising fulfillment costs, the importance of influencer partnerships, and consumer preferences for price promotions.

The findings suggest NovaMart should strengthen localization, deepen partnerships with high-performing KOLs, optimize logistics, and enhance brand communication. Recommended actions include better forecasting for live-stream campaigns, improved content review processes to adapt to fast-changing trends, and refined promotion strategies. The thesis contributes practical insights for cross-border e-commerce operators seeking to balance growth and profitability under evolving platform rules and consumer expectations. Limitations include the single-case design and reliance on descriptive data, which restricts causal inference. Future work could incorporate surveys and more granular econometric analysis.
