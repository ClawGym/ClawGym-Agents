Title: Patterns of Meaning: A Structural and Cognitive Reading of Greco-Roman Myth
Scholar: Dr. Lydia Green

Approach
This project integrates structuralist analysis (in the lineage of Lévi-Strauss) with cognitive narratology to explain how recurring mythic patterns organize meaning. It foregrounds motifs such as heroic descent, trickster figures, and metamorphosis, showing how audiences process these structures over time.

Corpus
Primary focus on Greek and Roman sources: Hesiod (Theogony), Homeric epics, Ovid’s Metamorphoses, complemented by select inscriptions and vase imagery for iconographic resonance.

Methods
Close reading; motif indexing; structural oppositions mapping; cognitive schema diagrams linking narrative patterns to audience inference.

Deliverables
A 400-page monograph with approximately 15 figure plates (schema diagrams and select images, rights-cleared during production). Prose pitched for educated general readers, with brief text boxes introducing technical terms.

Timeline
9 months to complete a full draft manuscript ready for editorial review.

Notes
The focused cultural scope supports depth and conceptual clarity; visuals aim to aid accessibility.
