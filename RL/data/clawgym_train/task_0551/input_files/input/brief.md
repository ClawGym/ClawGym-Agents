Creative Brief: “Dawn Drive”

Summary
- Concept: A reflective, forward-leaning song about leaving the city at first light and taking a coastal drive toward possibility. Tone is hopeful but grounded; the motion of the car mirrors an internal shift from crowded noise to open air.
- Title/Hook: “Dawn Drive” (must appear clearly in the chorus as an exact phrase).

Genre & Mood
- Genre: Indie pop / modern folk-pop (acoustic-led with subtle synth textures).
- Mood: Hopeful, nostalgic, quietly triumphant; steady pulse and gentle lift in the chorus.
- Vibe references: The intimacy of acoustic singer-songwriter with the shimmer of coastal indie pop.

Key & Tempo
- Suggested Key: A major (bright but not saccharine; open chords on guitar).
- Suggested Tempo: 100 BPM (mid-tempo, steady roll; feels like tires on a quiet road).

Narrative Arc
- Verse 1: Leaving the city while it’s still half-asleep; dashboard glow, empty streets, faint trains.
- Chorus: The release—open road by the coast, salt air, a sense of direction. The hook “dawn drive” anchors the emotional pivot.
- Verse 2: The city falls behind; shoreline opens. Little details: paper cup coffee, radio barely in tune, taillights thinning out.
- Bridge: A quieter reflection—what’s being left behind vs. what’s ahead (no melodrama; keep it specific and tactile).
- Outro (optional): A small tag or image (e.g., “the coast unfolding” or “headlights off by the waterline”).

Imagery & Sensory Notes
- Visual: Asphalt still holding night, first pale light on glass and signs, coastline unspooling, gulls skimming grey water.
- Sound: Radio murmurs, early trains in the distance, tires on wet pavement, a station fading in and out.
- Touch/Smell: Salt air, coffee warmth in a paper cup, cool steering wheel, marine breeze sneaking through a cracked window.

Structure & Section Lengths (guidance; adjust as needed)
- Pattern: ABABCB (Verse–Chorus–Verse–Chorus–Bridge–Chorus). Optional intro/outro.
- Bar counts at 100 BPM:
  - Intro: 4 bars
  - Verse: 16 bars
  - Pre-Chorus (optional): 4–8 bars
  - Chorus: 8 bars
  - Bridge: 8 bars
  - Outro: 4 bars

Rhyme & Meter (guidance)
- Verse rhyme: ABCB (allow slant/near rhymes), ~8–10 syllables per line, conversational.
- Chorus rhyme: ABAB (tighter), ~6–8 syllables per line, singable hook with “dawn drive”.
- Bridge rhyme: XAXA or free; prioritize flow over strictness.

Harmony Suggestions (Roman numerals in A major)
- Verse (lower energy, circular): vi – IV – I – V  (F#m – D – A – E)
- Pre-Chorus (lift): ii – IV – V  (Bm – D – E) or ii – V  (Bm – E)
- Chorus (anthemic resolution): I – V – vi – IV  (A – E – F#m – D)
- Bridge (contrast): IV – I – ii – V  (D – A – Bm – E) or start on IV for a change of color

Arrangement Notes
- Intro: fingerpicked or lightly strummed acoustic; subtle pad swell.
- Chorus 1: keep intimate; add low harmony or gentle tambourine on 2/4.
- Chorus 2/final: add harmony vocals, light electric guitar arpeggio, fuller drums.
- Bridge: pull back (bass and drums lighten), then ramp into the final chorus.

Lyric Tone & Boundaries
- Conversational, grounded imagery; avoid grand statements.
- Avoid melodrama; keep it tactile and specific to the scene.
- Do not use listed clichés (see preferences file).

Prompt-Friendly Metadata (for later use)
- Vocal: warm and intimate; androgynous or soft male/female.
- Instrumentation: acoustic guitar, clean electric guitar, live drums (brush/soft kit), bass, subtle synth pads.
- Production: organic with modern polish; spatial but not drenched in reverb.
