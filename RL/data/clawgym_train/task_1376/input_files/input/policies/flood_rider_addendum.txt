Bayshore Mutual Flood Rider Addendum
Endorsement Number: FLD-2026-447
Attached to Policy: BM-HO3-784512
Residence Premises: 123 Mariners Way, Bayshore, FL 33701
Endorsement Effective Date: 07/01/2026 (subject to Waiting Period)
Additional Annual Premium: $320

Section A — Coverage (Flood)
- Building (Flood) Limit: $250,000; Deductible: $2,000 per flood occurrence.
- Personal Property (Flood) Limit: $100,000; Deductible: $2,000 per flood occurrence.
- Loss of Use is not covered by this Flood Rider.
- Basement Limitation: We do not cover finished walls, floors, wall-to-wall carpet, cabinets, or other improvements located below ground level. We cover foundation elements, electrical systems, central air conditioners, furnaces, water heaters, and sump pumps located in a basement.

Section B — Exclusions (Flood)
- We do not cover earth movement (including landslide, sinkhole, subsidence), even if caused by flood.
- We do not cover currency, bullion, precious metals, valuable papers, or securities.
- We do not cover vehicles of any type, including their parts.
- We do not cover sewer backup or sump overflow unless it is a direct result of flood and not otherwise covered by another endorsement or policy.
- We do not cover mold, fungus, or wet rot unless caused by a covered flood and you begin remediation within 72 hours of the flood; flood-related mold remediation is subject to a $2,500 sub-limit per policy period.

Section C — Waiting Period
- Coverage under this Flood Rider begins 15 days after the Endorsement Effective Date.
- Exceptions: No waiting period applies if (1) coverage is required by a lender for a loan closing and is effective at closing, or (2) this rider replaces flood coverage with no lapse in coverage.

Section D — Definitions (Flood Rider)
- Flood means a general and temporary condition of partial or complete inundation of normally dry land area from: (1) overflow of inland or tidal waters; (2) unusual and rapid accumulation or runoff of surface waters from any source; or (3) mudflow.
- Basement means any area of the building having its floor subgrade (below ground level) on all sides.
- Deductible means the amount you pay out of pocket for each covered flood occurrence before we pay.
- Waiting Period means the time after the Endorsement Effective Date during which no coverage is provided unless an exception applies.

Section E — Conditions (Flood Rider)
- This Flood Rider modifies the attached policy for the peril of flood only.
- Settlement: We pay covered flood losses up to the applicable Flood limits and subject to the Flood deductibles and limitations stated in this Rider.
- This Flood Rider does not provide Additional Living Expense or Loss of Use.
