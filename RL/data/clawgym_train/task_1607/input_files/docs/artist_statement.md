# Artist Statement — Working Notes

## Practice
My paintings are slow, layered fields that invite sustained looking rather than quick capture. I work in sequences to explore shifts in perception, attention, and the way color holds silence. The work is not narrative; it tries to let an experience happen without pointing at it.

## Materials
Pigments, oil, and gesso on linen. I sand between layers, leave traces of decisions, and resist masking out accidents. The formats are human-scale and meant to be met at breathing distance.

## Intent
I am focused on the experience of seeing, not on depicting things. I want the paintings to be a present-tense space where the viewer and the work are both unguarded.
