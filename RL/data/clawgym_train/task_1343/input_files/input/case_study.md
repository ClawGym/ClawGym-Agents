Client type:
- Mid-market cybersecurity SaaS (Series B), ~180 employees, selling to IT and security teams.

Initial problem:
- Expansion revenue was flat and logo retention sliding. Seat-based pricing capped value at “admin users” while the product’s core value scaled with the number of protected endpoints and data processed.
- Sales complained about discount pressure; Finance reported high variance in effective price per customer without clear rationale.

Context:
- Product has strong usage telemetry (endpoints protected, GB of logs processed, number of alerts triaged).
- Billing infrastructure could support add-ons but not metered charges in real time.

Engagement goals:
- Identify a value-based meter.
- Pilot a usage-based price for new logos without disrupting existing customers.
- Reduce discounting and improve net revenue retention (NRR).

Approach:
1) Value mapping workshops with Product, Sales, and two customers to align on what correlates with outcomes (reduced breach exposure).
2) Meter selection: primary meter = number of protected endpoints; secondary meter = GB of log ingestion (for add-on tier).
3) Packaging: three tiers with clear guardrails; included a “safety band” so new customers wouldn’t be surprised by seasonal spikes.
4) Billing: phased rollout—monthly usage estimates with true-up at quarter end while the metering pipeline matured.
5) Change management: sales enablement deck, objection handling one-pagers, and pilot contracts for 12 design partners.

Actions taken:
- Implemented transparent in-app usage dashboards.
- Introduced a “starter” commitment (base includes up to X endpoints) plus a fair-use allowance.
- Created migration playbooks for existing customers at renewal with opt-in credits to smooth the transition.

Outcomes (6 months post-launch, pilot + first renewal cycle):
- Net revenue retention improved from 104% to 131%.
- Average discount rate fell from 18% to 9%.
- Time-to-value messaging shortened sales cycles by ~12%.
- Churn decreased by 2.3 percentage points, primarily due to right-sized entry and clearer ROI.
- No material increase in billing disputes; support tickets on billing actually declined due to in-app visibility.

Notes:
- The team intentionally kept one simple meter (endpoints) and used GB ingestion only as an add-on to avoid complexity creep.
- Leadership emphasized “no-gotchas” pricing—customers received proactive usage alerts and tips to manage consumption.
