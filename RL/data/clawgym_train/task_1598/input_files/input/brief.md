OpsForge — company brief

What we do
OpsForge is a B2B SaaS for mid‑market operations teams that orchestrates workflows across tools with AI routing and human‑in‑the‑loop approvals. We unify handoffs that currently hop between Slack, email, spreadsheets, and point automations, and we add policy‑aware guardrails plus a clean approval UX.

Who we serve
- Mid‑market companies (200–2,000 employees)
- Functions: Business Operations, RevOps, People Ops, IT Ops, Finance Ops
- Industries with compliance pressure: SaaS, logistics, fintech, biotech/healthtech, professional services

The pain
- Tool sprawl: Jira + Asana + Slack + Salesforce/HubSpot + NetSuite + BambooHR/Workday + ServiceNow + homegrown scripts
- Brittle automations: zap chains and scripts break on schema changes; no observability
- Approvals are scattered: email threads and DMs with no audit trail
- Compliance gap: SOC 2/ISO 27001 evidence collection is manual and error‑prone

Our approach
- AI Router learns from historical routing patterns and policy rules to send the right work to the right person or system
- Human‑in‑the‑loop approvals directly in Slack and email with clear SLAs and escalation paths
- Deterministic guardrails: policy graphs define who can approve what, with mandatory dual control when required
- Immutable audit logs and replayable runs for compliance
- 40+ high‑usage connectors at launch (Salesforce, HubSpot, NetSuite, Workday, BambooHR, Okta, Google Workspace, Slack, Jira, Asana, ServiceNow, Snowflake, Postgres, S3)
- Prebuilt cross‑tool playbooks (e.g., employee onboarding/offboarding, lead lifecycle QA, vendor onboarding, credit memo approvals)

Proof so far (beta)
- 11 design partners in SaaS, logistics, and fintech
- Median time‑to‑value: 12 days from kickoff to first production workflow
- Northbridge Logistics cut onboarding lead time by 43% and eliminated 18 hours/week of manual updates across HRIS, Okta, and IT tickets
- Finovo Capital reduced average vendor approval time from 5.2 days to 1.9 days with dual‑approval and SLA reminders

Business goals (12 months post‑GA)
- $1.5M ARR
- 50 paying customers with 2+ production workflows each
- 70% of trials convert to pilots; 40% of pilots convert to paid within 60 days

Brand constraints and preferences
- Personality: decisive, pragmatic, proof‑driven. Internally we describe it as Hero + Sage: bold about outcomes, precise about how.
- Voice: short sentences, concrete examples, numbers over adjectives.
- Visuals: modern SaaS palette (teal/black/white) with high contrast. Avoid neon gradients and cartoonish illustrations.
- We never say “AI automates everything.” We say “AI routes work and humans approve with guardrails.”

Buying insights
- Decision makers: Head/Director of Operations, VP RevOps, COO at smaller firms; Finance leaders for approval use cases; IT approves security and SSO/SAML
- Influencers: Operations Managers, Systems Admins, BizOps/RevOps ICs
- Common objections: “We already use Zapier/Make,” “Approvals live in ServiceNow,” “AI is risky for approvals,” “We don’t have time to migrate”
- Rebuttals: policy‑aware routing + immutable logs, in‑Slack approval UX, weeks to value, runs alongside existing tools, start with one high‑leverage workflow
