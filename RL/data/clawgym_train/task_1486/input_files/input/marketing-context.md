Audience
- Small coworking space in Bogotá (staff + front-desk check-in kiosks)
- Decision makers: operations manager and founder
- Technical literacy: moderate; prioritize reliability and seller reputation over bleeding-edge specs

Tone & Style
- Professional, data-driven, concise
- Friendly but firm; focus on total cost of ownership (TCO) and risk reduction
- Ready to walk away from suspicious deals

Key Constraints
- Budget cap: 18,000,000 COP total (target ≤ 1,500,000 COP per unit for 12 units)
- Timeline: up to 3 weeks (open to waiting for better price or end-of-month promos)
- Quality tolerance: used/refurbished acceptable; require solid seller history, returns window, and at least 1–3 months warranty

Negotiation Priorities
- Anchor first with recent sold-price median in COP
- Bundle purchase leverage (12 units): ask for 3–5% volume discount or accessories (docks, chargers)
- Avoid sellers with red flags: crypto/wire only, no returns, pickup-only, or unknown third‑party storefronts

Psychology Cues to Use
- Anchoring: lead with a data-backed unit target based on recent sold median
- Loss Aversion: highlight risk of downtime and returns cost if warranty is missing
- Social Proof: cite staff count and ongoing purchasing for additional seats to signal repeat business potential

Success Indicators
- Achieve ≤ 1,300,000–1,400,000 COP per unit for at least 12 units from reputable sellers
- Written invoice, returns window ≥ 15 days, warranty ≥ 3 months for most units
- Clear payment via MercadoPago/credit (no crypto or gift cards)
