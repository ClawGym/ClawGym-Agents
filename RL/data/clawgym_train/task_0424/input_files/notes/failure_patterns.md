# Failure Patterns and Heuristics

Over the last few ventures, recurring anti-patterns have appeared:

- Chasing novelty over a clear, monetizable customer need.
- No paying customer defined (unclear buyer, user vs. purchaser confusion).
- Mistaking lack of competition as a positive signal (often indicates no real demand or distribution challenges).
- Vague problem statements that are hard to validate or measure.

Novelty words to watch (case-insensitive): disrupt, disruptive, revolutionary, reinvent

Use these as heuristics to flag risky framing in ideas and to temper the value proposition language.
