# Diagnostic Answers — Insider Perspective

Author: Growth Operations Manager, NovaMart China (2022–2024)

## Q1: Your Role and Access
I led growth operations for NovaMart China, owning campaign calendar planning, KOL negotiations for Douyin, and the promotion budget for 120+ SKUs across beauty, wellness, and snacks. I had direct access to campaign P&L, rebate accruals, SKU-level conversion data, and post-event post-mortems. I also coordinated with warehouse ops on inventory buffers and with global compliance on content approval.

## Q2: The Internal vs. External Story Gap
Externally, our 2023 “channel optimization” narrative emphasized focusing on higher-ROI channels. Internally, the trigger was rebate abuse and compliance friction. We discovered that KOL deals front-loaded GMV to meet quarterly targets, but returns and cancellations surged afterward. Next-day cancellation rates reached ~14% during deep-coupon weeks (baseline ~7%). Also, global-led content approval took ~72 hours, making us miss timely memes; local competitors could approve in ~12–18 hours, costing us ~0.8–1.2 percentage points in CTR during trend windows.

## Q3: The “This Makes No Sense” Observation
Our quarterly bonus plan paid teams on GMV, not contribution margin. KOL contracts often required 50% prepayment with GMV targets. This encouraged broad, low-quality traffic and heavy couponing. In September 2023, we ran an A/B on 30 SKUs: we cut rebate caps by 20% for the test group. GMV dipped ~4%, but contribution margin rose by 2.7 percentage points, returns fell by 3 points, and net profit per order increased ~9%. It made no sense that the company still called the control’s higher GMV the “win.”

## Q4: The Structural Disadvantage (Specific)
Two concrete bottlenecks:
1) Content approval SLA asymmetry: ~72 hours for us (global legal/compliance chain) vs. ~12–18 hours for local brands. We routinely missed “trend windows,” lowering CTR and pushing us to compensate with higher rebates.
2) Split P&L governance: growth owned promotions, ops owned fulfillment. During live-stream spikes, thin inventory buffers caused stockouts, emergency air freight (costly), and late deliveries. In March–April 2024, raising the minimum “days of cover” threshold from 7 to 12 for pre-scheduled live events reduced stockouts by ~35% and emergency air freight by ~41%, while on-time delivery improved ~6 percentage points.

## Q5: The Non-Textbook Solution
If I were China CEO for one year, I would:
- Implement a Contribution-Margin Budget Gate: promotions and KOL deals must pass a projected contribution margin threshold before approval. GMV-only targets would be sunset.
- Introduce Two-Stage KOL Contracts: smaller upfront, with a pay-out kicker tied to post-7-day net revenue (after returns).
- Enforce a Pre-Event Logistics Freeze: 10–14 days before big live streams, lock pricing and SKU lists; require ≥12 days of cover in regional warehouses to avoid air freight.
These are not standard “more localization” suggestions; they directly address incentive misalignment and spike logistics that quietly destroy profitability.
