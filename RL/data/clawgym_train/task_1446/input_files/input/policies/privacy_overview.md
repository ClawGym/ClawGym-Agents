# Privacy Overview

This overview summarizes our privacy principles and how they relate to data retention and user rights.

## Principles

We commit to transparency, security, and strict data retention aligned with legal and business needs.
We limit PII collection to what is necessary and retain it only as long as required.

Definition: Data Controller - The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
Definition: Anonymization - An irreversible process that transforms data so individuals are not identifiable.
Definition: CCPA - California Consumer Privacy Act providing rights such as access and deletion for California residents.
Definition: GDPR - General Data Protection Regulation establishing principles including storage limitation, lawfulness, and transparency.

## Legal Compliance

Our data retention practices reflect the storage limitation principle in GDPR.
Where applicable, we disclose data retention periods as part of our privacy notice to meet CCPA and GDPR requirements.
PII retention is documented by system and dataset, and regular audits verify adherence.

## Data Subject Rights

- Access: Individuals can request access to their PII.
- Deletion: We delete PII upon verified request unless a legal obligation requires retention.
- Portability: Where feasible, we provide machine-readable copies of PII.

## Governance

The Privacy Committee reviews data retention schedules quarterly.
System owners must ensure their services implement the approved data retention timelines.

## Communications

We publish high-level data retention expectations in external privacy notices.
Internal runbooks detail how engineering teams enforce deletion and retention.

## Training

All staff receive annual training covering GDPR fundamentals and the linkage between privacy rights and data retention.

## Monitoring

Metrics track deletion job success rates and the age distribution of stored PII.
Alerts trigger when data retention thresholds are exceeded.

End of overview.
