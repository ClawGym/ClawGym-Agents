To our neighbors and friends,

I am writing as the spouse of the official who has chosen to speak up. I support the decision to expose wrongdoing, even though I am worried about what comes next for our family. We value accountability and we believe that sunlight can help repair trust. At the same time, I am anxious about retaliation, rumors, and how this might affect our children’s day-to-day lives.

This draft is my attempt to explain where we stand. I know emotions are running high. Some people have said there is a cover-up, and others think it’s a vendetta. Regardless of labels, I want to be careful with what we share publicly. I don’t want to put private details into the world or say something that could escalate tensions. We need calm, patience, and a commitment to due process.

Our family believes that institutions can be better when people tell the truth. I also don’t want our home life to be consumed by conflict. We are trying to keep a routine, check on one another, and stay grounded. If I could ask one thing, it would be for space and kindness while this plays out.

I plan to include a few short quotes from supportive voices to show this is bigger than just us. But I want to avoid overly charged language, and I don’t want to repeat anything that could cause harm. I am asking for help to make this letter more measured, more focused on accountability and compassion, and less likely to put us at risk.

Thank you for reading and for looking out for each other.

— A concerned spouse
