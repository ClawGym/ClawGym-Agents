# Beliefs and Fulfillment Notes

## Growth Mindset and Fulfillment
Reflecting on Dweck's work, embracing challenge often coincides with a sense of progress. This may relate to fulfillment through learning-oriented beliefs.
Keywords: belief, growth mindset, learning
Draft summary: My current belief is that a growth mindset might support feeling fulfilled because it frames challenges as opportunities.

## Fixed Mindset Costs
When beliefs feel rigid, I notice avoidance of feedback and increased self-criticism. This seems tied to reduced satisfaction after setbacks.
Keywords: fixed mindset, avoidance, self-judgment
Draft summary: I suspect holding rigid beliefs leads to avoidance and less satisfaction; I'm not 100% sure but it seems plausible.

## Intrinsic Motivation Beliefs
Autonomy and values-aligned goals often sustain effort longer than external rewards. This belief might help tasks feel meaningful.
Keywords: intrinsic motivation, autonomy, values
Draft summary: When I believe my actions align with values, tasks feel more meaningful and satisfying.
