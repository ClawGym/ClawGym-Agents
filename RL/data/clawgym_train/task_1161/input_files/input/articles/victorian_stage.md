Title: Victorian Stage Rouge
Era: Victorian (1860s-1880s)

Overview:
Stage performers favored vivid rouge and pale powder under gaslight to read from a distance.

Materials:
- Rice powder (pale)
- Carmine-based rouge (cake)
- Beeswax pomade
- Kohl pencil

Steps:
1) Prepare skin with a thin layer of pomade.
2) Dust rice powder for a pale finish.
3) Warm the rouge cake and tap onto cheeks in circles.
4) Define eyes with kohl; keep line simple.
5) Set lightly with powder.

Cautions:
- Carmine can stain; test on a small area first.
- Avoid heavy rouge in daylight—it was designed for stage lighting.
