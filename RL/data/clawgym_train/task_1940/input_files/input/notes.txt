Relocation notes for senior SWE couple (mid-2026)

Must-haves
- Fiber internet at least 300 Mbps (prefer 500+ if available)
- Strong AC and reliable heating (split units) — buildings can be cold in winter and hot in summer
- Soundproofed windows or rear-facing unit away from loud streets
- Secure building with controlled access; elevator a must
- Space for a proper desk and external monitor; stable power for summer heat waves
- Within 10 minutes’ walk of a sizable park (Bosques de Palermo, Plaza Armenia/Serrano, Barrancas de Belgrano; if Puerto Madero, near Costanera Sur)
- Within 10 minutes’ walk of Subte Line D or B if possible; quick access to buses 39/60/67 acceptable

Nice-to-have
- Building amenities (gym/pool) or quality nearby gym within 10 minutes
- Balcony with morning light (east-facing)
- Laundry in-building or a good lavandería within two blocks
- Quiet, tree-lined street with cafe culture and grocery options nearby

Must-avoid
- Bars/clubs directly below or across the street; especially on loud Palermo Soho party blocks (e.g., parts of Honduras, Serrano late-night stretches)
- First floors on major avenues with bus traffic (e.g., Av. Santa Fe, Av. Cabildo) due to noise and dust
- Once and Constitución at night; La Boca outside Caminito
- Apartments without AC or with only weak portable units

Budget signals (for agent’s calculations)
- Target 1BR rent: $600–700 USD; can stretch to $800 USD for modern building with amenities
- Groceries (couple): ~$350–450 USD/month
- Dining out (couple): ~$300–400 USD/month (mix of casual and a nice dinner every other week)
- Coworking: mid-range, expect ~$120–150 USD/month for one hot desk (may use 2–3 days/week)
- Transport: Mostly Subte/bus; occasional Uber/Cabify nights out

Commute and routine
- Work from home 3–4 days/week; 2 trips/week to coworking (Palermo/Belgrano)
- 1 trip/month to Microcentro or Puerto Madero offices/meetings
- Max preferred commute: 30 minutes door-to-door on transit

Neighborhood lean
- Palermo: Prefer Soho/Hollywood edges that are quieter but walkable; near Line D stations (Palermo, Carranza, Olleros) ideal
- Belgrano: Near green spaces (Barrancas) and Line D (Olleros/Juramento access)
- Puerto Madero: Only if building amenities and quiet; proximity to Costanera Sur for runs/walks

Dining & lifestyle
- Enjoys steakhouses, wine bars, and cafes; late dinners okay but home base should be calm
- Comfortable using Uber/Cabify at night; taxis acceptable when convenient
- Safety is a high priority; well-lit streets preferred

Misc
- Bringing own laptop/monitor; aware electronics are pricey locally
- Spanish A2; will take weekly lessons
- Open to 12+ month lease if place checks all boxes
