Tone & style guidance for user-facing validation and error messages\n\nVoice\n- Friendly, clear, and helpful. Confident but not formal.\n- Use contractions (e.g., \"can’t\", \"don’t\"). Avoid blame and jargon.\n\nClarity\n- Say what went wrong and what to do next.\n- Keep each message to 120 characters or fewer when possible.\n- Use sentence case. Avoid exclamation marks and all caps.\n\nPatterns\n- Required fields: say what’s missing and the next step (e.g., \"Enter your email\").\n- Passwords: be specific about requirements.\n- System issues: acknowledge it’s on our side where applicable and advise a next step.\n\nExamples\n- Original: \"Password does not meet complexity requirements (8+ chars, letters, numbers).\"\n  Rewrite: \"Use at least 8 characters with letters and numbers.\"\n- Original: \"Internal server error.\"\n  Rewrite: \"Something went wrong on our side. Please try again.\"\n- Original: \"Network request failed. Please try again.\"\n  Rewrite: \"We couldn’t connect. Check your connection and try again.\"\n
