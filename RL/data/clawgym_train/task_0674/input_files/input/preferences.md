# Lifestyle & Neighborhood Preferences

## Priorities
- Walkable streets, cafés, and groceries within 10 minutes on foot
- Quiet at night on weekdays; okay with some nightlife nearby on weekends
- Good natural light, modern bathroom, decent insulation if possible
- Access to riverside paths for running/walking; green spaces for breaks
- Easy access to coworking (2x/week), otherwise work from home

## Neighborhood Likes
- Trendy but residential: Príncipe Real, Santos, Alcântara
- Also considering: Campo de Ourique (quiet/local), Arroios (value), Parque das Nações (modern/quiet/waterfront)
- Not ideal: Very tourist-heavy cores (Baixa/Chiado) or steep hill climbs to front door daily

## Transport Preferences
- No car; want strong public transport
- Plan to get a Navegante card and use metro/trams/buses/ferries
- Comfortable biking on flatter routes and along the river
- OK with Green and Yellow metro lines; proximity to Cais do Sodré station is a plus (for ferries and trains)

## Work & Social
- Remote schedule skewed late afternoons/evenings for US overlap
- Interested in tech meetups and low-key music venues
- Prefers cafés with good Wi‑Fi and coworking spaces with quiet zones

## Fitness & Outdoors
- Outdoor-friendly workouts using Lisbon hills/stairs and waterfront paths
- Weekend surfing at Costa da Caparica when weather allows
- Enjoys morning runs in cooler months and sunset walks year-round

## Budget & Housing
- Target rent: €1,200–€1,600 for a 1BR, furnished if possible
- Willing to live semi-central for value if transit is great
- Avoid heavy street noise and bars immediately under the apartment
