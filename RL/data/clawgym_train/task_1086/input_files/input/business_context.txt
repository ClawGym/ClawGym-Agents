Business: Solo consultant specializing in marketing analytics and automation for B2B startups (retainer + project work). I spend ~10–15 hours/week on client work and the rest on sales, content, and admin.

Key tools:
- Google Workspace (Gmail, Calendar, Sheets)
- Airtable (simple CRM)
- Notion (project management, meeting notes, templates)
- Stripe (one-off invoices and subscription payments)
- Calendly (prospect and client scheduling)
- Typeform (lead magnet and mini-surveys)
- ConvertKit (newsletter + tagging)
- Slack (personal workspace for alerts)
- Webflow (site/blog with RSS)
- LinkedIn and Twitter (content distribution)

Constraints and preferences:
- Keep monthly automation spend under $30.
- I do not want to self-host or maintain servers (prefer Zapier or Make).
- Security and data privacy matter (handle PII minimally, avoid copying payment data).
- I want failure alerts in Slack (#ops-alerts) and escalation by email for persistent failures.
- I prefer simple, reliable automations over complex ones; start with 2–4 steps then iterate.
- Working hours are typically 9am–6pm local time; avoid sending client emails outside business hours when possible.

Pain points (weekly):
- Manually copying web/contact form leads into Airtable, sending intro emails, and creating follow-up tasks.
- Calendly bookings require creating a Notion meeting page and emailing prep resources.
- Reconciling Stripe payments: marking invoices paid, sending receipts, and updating a revenue tracker.
- Copying Typeform lead magnet sign-ups into Sheets and tagging in ConvertKit.
- Posting blog excerpts to LinkedIn/Twitter.
- Compiling a weekly status digest from Notion tasks.

Goals:
- Reduce repetitive admin time by ~5–8 hours/month.
- Ensure reliable notifications so silent failures don’t cause missed leads or payments.
- Keep workflows documented and easy to maintain.
