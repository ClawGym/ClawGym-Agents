Background: Drug Y is a biologic therapy investigated as add-on treatment for patients with moderate-to-severe asthma characterized by eosinophilic inflammation.

Methods: In a randomized controlled trial that was double-blind, placebo-controlled, and multicenter, adults with uncontrolled asthma on inhaled corticosteroid/long-acting β2-agonist therapy who met inclusion criteria (including blood eosinophils ≥300 cells/µL) and no exclusion criteria (e.g., smoking >10 pack-years, recent respiratory infection) were randomized 1:1 to Drug Y 200 mg subcutaneously every 4 weeks or placebo for 48 weeks. The primary endpoint was the annualized rate of asthma exacerbations requiring systemic corticosteroids. Secondary endpoints included change from baseline in pre-bronchodilator FEV1, Asthma Control Questionnaire-5 (ACQ-5) score, rescue medication use, and exacerbation-related healthcare utilization.

Results: Of 620 participants randomized, 602 (97%) received ≥1 dose and were included in the primary analysis. The annualized exacerbation rate was 0.85 with Drug Y versus 1.35 with placebo (rate ratio 0.63; 95% CI 0.52–0.77; p<0.001). Mean FEV1 improved by +180 mL with Drug Y versus +60 mL with placebo (between-group difference +120 mL; p=0.02). ACQ-5 scores improved by −0.45 with Drug Y versus −0.20 with placebo (difference −0.25; p=0.01). Efficacy was observed by week 12 and sustained through week 48. Adverse events were similar between groups; the most common were injection-site reactions (10% Drug Y vs 8% placebo) and nasopharyngitis (12% vs 11%). Serious adverse events occurred in 3% of participants in each group; hypersensitivity events were rare. Exploratory analyses suggested greater benefit in patients with higher baseline eosinophil counts.

Conclusions: Among patients with eosinophilic asthma, Drug Y reduced exacerbations and improved lung function over 48 weeks with a safety profile comparable to placebo. Generalizability to non-eosinophilic populations requires further study.
