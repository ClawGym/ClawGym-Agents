Financial Stability and Sanctions — Draft (Aronia)

We support targeted sanctions to pressure specific actors while minimizing collateral damage and preserving humanitarian channels.

We oppose broad sanctions that indiscriminately harm civilians and can entrench black markets without achieving policy goals.

Temporary capital controls can dampen speculative surges during crises; stronger capital controls may be warranted in thin markets to preserve monetary autonomy.

Fiscal consolidation should avoid blunt austerity; austerity without safeguards can deepen downturns and undermine social cohesion.

Privatization of select state-owned banks should proceed only with robust regulatory oversight and credible resolution frameworks.
