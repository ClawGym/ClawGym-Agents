I live in Pine County, Riverland. In 2002, I was convicted of a nonviolent felony. I completed parole in 2006 and finished paying all fines and costs by 2007—I've kept the receipts. In 2019, I tried to register to vote because I believed my rights had been restored. During the 2020 general election, a poll worker told me I still could not vote and pointed to “Election Code §12.4(b).” I was turned away. 

What hurt most is that Riverland’s legislature passed HB-88 in 2018 making it clear that once a person completes their sentence, they do not have to keep paying off fees and costs to vote. Even though the law changed, the county office kept using old forms and gave me outdated guidance. In 2022, I tried again, bringing my 2007 receipt and a printout of the amendment, but I was told their system still showed me as ineligible. I left without a ballot a second time.

I’ve lived here my whole life, I pay taxes, and I care deeply about my community. The law says my rights should be restored, but outdated rules and misinformation have kept me from the polls. I’m speaking up because I want my vote to count and I don’t want anyone else to go through this.
