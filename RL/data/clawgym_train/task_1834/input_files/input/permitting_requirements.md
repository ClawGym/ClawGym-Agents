# City of Phoenix Permitting & APS Interconnection — Residential PV

AHJ: City of Phoenix, Planning & Development Department  
Utility: APS (Arizona Public Service) — Residential Customer Generation (RCP Net Billing)

## Permits Required
- Building Permit (Residential Roof-Mounted PV)
- Electrical Permit
- Structural review/letter for truss roofs (engineer letter required when truss or when edge distances/loads necessitate verification)
- HOA approval letter (flush-mount, all-black hardware per HOA)

Typical City Fees (subject to change):
- Building permit: ~$300
- Electrical permit: ~$150

## Plan Set Requirements
- Site plan with array location, setbacks, and equipment placement (inverter, AC disconnect)
- Roof plan with attachment layout, rail spans, and module count
- Electrical single-line diagram (stringing/microinverters, conductor sizing, OCPD, grounding)
- Spec sheets: modules, inverter(s), racking (e.g., IronRidge/Unirac), rapid shutdown devices
- Structural calculations or engineer’s letter confirming roof capacity and attachment details

## Inspection
- Building + Electrical final inspections; schedule within 3–5 business days after install completion
- Provide torque specs, labeling, and rapid shutdown verification at inspection

## APS Interconnection (RCP Net Billing)
- Pre-construction interconnection application required
- Post-install as-built and passed inspections required before APS Permission to Operate (PTO)
- Export credit under RCP (approx. $0.076/kWh) applied to net exports
- PTO timeline: ~2–6 weeks after final inspection approval
- Meter upgrade typically not required under 10 kW residential; verify during application

## Typical Timelines (Subject to Backlogs)
- Permit review: 2–3 weeks (can extend to 4 weeks during peak season)
- Equipment procurement: 1–2 weeks
- Roof install: 1–2 days
- Electrical work: 0.5–1 day (round to 1 day on schedule)
- Final inspection: 1–2 weeks
- APS PTO: 2–6 weeks after inspection pass

## Notes & Constraints
- Maintain fire setbacks per Phoenix code (typically 36” pathways; verify current code)
- Conduit runs through attic where possible; exterior runs painted to match
- Rapid shutdown per NEC 690.12 required
- Labeling per NEC and APS requirements
- Ensure ampacity calculations for 200A MSP with PV backfeed; common solutions include 225A bus with 200A main or 120% rule placement as applicable
- For microinverter/optimizer systems, provide module-level rapid shutdown documentation
