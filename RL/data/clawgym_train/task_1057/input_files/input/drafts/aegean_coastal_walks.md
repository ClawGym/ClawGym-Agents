# Aegean Coastal Walks: from Ayvalık to Foça

When I walked this stretch years ago for a guidebook update, the olive groves felt endless and the wind kept rearranging my plans; I’d jot notes under a fig tree, then follow a path just to see where it curled back toward the sea. This is me thinking aloud about angles for a slow-travel piece—probably too chatty for the first paragraph. There are easy cliff paths, coves where the water stays calm, and village squares that come alive at sunset when the tables fill with mezze. Ferries shuffle in and out, and mornings start late with coffee and simit under a stubborn sun. I want to keep the mood but trim the fat.

## Highlights
- Olive groves and stone villages
- Sea breezes along cliff paths
- Sunset mezze in harbors
- Island ferries and lazy mornings

## Practicalities
Public transport links are decent on weekends; bring a hat and plenty of water in summer.
