# Call with Prof. Eleanor Whitcombe — Preliminary collaboration on ancient scripts
Date (UTC): 2025-09-14 16:00–16:45
Participants: Me (doctoral researcher in epigraphy), Prof. Eleanor Whitcombe (Institute for Comparative Epigraphy)

Agenda
- Introductions and overview of interests
- Comparative discussion: Brahmi vs. Kharoṣṭhī and possible Aramaic influence
- Methods for documenting letterform variants (epigraphy methodology)
- Feasibility of joint workshop and shared corpus annotations

Notes
- Prof. W emphasized tracing Brahmi letterforms via securely dated inscriptions; cautioned against over-reliance on later manuscripts.
- We discussed Kharoṣṭhī ductus variations in the northwest; she highlighted slope angles and ductus order as key discriminants.
- Q: Are there digitized squeezes or estampages from Gandharan sites under an open license that we could mine for letterforms?
- On Aramaic influence: Prof. W prefers speaking in terms of scripts as systems rather than a one-to-one lineage; suggested surveying Imperial Aramaic letterforms for functional parallels.
- Epigraphy methodology: she recommended documenting stroke order hypotheses explicitly alongside photos and tracings.
- Q: Which corpus metadata fields are essential for cross-institutional interoperability (e.g., museum IDs vs. persistent URIs)?
- We touched on Old Persian cuneiform as a teaching case for introducing novices to sign inventories.
- Radiocarbon dating of manuscripts: she noted that AMS reports can be uneven; we should include lab uncertainty and calibration references in our corpus.
