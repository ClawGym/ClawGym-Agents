University Privacy and Student Records Policy (Excerpts)
Effective: August 1, 2024

1. Education Records and Access
- The University complies with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).
- Education records are records that are directly related to a student and maintained by the University or a party acting for the University, regardless of format.
- Students (or parents when applicable) have the right to inspect and review their education records within 45 days of the University’s receipt of a request.
- Internal access to education records is limited to school officials with a legitimate educational interest, meaning the information is needed to fulfill professional responsibilities for the University.

2. Use of Student Data for Success and Advising
- The University may use learning management system (LMS) activity, assignment timing, and other engagement indicators to support academic advising and student success initiatives.
- Advisors and designated Student Success personnel may access such indicators when performing their advising duties under legitimate educational interest.
- Email headers from University systems (e.g., to/from identifiers and timestamps) may be treated as education records when linked to an identifiable student; the University does not authorize the use of subject lines or email body content for advising analytics without additional approval.

3. Directory Information
- The University defines directory information as: name, local and permanent address, University email address, telephone number, major field of study, class level, enrollment status, dates of attendance, degrees and awards received, and participation in officially recognized activities and sports.
- Student identification numbers are not directory information unless they cannot be used to access education records without a PIN or password.
- Students may opt out of directory information disclosures at any time by submitting the Directory Opt‑Out Form to the Registrar. Opt‑out prevents the University from releasing directory information to external parties but does not limit internal use where legitimate educational interest applies.

4. Consent for Disclosure
- Written consent of the student is generally required before the University discloses education records to third parties, except as permitted by FERPA exceptions.
- Consent must specify the records to be disclosed, the purpose of the disclosure, and the parties to whom the disclosure may be made. Electronic consent is acceptable if it authentically identifies the student and indicates approval.

5. FERPA Exceptions (Summary)
- The University may disclose education records without consent to: school officials with legitimate educational interest; another institution where the student seeks or intends to enroll; parties in connection with financial aid; accrediting organizations; compliance with a judicial order or lawfully issued subpoena (with notice to the student unless prohibited); and appropriate officials in cases of health and safety emergencies.
- Directory information may be released without consent unless a student has exercised the right to opt out.

6. Data Minimization and Security
- University offices must collect and retain only the minimum student data necessary to fulfill academic or administrative purposes.
- Student success analytics must avoid using sensitive content where a less intrusive indicator is sufficient.
- Access to analytics dashboards is role‑based and logged; quarterly reviews are required.

7. Transparency and Student Rights
- The University will publish a Student Success Analytics Notice describing categories of data used, purposes, and safeguards.
- Upon request, students may receive a summary of data used for advising analytics and may challenge inaccuracies through the established amendment process.
- The Registrar’s Office will coordinate responses to requests for access within 45 days.

8. Retention and Destruction
- Engagement indicators and derived risk classifications may be retained for up to two years for longitudinal evaluation and continuous improvement, after which they must be securely destroyed unless a longer period is approved for research with appropriate oversight.

9. Contact
- Questions about this policy may be directed to the Office of the Registrar or the Office of General Counsel.
