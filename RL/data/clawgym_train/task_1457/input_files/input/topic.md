Setting: You plan to meet your friend Daniel at a quiet coffee shop. He is a kind, devoted Christian who believes the Trinity. He says the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit are coequal and coeternal. He often mentions John 1:1 and Matthew 28:19. He respects the Bible and likes calm, friendly talks. You have about ten minutes before he needs to leave.

Daniel’s view: He believes Jesus is fully God. He feels that if Jesus is not equal to the Father, then our salvation would be at risk. He sees the Holy Spirit as a person in the Godhead. He is open to reading scriptures together.

Your goal: Share clear, simple verses that let Jesus speak for himself and show that Jesus is God’s Son and is subject to his Father. Keep a warm tone. Use a father-and-son illustration to make the point gentle and easy to grasp. Emphasize how this view draws us closer to Jehovah and to Jesus in the right way.

Approach: Begin by acknowledging Daniel’s deep respect for Jesus. Explain that you also love Jesus and want to honor him as the Bible presents him. Then read two clear scriptures that show Jesus’ relationship to his Father. Follow with two more verses that show the headship order and Jesus’ role assigned by God. Keep the language simple and easy to read aloud. Use Jehovah’s name naturally to highlight the Father’s loving role.

Desired outcome: Daniel feels respected and heard. He sees from the Bible that Jesus lovingly submits to his Father, Jehovah, and that this does not lessen Jesus’ honor. He leaves with a positive view of continuing the conversation another time.
