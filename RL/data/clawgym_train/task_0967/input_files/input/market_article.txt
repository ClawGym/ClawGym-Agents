Title: Mid-Tier Banking Tech 2024–2025 — Migrations, Open Banking, and AI Risk Controls
Byline: MarketView Research — 2025-05-20

Summary
European mid-tier banks accelerated cloud migrations in 2024–2025 under PSD3 and evolving EBA guidance. Vendors with strong reliability and compliance stories are winning multi-year extensions. Helios Bank, among others, is transitioning risk analytics to a modern platform while tightening authentication and AI governance.

Named Companies and Products
- VoltEdge Technologies (platform vendor; products: Nexus Platform, Atlas Risk Engine, Harbor Data Lake, Riverton Pipelines, Sentinel IAM, Beacon Monitoring)
- Helios Bank (EU bank; HQ: Frankfurt)
- Northstar Fintech (competitor; recent acquisition: Quill Data)
- RiverPay Systems (payment gateway; API-first)
- Orion Cloud (IaaS; eu-central-2 region cited for banking workloads)

Timeline and Events
- 2024-09-05: Northstar Fintech acquires Quill Data to bolster ETL tooling in regulated markets. This puts pressure on rivals to retire older pipelines.
- 2024-11-12: U.S. CFPB proposes the Section 1033 Open Banking rule; EU PSD3 negotiations intensify in parallel.
- 2025-03-22: EU PSD3 trilogue reaches provisional agreement; banks prepare for Strong Customer Authentication updates and data access harmonization.
- 2025-04-17: VoltEdge reports a contained staging data exposure (harbor-lake-staging), lasting 42 minutes; no customer PII involved. EKM guardrails introduced afterward.
- 2025-05-15: Helios Bank signs a 24-month extension with VoltEdge (~$4.5M TCV), citing reliability and PSD3 roadmap alignment. Target SLA: 99.95%.
- 2025-06-30: Helios Bank targets cutover of credit risk analytics to VoltEdge’s Nexus Platform running on Orion Cloud (eu-central-2), with Atlas Risk Engine explainability enabled by default.

Regulatory and Policy Context
- PSD3 (EU Payment Services Directive 3): Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) and standardized data access are top priorities for 2025. Vendors offering “policy packs” for PSD3 compliance have an edge.
- EU AI Act: High-risk AI systems used in credit assessment must include transparency, logging, human oversight, and bias monitoring. Atlas Risk Engine configurations are being evaluated by several banks for out-of-the-box compliance.
- Basel III Endgame: Capital requirement adjustments affect credit models’ thresholds; risk engines must provide traceability to satisfy auditors.
- GDPR: Data residency in EU regions (e.g., Frankfurt) and encryption with customer-managed keys (EKM) remain table stakes.
- EBA Outsourcing Guidelines: Emphasis on incident response SLAs, audit trails, and key management.

Market Shares and KPIs (2025E)
- VoltEdge: ~12% share among EU mid-tier banks; RiverPay: ~9%; Northstar Fintech: ~18%.
- Total addressable market for compliant risk+data platforms in EU mid-tier banks: ~$2.1B (CAGR ~14% through 2028).
- Winning KPIs: 99.95% SLA, <15 min P1 triage, comprehensive audit logs, FIDO2/WebAuthn for admin access.

Helios Bank Case Study
- Motivation: Align with PSD3 timelines and enhance explainability in credit decisioning.
- Scope: Migrate credit risk analytics from on-prem to Nexus Platform; integrate Sentinel IAM for FIDO2; enable Atlas Risk Engine explainability and fairness reports.
- Region: Orion Cloud eu-central-2 (Frankfurt) to meet GDPR.
- Governance: Atlas configured as “high-risk AI” under EU AI Act; periodic bias audits and human-in-the-loop approvals introduced.
- People: Helios Bank CIO Anika Vogel sponsors the program; day-to-day run by Program Manager Tomasz Lewandowski.
- Dependencies: EBA audit-readiness; Basel III documentation updates for model thresholds.

Competitive Notes
- Northstar’s Quill Data acquisition (2024-09-05) pressures VoltEdge to sunset its legacy Quill ETL pipeline, which it has begun replacing with Riverton Pipelines.
- RiverPay is pushing an Open Banking sandbox for PSD3, but lacks integrated AI explainability; several banks trialed it only for payments, not risk.
- Vendors emphasizing “policy packs” (PSD3, EBA, GDPR, EU AI Act) and passwordless admin access are outperforming peers.

Security Posture Highlights
- Following the 2025-04-17 incident, VoltEdge tightened EKM policies to deny non-regional keys and added Terraform guardrails for storage buckets.
- Sentinel IAM adoption surged after multiple banks moved to enforce FIDO2 for privileged roles.

Quotes
- “Reliability plus ready-to-audit controls are determining winners,” says MarketView’s senior analyst, Carla Moretti.
- Helios Bank CIO Anika Vogel: “We selected VoltEdge because Nexus and Atlas met our PSD3 roadmap and explainability needs without delaying our 2025 cutover.”

Looking Ahead
- Expect PSD3 policy finalization to trigger broader SCA upgrades in H2 2025.
- Basel III Endgame documentation requirements will push vendors to improve model traceability features.
- EU AI Act enforcement phases will move banks to standardize “high-risk AI” controls in platforms like Atlas Risk Engine.
