# BrightOak Brand Voice

Purpose: Keep our writing confident, warm, and plain-spoken. Readers should feel informed, respected, and ready to act—never sold to.

Voice pillars:
- Confident, not cocky: We know our craft and speak with steady clarity.
- Warm and human: Sound like people who care, not like a pitch deck.
- Plain-spoken: Use simple words. Prefer familiar verbs. Cut filler.

Style rules:
- Sentences: Aim for 8–18 words on average. Break long ones.
- Verbs: Prefer active voice. Say “We build…” not “We are building…”.
- Jargon: Avoid buzzwords and clichés. Explain with concrete language.
- Structure: Lead with the point. Support with one clear example.
- Tone: Friendly, respectful, helpful. No hype. No exclamation points.
- Rhythm: Vary sentence length for flow. Keep paragraphs short (3–5 lines).
- Lists: Use lists to clarify steps or features. Keep items parallel.
- Punctuation: Use the Oxford comma; avoid semicolons.
- Tense: Present tense for what the product does; past tense for the story.

Brand phrases to keep (verbatim, case-insensitive):
- BrightOak
- built to be simple
- people-first
- no-nonsense pricing
- privacy by default

Perspective:
- “We” for the team; “you” for the customer.
- Be specific. Instead of “optimize workflows,” say “reduce steps from five to two.”

Reading level:
- Shoot for Grade 8–9. Prefer clarity over ornament.

What to avoid:
- Hype language (e.g., “revolutionary,” “world-class,” “cutting-edge”).
- Empty abstractions (“solutions,” “synergy,” “paradigm”) unless grounded in examples.
- Vague promises. State what we do and how it helps.
