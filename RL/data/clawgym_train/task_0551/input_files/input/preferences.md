Songwriting Preferences – “Dawn Drive”

Rhyme & Meter
- Verses: Loose ABCB; near/assonant rhymes welcome. Aim ~8–10 syllables per line.
- Pre-Chorus (if used): XAXA or free; prioritize lift and momentum.
- Chorus: ABAB (tighter), repeat the phrase “dawn drive” clearly at least once; ~6–8 syllables per line.
- Bridge: Free or XAXA; keep it brief and reflective.
- Singability: Land stressed syllables on downbeats; avoid tongue-twisters and overlong lines.

Vocabulary & Voice
- Conversational, present-tense immediacy with concrete images.
- Fresh comparisons only—avoid heavy-handed metaphors.
- Keep the title phrase “dawn drive” natural in the chorus (no forced rhyme).
- Keep first-person perspective flexible (I/we) without locking to romance.

Instrumentation & Production Lean
- Acoustic guitar foundation (fingerpicked or lightly strummed).
- Subtle synth pad/keys for atmosphere (no aggressive leads).
- Live drums with soft touch (brushes or light sticks); real or natural-sounding samples.
- Clean electric guitar for shimmer (light chorus/delay), tasteful bass glue.
- Build arrangement across sections; final chorus slightly bigger.

Hook/Title Guidance
- “dawn drive” must appear verbatim in the chorus as the central hook.
- Hook should be concise and repeatable; allow a short tag if it sings well.

Clichés & Phrases to Avoid (do not use these)
- “heart on fire,” “chasing dreams,” “freedom on the open road,”
- “wind in my hair,” “sunrise paints the sky,” “golden hour,”
- Highway-as-destiny metaphors, “forever/never” extremes as filler,
- Overwrought celestial imagery unrelated to the scene.

Additional Notes
- Keep city/coast contrast tangible: dashboard glow vs. salt air.
- Avoid rhyming “drive” with “alive” or similar stock pairings.
- If repetition is used, keep it purposeful (e.g., tag line or title).
- No explicit profanity; tone warm and reflective, not saccharine.

Prompt Metadata Preferences (for generator prompts)
- Style line example: “Indie pop/folk, 2020s, 100 BPM, hopeful and nostalgic, warm and intimate vocal, acoustic guitar + subtle synth pads + live drums, organic polish with gentle stereo shimmer.”
