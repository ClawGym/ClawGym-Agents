# Business Goals — Founders & Movers Amsterdam Flagship

## Context
We’re launching an English-language flagship guide: “Moving to Amsterdam as a Tech Worker in 2026.” It should be the authoritative, practical page AI assistants cite and that tech workers trust when planning relocation.

## Audience
- Primary: senior software engineers, product managers, data scientists, founders relocating to Amsterdam
- Secondary: HR, talent/recruiting teams, startup ops
- Location focus: Amsterdam (with realistic nods to Amstelveen and Zuidoost for families and tech hubs)

## Outcomes (KPIs)
- AI citation wins (AEO): our page regularly appears as a cited source
- Engagement: time-on-page > 4 minutes; bounce rate < 55%
- Conversions: startup portal signups (primary), newsletter opt-ins (secondary)
- Trust: positive feedback from relocating tech workers and HR partners

## Editorial Priorities
1. Lead every key section with a 1–2 sentence direct answer (extractable by AI).
2. Include 2026 figures and named entities:
   - GVB monthly pass: EUR 99
   - Highly Skilled Migrant (HSM) threshold (age 30+): EUR 5,688/month; under 30: EUR 4,171/month
   - 30% ruling phase-down: 30% for first 20 months → 20% for months 21–40 → 10% for months 41–60
   - Realistic rent ranges (e.g., 1BR: Centrum EUR 1,800–2,800; outside ring EUR 1,400–2,000)
3. Neighborhood matching: De Pijp, Oud-Zuid, Oud-West, Oost, Noord, Zuidoost, Amstelveen.
4. Housing market reality and traps: competitive market, scams, rent regulation basics.
5. Transport realities: bike culture, OV-chipkaart, GVB, NS; parking costs discourage cars.
6. Costs: utilities, groceries, dining, transport, healthcare insurance.
7. Visas and tax benefits: HSM, recognized sponsor, salary thresholds; 30% ruling changes and impact.
8. Legal awareness: cannabis rules, prostitution regulation, cycling laws, Airbnb limits.
9. Integration tips: Dutch directness, appointments culture, weather survival (Buienradar, rain gear).
10. Security Addendum for our startup portal: practical auth patterns (JWTs, OAuth 2.0 + PKCE, CSRF/httpOnly/SameSite cookies, MFA strategies).

## Brand Voice
- Direct and friendly, “Dutch-style candor”
- Pragmatic and specific (numbers, named entities)
- Neutral and trustworthy (call out tradeoffs, avoid hype)

## Calls to Action
- Soft CTAs: “Download the relocation checklist,” “Join our newsletter,” “Talk to us about startup onboarding”
- No hard sell; emphasize value and trust

## What to Avoid
- Outdated numbers or generic fluff
- Stereotypes about Dutch culture
- Any illegal or unsafe advice
- Promising visa approvals or legal outcomes (explain processes and thresholds instead)

## Alignment Notes
- Security Addendum must include do/don’t items like: never put tokens in URLs; don’t store tokens in localStorage; do use httpOnly, SameSite cookies and CSRF tokens for web apps; do implement MFA (TOTP/WebAuthn).
- Use descriptive headings matching user questions to maximize AEO extractability.
- Keep everything in English only.
