# Chapter 1 — Introduction

## 1.1 Background
NovaMart China is the cross-border consumer goods arm of a global e-commerce retailer. It operates flagship stores on Tmall Global and JD Worldwide and increasingly relies on Douyin live-stream commerce. Between 2022 and 2024, GMV grew from RMB 1.2 billion to RMB 1.78 billion. Yet contribution margin declined from 18% to 12% over the same period. This divergence reflects pressure from rising promotion spend, fulfillment complexity, and rapid shifts in platform policies.

## 1.2 Research Problem and Objective
This thesis asks: How can NovaMart China improve marketing operations so that GMV growth translates into sustainable profitability in China’s social commerce ecosystem?

Objectives:
- Describe the external environment, competitive landscape, and internal marketing mix.
- Identify the main factors driving the GMV–profitability gap.
- Propose actionable recommendations to improve marketing efficiency and margins.

## 1.3 Scope
The study focuses on NovaMart China’s consumer goods sub-categories (beauty, wellness, snacks) sold on Tmall Global and Douyin, from January 2022 to December 2024. It examines campaign performance, promotion intensity, and fulfillment processes around major shopping festivals and KOL live-stream events.

## 1.4 Methodology
This is a descriptive, single-case study. Analytical tools include PEST for macro factors, SWOT to evaluate internal strengths and weaknesses, 4P to assess the marketing mix, and Porter’s Five Forces to understand competitive pressures. Data sources include public reports, internal dashboards, and campaign post-mortems supplied by the company’s operations team.

## 1.5 Data Overview
- GMV (RMB): 1.2B (2022), 1.55B (2023), 1.78B (2024)
- Contribution margin: 18% (2022), 14% (2023), 12% (2024)
- Douyin share of GMV: 28% (2022) → 37% (2023) → 43% (2024)
- Rebate/promo expense ratio: 14% (2022) → 18% (2023) → 19% (2024)

## 1.6 Significance
For cross-border sellers, the China market offers scale but demands operational precision. By analyzing NovaMart China’s experience, the thesis seeks to provide guidance to firms balancing growth via social commerce with the need for margin discipline.
