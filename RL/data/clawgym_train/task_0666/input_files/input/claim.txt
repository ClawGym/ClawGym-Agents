The vendor asserts that their platform is both “open-source” and “proprietary” at the same time. They emphasize full transparency around security and privacy practices, yet refuse to publish the source code or third-party security reports “to preserve transparency and prevent confusion.” They also claim to operate with “zero-knowledge data retention,” while simultaneously acknowledging persistent storage of user analytics and behavior metrics for product improvement.

In their marketing, they insist the system is “fully decentralized with no single point of control,” but product documentation shows a mandatory central orchestration service that can disable client access globally during maintenance windows. The company promises “no tracking,” while their privacy policy lists a number of analytics partners with cross-site identifiers.
