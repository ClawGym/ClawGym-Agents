Austin, TX Hazard Notes and Nearby Landmarks

Primary Local Hazards
- Flash flooding: Sudden heavy rainfall can quickly flood low-water crossings, creeks, and underpasses. Austin emphasizes “Turn Around, Don’t Drown.”
- Tornado watches & severe thunderstorms: Fast-moving storms bring damaging wind, hail, and occasional tornadoes. No basements in most homes; shelter on lowest interior level.
- Wildfire smoke days: Regional smoke can reduce air quality; sensitive individuals may need N95 masks and indoor air filtration.
- Occasional ice storms: Tree/line damage, slick roads, extended power outages possible.
- Heat waves: High temps 100°F+; risk of heat illness and grid strain; cooling centers may open.
- Multi-hour power outages: Typically 2–8 hours; can be longer during major events.

Preparedness Tips Tailored to Area
- Tornado/severe thunderstorm: Use the first-floor interior half-bath under the stairs for shelter (no windows, multiple walls around it). Helmets for head protection if available.
- Flash flood: Avoid driving through any water at low-water crossings; plan alternate routes that stay on higher ground and main arteries. Keep paper map backup.
- Wildfire smoke: Keep N95 masks available; set one room as a “clean air” space with door closed and an air purifier (MERV-13 or HEPA). Seal gaps if smoke intrudes.
- Ice storm/winter: Expect tree limbs and lines down; move vehicles away from large tree branches if time permits. Have blankets, warm layers, and a way to safely keep warm. Drip faucets to protect pipes if temperatures fall below freezing for extended periods.
- Heat waves: Hydration and electrolyte packets in go-bags; identify nearest public cooling centers (libraries/recreation centers) if AC fails for long.
- Power outages: NOAA Weather Radio recommended; charge battery packs before storms. Insulin refrigeration backup: small cooler with ice packs; consider evaporative insulin cooling wallet as secondary measure.

Radio & Alerts
- NOAA Weather Radio: Station WXK27 — 162.400 MHz (Austin)
- Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA) enabled on all family phones recommended.
- Local info sources: City of Austin alerts and Austin Energy outage map (check official channels when power/internet available).

Destination/Route Starters (for planning)
- Primary route suggestion: North on S Lamar to Barton Springs Rd, then to MoPac (Loop 1) for regional access.
- Alternate route suggestion: East on Oltorf to I-35 (major corridor), then north or south as conditions dictate.
- Backup route suggestion: Neighborhood streets west to S 5th then to Ben White (US 290/71) to bypass flooded underpasses.
- Avoid during floods: all signed low-water crossings and creek underpasses; particularly Barton Springs Rd under heavy rain and S 1st St at creek dips.

Special Considerations
- Mobility: Stairs required for upstairs bedrooms; plan for assisted descent during evacuation.
- Medical: Insulin requires refrigeration — prepare cooler and ice packs, and identify pharmacy options on both sides of the river for refills if needed.
- Pet: Cat carrier stored upstairs hall closet; keep spare leash/harness (if used) and vaccination records available for shelters that accept pets.
