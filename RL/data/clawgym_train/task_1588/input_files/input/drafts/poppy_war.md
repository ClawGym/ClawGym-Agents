I don’t even know where to start because this book is heavy, like HEAVY, and I kept pausing to breathe. It moves from school vibes to war in a way that felt like falling off a cliff. It’s not nice or gentle!! The tone is sharp, sometimes mean, but it also feels precise, like a blade. I have thoughts about how it uses power and cost and anger and ambition and they’re a mess in my head, sorry. Some scenes were so intense I almost looked away (you’ll know them when you see them), and I was impressed by how everything has consequences, even tiny choices. It’s the kind of story that leaves ash on your hands. Not an easy recommendation, but if you want grimdark that stares back at you and asks how much you’re willing to pay for what you want, this sings—though it sings like smoke.
