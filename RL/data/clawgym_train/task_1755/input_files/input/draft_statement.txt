To the City Council Transportation Committee,

We are frankly fed up with how fast this automation push is barreling ahead. Members are telling us that routes are being tinkered with, schedules are whiplashing, and overtime is dropping without any warning or accountability. People feel like they are being shoved aside to make room for shiny gadgets that slam on the brakes at random. This isn’t a laboratory—it’s our city and our livelihoods. If the plan is to rip jobs out from under veteran drivers just to hit a headline, then you are failing the workforce and the riding public.

We have already seen so-called pilots expand before anyone blinked. There’s talk of more testing, and, despite what vendors claim, safety is not proven. Training and retraining are vague promises that too often evaporate. We expect ironclad protections: no layoffs due to automation, no cutting of hours that guts income, and meaningful paid training pathways into roles like maintenance and dispatch that pay a living wage. Seniority must be respected, and any deployment needs real oversight with worker input—not after-the-fact briefings.

We demand a slowdown right now until these issues are fixed. We will amplify our message in the media and at public hearings if we do not get movement. This is about safety, fairness, and our city’s commitment to people who keep transit running every day.
