Humanization Guide for Loot Flavor Text

Goals
- Keep flavor text short: 1–3 sentences.
- Sound like a human wrote it after the actual event.
- Refer to concrete details (e.g., commit message, incident ID, file names if present).
- Use simple words. Prefer is/are/has over fancy phrasing.

Do not use these banned words or phrases (case-insensitive):
- pivotal
- landscape
- underscore / underscores
- testament
- nestled
- groundbreaking
- seamless
- holistic
- synergy
- realm
- multifaceted
- nuanced
- delve / delves / delving
- tapestry
- vibrant

Common issues to avoid
- Hype or marketing tone. No grand claims.
- Em dash overuse. Use commas or periods instead.
- Rule-of-three lists for rhythm. Vary the sentence lengths.
- Vague attributions like “experts say.” Be specific or cut it.
- Curly quotes. Use straight quotes.

Better patterns
- Specificity: “Named after INC-5000 at 3:07 UTC.”
- Honest voice: “You found the bug by reading the logs the boring way.”
- Small humor is fine, but keep it grounded.

Examples
- OK: “It smells like coffee and hot logs from the db failover.”
- Avoid: “A groundbreaking artifact that stands as a testament to your pivotal role in the evolving landscape of on-call excellence.”

Editing checklist
- Replace puffed-up words with plain ones.
- Remove vague praise and add one concrete detail.
- Read it aloud once. If it sounds like a press release, cut it.
