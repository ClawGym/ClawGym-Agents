# Company Tone & Style Guide (Human-Benefit First)

Voice
- Warm, direct, and helpful. We talk to people, not to “users.”
- Confident without hype. We promise what we can deliver.
- Respect attention. Short sentences. Strong verbs. Clear structure.

Principles
1) Lead with the human benefit
   - Say what changes in their day, not what our tech does.
   - Example: “See your team’s week at a glance. Fewer surprises. More flow.”

2) Translate features into outcomes
   - Feature: “Time zone normalization.”
   - Benefit: “Everyone sees times in their local time. No mental math.”

3) Set up the before/after
   - Before: juggling calendars and HR tools.
   - After: one clean weekly view that catches conflicts early.

4) Be specific and scannable
   - Use headings and bullets.
   - Keep paragraphs short. One idea per paragraph.

5) Accessible and inclusive
   - Write for clarity. Avoid jargon unless it’s industry‑standard and necessary.
   - Support readers with clear labels and descriptive links.

Microcopy patterns
- “You’ll see…”
- “At a glance…”
- “No surprises.”
- “Plan your week in minutes, not hours.”

Words to avoid
- “Leverage,” “utilize,” “state‑of‑the‑art,” “AI‑powered” (unless essential)
- Vague promises like “revolutionary” without proof

Formatting
- Use sentence case for headings unless proper nouns require otherwise.
- Use present tense and second person (“you”) where possible.

Examples
- Good: “Your week, simplified. Important meetings, time‑off, and conflicts in one place.”
- Less good: “Our system aggregates multiple data sources via API integrations.”

Apply this tone to specifications, acceptance criteria, and plans so teams can feel the user value in every decision.
