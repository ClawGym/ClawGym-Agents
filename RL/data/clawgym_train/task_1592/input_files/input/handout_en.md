# Balanced Plate Guide for Busy Professionals

This brief handout supports a balanced, sustainable approach to eating for busy schedules.

## Key Principles
- Build a balanced plate: fill half your plate with vegetables and fruits, one quarter with protein, and one quarter with whole grains.
- Include healthy fats and prioritize fiber at most meals.
- Portion awareness: aim for comfortable fullness, not perfection.
- Hydration matters: keep a water bottle nearby and drink regularly.
- Sustainable habits: plan ahead, stay flexible, and reduce food waste.

## Sample Meal Pattern
- Breakfast: Overnight oats with milk, chia seeds for fiber, and berries (fruits); side of yogurt for protein.
- Lunch: Grain bowl with brown rice (whole grains), beans for protein, mixed vegetables, and olive oil for healthy fats.
- Snack: Apple (fruits) with hummus for protein.
- Dinner: Stir-fry with tofu (protein), broccoli and carrots (vegetables), and quinoa (whole grains); drizzle of olive oil for healthy fats.

## Sustainability Note
Sustainable, balanced eating focuses on small, consistent changes that fit real life. It is flexible, culturally appropriate, budget-friendly, and helps reduce waste over time.

## Allergy Reminder
Always review ingredient lists carefully, and substitute any items that may cause an allergic reaction. If you are unsure, consult your dietitian before trying new products.
