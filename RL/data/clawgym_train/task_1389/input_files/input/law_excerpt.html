Riverland Election Code

§12.4(b) (2014)

Persons convicted of a felony are ineligible to vote until completion of all terms of sentence, including parole and any outstanding court costs and fees.

Note: This clause has been cited inconsistently by local officials and has led to confusion about whether payment of fees and costs beyond the custodial sentence can delay restoration.

HB-88 (2018 Amendment to §12.4)

For purposes of §12.4, “completion of all terms of sentence” shall not include payment of fines, court costs, fees, or restitution that extend beyond the period of incarceration, probation, or parole.

Effective date: July 1, 2018. Codified as §12.4(b-1).

Official note: This amendment clarifies that voting rights are restored upon completion of incarceration, probation, or parole, regardless of outstanding legal financial obligations.
