Trade and Industrial Policy — Draft (Aronia)

Our delegation will articulate a pragmatic stance on trade. We support free trade in intermediate goods to boost efficiency and support lower tariffs on critical inputs. However, we oppose any blanket subsidy that distorts competition and entrenches rent-seeking.

Selective tariff protection for strategic infant industries may be justified only with transparent, measurable criteria and clear sunset clauses that prevent permanent protection.

We support privatization of inefficient state-owned enterprises where competitive markets can function, while maintaining capital controls during transition periods to prevent destabilizing inflows and sudden stops.

Free trade agreements should incorporate labor and environmental standards, paired with adjustment assistance to minimize dislocation while preserving overall gains from openness.
