# AuroraCharge Voice & Messaging

## Brand Voice Overview
AuroraCharge speaks with clarity and purpose. We help people move freely with clean energy.

- Confident, not boastful
- Optimistic, not unrealistic
- Helpful and human
- Pioneering and forward-looking
- Concise and clear

Primary archetype: Explorer — freedom, discovery, and movement.  
Secondary archetype: Hero — reliability, courage, and problem-solving.

## Tone by Context
- Marketing site and ads: Inspiring and energetic; focus on benefits and simplicity.
- Product/UI microcopy: Direct and supportive; minimize friction and clarify next steps.
- Support and help center: Calm, empathetic, and step-by-step guidance.
- Social media: Conversational and community-minded; celebrate EV milestones and sustainable living.

## Key Messages
1. Reliable charging you can count on — clear station status and maintenance transparency.
2. Fast, clean energy — high-speed chargers and renewable energy matching roadmap.
3. Simple pricing — transparent rates with no surprises.
4. Everywhere you go — growing network coverage where drivers need it most.

## Value Proposition
Fast, reliable, and sustainable charging — made simple and transparent.

## Taglines
- Charge with confidence
- Powering every mile
- Fast. Clean. Everywhere.

## Messaging Pillars
- Reliability: “Up and ready when you are.”
- Simplicity: “Know the price, find a spot, start charging.”
- Sustainability: “Charging aligned with renewable energy goals.”
- Community: “Supporting drivers, fleets, and neighborhoods.”

## Language Do/Don't
Do:
- Use direct verbs: “Start,” “Find,” “Charge,” “Go.”
- Use inclusive, people-first language.
- Provide specific details when they reduce uncertainty (e.g., charger kW, wait times).

Avoid:
- Jargon that confuses non-experts.
- Overpromises regarding availability or speed.
- Fear-based framing.

## Examples
- Headline (site): “Fast, reliable charging. No guesswork.”
- CTA: “Find a station”
- Support snippet: “If the station is busy, we’ll suggest the closest available charger.”
