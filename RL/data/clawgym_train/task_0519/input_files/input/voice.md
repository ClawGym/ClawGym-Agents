Audience: clinicians, public health practitioners, and informed parents.
Tone: evidence-based, plain language, respectful, non-stigmatizing, and inclusive (use terms like "pregnant people" where appropriate). Avoid sensationalism and absolute claims.
Causality: prefer cautious phrasing (e.g., "is associated with", "may increase risk") rather than definitive causal language unless supported by strong evidence.
Clarity: aim for readable sentences (approximately 8th–10th grade level). Avoid jargon or briefly define it.
Equity: avoid blaming individuals; acknowledge social determinants where relevant.
Call-to-action: encourage learning, screening, or policy awareness rather than medical advice.
