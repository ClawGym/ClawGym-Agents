# Bursa & Uludağ: snow, silk, and thermal springs

I first learned to love Bursa in winter, when the city seems to slow down just enough for you to notice the steam rising from the baths and the snow dusting the chestnut trees. This draft is a bit rambly because I kept thinking about old LP assignments and the way I used to cram three neighborhoods into one morning. Anyway, I wandered between hammams in Çekirge, hopped the cable car for a quiet walk on Uludağ, and then got lured back into town by the scent of sizzling butter over döner. There’s also that beautiful hush in the caravanserais, where the silk stalls still trade stories as much as shawls. I should tighten this up for a newsletter that goes to folks who only have a few minutes over tea.

## Highlights
- Thermal baths in Çekirge and hamams
- Cable car up to Uludağ for winter walks
- Ottoman caravanserais and silk market in Koza Han
- Iskender kebab and candied chestnuts

## Notes
The funicular/cable car timing changes with the season; weekday mornings can be wonderfully quiet. Koza Han still has lovely light in the courtyard around 10 am.
