# Foreword

I came up in an era when cameras were heavier than the secrets we carried and the sets smelled of sawdust and fresh paint. We made pictures as if they were summer storms—sudden, electric, and gone before you knew it.

The applause fades, but the work endures. If there's any virtue in these old reels, it belongs as much to the crews and playwrights as to the faces in the frame.

## On Craft

I was never one for speeches between takes. I'd listen to the lights warming up and find my mark in the hum. Acting, to my mind, was mostly listening—listening to the other players, to the silence, to the doubt.
