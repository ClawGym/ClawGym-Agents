Hard constraints and planning notes for our Austria trip (July 12–21, 2026)

Trip skeleton and bases
- Max 3 bases on the Vienna → Salzburg → Innsbruck/Tyrol westbound rail corridor.
- We want to end smoothly in Munich with a last-night buffer on July 20 for our July 21 (~13:10) departure from MUC.
- Rail-first for all intercity moves; do not recommend driving within Vienna or central Salzburg.

Arrival and departure
- Arrival: Vienna (VIE) on Sunday, 2026-07-12 at ~09:20 (Austrian OS 088 from JFK).
- Departure: Munich (MUC) on Tuesday, 2026-07-21 at ~13:10 (Lufthansa LH 410 to JFK).
- Protect daylight and keep first day light due to Sunday trading hours and jet lag.

Accessibility and mobility
- One traveler (age 68) has low mobility: can walk ~10 minutes on flat ground; avoids long staircases and steep uphill/downhill segments.
- Elevators required at hotels; avoid long walks with luggage; short station-to-hotel transfers preferred.
- For mountain days, prioritize lift-accessible viewpoints with benches, restrooms, and straightforward egress; include clear low-mobility alternatives.

Activities and day structure
- Include exactly: 
  1) One relaxed Wachau day from Vienna (e.g., Krems/Dürnstein/Abbey context; season-aware boat optional).
  2) One Salzkammergut lake day that explicitly avoids Hallstatt crowd crunch; candidates include Fuschlsee, St. Gilgen/Wolfgangsee, or Mondsee.
  3) One easy Innsbruck-area lift day (e.g., Patscherkofel or Seegrube/Nordkette) with low-mobility paths and café/rest area.
- Start activities no earlier than 08:00 when possible; include midday rest or café time most days.
- Sunday and holiday closures awareness: plan city parks, open museums, and coffeehouses on Sundays and avoid relying on supermarkets for lunch.

Transport rules
- Intercity: default to ÖBB Railjet/RJX or equivalent; reserve seats in family-friendly configuration; avoid tight connections.
- City cores: no car use in Vienna or central Salzburg; rely on U-Bahn/tram/bus/walk with minimal stairs where feasible.
- Optional car variant: allowed only for a targeted lake/rural day if it clearly improves routing; if used, request automatic transmission, add a booster seat for the 7-year-old, and account for vignette, parking, and potential lake-road tolls. Pick-up/return near Salzburg Hbf preferred; same-day rental only.

- Avoid Hallstatt due to crowding; use earlier departures for popular lifts or lake boats where helpful.
- Build weather backups for all outdoor/lift days (e.g., museums, spa/thermal, covered attractions).

Budget and reservations
- Mid-range budget expectation; two rooms or a family apartment with AC.
- Request: clear reservation deadlines for key trains, hotels, and any mountain lifts/boats likely to sell out.
- Prefer refundable rates within reason but do not lose critical locations by waiting too long in July.

Food and timing

- Prefer early, family-friendly dinners; avoid long waits in tourist hotspots.
- Plan for bakery/café lunches; avoid long detours solely for dining.

Payment and practicalities

- Include brief tipping guidance for Austria and Germany.
- Provide emergency and safety notes, including mountain weather cautions and EU emergency numbers.

Miscellaneous
