# Review Draft: Classic Bouillabaisse

The bouillabaisse at Maison de la Mer deserves praise for its fidelity to Marseille’s original character. The broth was clear yet profound, the saffron used with restraint, and the rouille carried the precise warmth required to complete the bowl.

One hopes contemporary zeal does not compel chefs to garnish tradition with novelty for novelty’s sake. A dish perfected by generations asks only for care and continuity, not invention. The same should be said of prose.
