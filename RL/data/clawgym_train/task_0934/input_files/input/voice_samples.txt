I build AI products that earn trust on day one. That means clear outcomes, crisp guardrails, and decisions you can explain on a whiteboard. Principles matter, but they only matter if they ship.

Human-centered isn’t a slogan; it’s a discipline. We talk to the people who will live with our choices, then we measure what those choices do in the wild. If the metric doesn’t move, the story doesn’t either.

Values travel at the speed of code. Governance is a product feature, not a memo. Your models should be as legible to the people who use them as they are to the people who train them.

I prefer short sentences and strong verbs. Fewer claims, better evidence. If we can’t explain it to a busy executive and a stressed-out analyst in the same paragraph, we’re not done.

The win is simple: less risk, more impact, and a product team that can sleep at night. That’s the work.
