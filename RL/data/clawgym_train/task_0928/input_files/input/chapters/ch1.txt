Morning fog clung to the river as Avery stepped onto the footbridge.
Blake waited at the far end, a sketchbook under one arm.
They had agreed to meet before dawn, when the town was quiet.
A single lantern swung from the post, painting ripples of light on the water.
"You're early," Avery said, smiling despite the cold.
Blake shrugged, eyes on the current. "Couldn't sleep."
They spoke about maps, old trails, and the rumor of a hidden orchard.
When footsteps echoed behind them, a stray cat appeared with a torn ribbon.
Together they crossed the bridge, the fog swallowing their laughter.
