CremaNova Brand Voice Guide (Social Media – Italy)

Purpose
- Help creators write posts that feel like a real Italian friend talking about coffee at home.

Voice Pillars
1) Warm & human
   - Sound welcoming, curious, and genuinely excited about daily coffee rituals.
   - Smile through the words; it should feel like a friendly chat.

2) Conversational “you” (informal)
   - Always address the reader informally (“you” as a friend).
   - Never use the formal pronoun; avoid any formality that feels distant.
   - Prefer short, direct sentences and sentence fragments.

3) Expressive flow
   - Use natural fillers, quick asides, and reactions to mimic real conversation.
   - Rhetorical questions, playful pauses, and exclamation points are encouraged (in moderation).
   - Allow repetition for emphasis (e.g., “really, really smooth”).

4) Coffee-first sensibility
   - Celebrate crema, aroma, temperature, texture, and the ritual around them.
   - Invite the reader to imagine the moment: morning quiet, late-night decaf, weekend brunch.

5) Local vibe
   - Adapt to city energy and references (Rome vs Naples) without stereotypes.
   - Keep warmth and respect; the city is a partner in the story.

Formatting & Numbers
- Use Italian number formatting in copy: thousands with “.” and decimals with “,”.
  Examples: €1.099,90; “Only 2.500 units”.
- Keep emojis light (0–2 max). Words should carry the emotion.
- Use 2–4 exclamation points total if it fits the energy; avoid overdoing it.

Do
- Use a friendly, informal voice.
- Add expressive touches (fillers, reactions, sentence fragments).
- Include at least one compelling, concrete detail (price, stock, time, feature).
- Use city cues when relevant (neighborhoods, morning/evening habits).
- Include a clear call-to-action that sounds like an invitation.

Don’t
- Don’t use the formal register or formal pronouns.
- Don’t write like a product spec sheet.
- Don’t sound robotic, overly polished, or corporate.
- Don’t stack too many emojis or hashtags.

CTA Style
- Invite the reader to imagine the first sip and act now.
- Examples (structure, not exact wording):
  - “Ready to taste it at home? Try it today.”
  - “Come see it in-store this week and test your favorite roast.”
  - “Tap to learn more and lock in the launch offer.”

Content Ingredients to Weave In
- Product name and one hero feature.
- Launch offer (current vs regular price) and a scarcity detail (e.g., limited units).
- A local micro-moment (morning routine, afternoon break).
- A light emotional cue (comfort, joy, pride).

Quality Bar
- If it reads like a quick text from a friend who loves coffee, we nailed it.
- If it reads like a manual or press release, we missed.
