# PulseBoard Launch Brief

Product: PulseBoard — SMB analytics made simple.

One-liner:
PulseBoard pulls your key metrics into one clean board in under 15 minutes, alerts you when something moves, and gives you a daily digest you’ll actually read.

What it does:
- Connectors: Stripe, Shopify, WooCommerce, QuickBooks Online, Google Analytics, Facebook Ads, Google Ads. One-click auth, auto-sync hourly.
- Dashboards: Revenue, cash, CAC/LTV, cohort retention, marketing ROAS, inventory turns (retail), job margin (services).
- Alerts: Anomaly detection on revenue, cash balance, ad spend, conversion rate; delivered to Slack and email.
- Daily Digest: “PulsePoints” — a plain-english morning brief of changes vs yesterday and last week.
- Exports: Spreadsheet-friendly CSVs and a “Board-Ready” slide snapshot.
- Setup: Guided templates (“30-60-90 metrics”) and a Weekly Review checklist.

Audience (short):
- Owner-operators, operations managers, and fractional CFOs at 5–200 employee businesses who need clarity without hiring a data team.

Positioning:
- For SMBs who live in spreadsheets and too many dashboards, PulseBoard is the fastest way to see what matters every morning, fix issues before they snowball, and get back to work.

Differentiators:
- 15-minute setup from zero to daily digest.
- Alerts that point to the “why,” not just the “what.”
- Templates tuned for SMBs (e.g., cash runway gauge, blended ROAS sanity check).
- No data team required.

Brand pillars for content:
1) How-tos — practical walkthroughs that someone can try the same day.
2) Customer stories — real outcomes, numbers when allowed.
3) Founder journey — why we built it, tradeoffs, behind-the-scenes decisions.
4) Data literacy — plain-English explanations of common metrics and how to use them.
5) Industry commentary — grounded takes on SMB analytics trends.

Founding story notes (for 2+ posts):
- Started as a Saturday script after a friend missed a cash dip hidden across apps. That “near-miss” led to the first Slack alert prototype.
- We killed two “cool” features because they added 10 minutes to setup without improving decisions. Speed to first insight is the north star.
- Early customers taught us to translate metrics into actions (e.g., “Pause Channel X?” vs “ROAS down 18%”).

How-to angles (for 2+ posts):
- Set up a cash runway gauge in 10 minutes using QuickBooks and past burn.
- Build a weekly CEO dashboard: revenue trend, receivables, CAC payback, top driver.
- Create anomaly alerts for conversion rate dips before campaigns bleed budget.
- Connect Shopify + Ads to see blended ROAS and avoid silo bias.

Tone and style:
- Plainspoken, helpful, specific. Non-buzzy.
- Short sentences. Verbs over adjectives.
- Show numbers when possible, or concrete steps.
- Use questions to invite comments and examples from others.
- If referencing resources, say “link in comments” instead of including a URL in the body.

Do:
- Use real-world examples and simple math.
- Acknowledge tradeoffs and limits.
- Encourage dialogue: ask “How do you do X?”

Don’t:
- Say “disrupt,” “revolutionize,” “AI-powered” unless the mechanism matters.
- Overpromise or hide requirements.
- Drop links in the body. Use “link in comments.”

CTAs we like (directional):
- “How do you track this today?”
- “What metric surprised you last quarter?”
- “Want the template? Comment ‘template’ and I’ll share.”

Hashtag approach:
- 3–5 tags per post, mix broad (#SMB, #SmallBusiness, #Data) + specific (#CashFlow, #Ecommerce, #Operations, #FractionalCFO).

Reference cadence:
- One post per day for launch week.
- At least two founder-journey posts and at least two product how-tos.
- Alternate between practical and perspective to avoid “pitch fatigue.”

Proof points available:
- Median setup time in pilot: 14m 42s.
- First-week insight: alert caught a 27% conversion dip in paid search for a DTC brand; they paused a campaign and saved ~$2.1k that week.
- 9/10 pilot users kept the daily digest on after week two.

Voice checklist (use before shipping):
- Is it clear at a glance what the reader can do next?
- Is there at least one question that invites a comment?
- Can a time-strapped owner read this in 60–90 seconds?
- Would this help someone even if they never buy PulseBoard?

Brand sign-off:
- Use “PulseBoard” (CamelCase) on first mention, then “PulseBoard” or “we.”
- Avoid abbreviations like “PB” unless quoting a user.
- No emojis in headlines; optional sparing use in body if clarifying (e.g., “(template)” is preferred over 📄).
