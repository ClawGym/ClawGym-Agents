I’m writing this really late at night so this might sound rambling, but The Black Company felt like a rough, gritty march through shadows. It’s a journal style? Sort of, and sometimes it’s confusing (on purpose?) and also super cynical. I loved and hated how it kept me guessing and I kept rereading some bits because the narrator is unreliable (maybe?) and it’s all very bleak, bleak in a way that’s not just sad but like… honest? The humor is dry like bones (ha) and the action is punchy but sometimes I wasn’t sure who was talking!! There’s a lot of names and nicknames and I tripped over those, but the atmosphere pulled me along anyway. If you’re looking for shining heroes, nope, this is not that. But if you want mercenaries doing mercenary things, with a weird grace to it, this might be your kind of storm.
