# Pewter Knight Herald – Assembly & Paint Workflow

## Surface Prep
- Wash all metal parts in warm, soapy water and scrub with a soft toothbrush to remove mold release.
- Rinse thoroughly and dry. Degrease with 91% isopropyl alcohol on a lint-free cloth.
- Test-fit, then remove mold lines with needle files; de-burr with a sharp #11 blade.
- Lightly scuff glue contact points with 600–800 grit for better adhesion.

## Adhesives & Pinning
- Use medium-viscosity cyanoacrylate (CA) for metal-to-metal bonds; pin larger joints with 0.8 mm brass rod.
- Score both mating surfaces before applying CA. Use a slow-setting CA if you need adjustment time.
- Avoid plastic cement; it does not dissolve pewter and will not bond reliably.

## Priming
- Apply a self-etching primer rated for bare metal in 2–3 light coats.
- Allow a full 24-hour cure for maximum bite before masking or taping.
- Keep the can 6–8 inches away; start each pass off-part to minimize pooling.

## Paint Layers
- Build color with thin acrylic layers (1:1 to 1:2 paint:thinner). Let each coat dry before the next.
- Seal with an acrylic gloss coat before applying enamel or oil washes.

## Weathering Guidance
- Apply enamel pin washes after gloss. Avoid flooding unsealed acrylic.

## Safety Practices
- Spray primer in a well-ventilated area while wearing a respirator rated for organic vapors.

## Tools
- Needle files, pin vise with 0.8 mm bit, hobby knife (#11), CA glue, brass rod, etching primer, respirator.

## Pitfalls
- Heavy primer coats can soften detail.
- Plastic cement will fail on metal joints.
