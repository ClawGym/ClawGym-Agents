Weekly Client-Reporting Use Case

Context
- We produce a Monday-morning performance report for each client summarizing last week’s KPIs, notable changes, risks, and next steps.
- Audience: client executives (C-suite/VP) and account managers.
- Sources: analytics exports (ads/web), CRM snapshots, campaign change logs, internal notes.

Goals
- Accuracy first: numbers and deltas must match source exports.
- Clear narrative: executive-ready summary (8–12 sentences) with crisp chart annotations and concrete recommendations.
- Consistency: stable tone across clients; reuse a standard structure (Overview → Key Movements → Risks/Opportunities → Next Steps).
- Turnaround: end-to-end per client within 3 hours; all reports delivered by Monday 9:00 AM local time.

Constraints & Budget
- PII: raw CRM data and some analytics exports contain names, emails, and phone numbers. Any task that touches raw PII must run on a local/on‑prem model only. No cloud logging or training on our data.
- Budget: keep routine steps on low cost_score models; reserve high cost_score and high power_score models for analysis-heavy sections only when warranted.
- Practical targets:
  - Default for low/medium complexity: models with cost_score ≤ 2.
  - Allow up to cost_score 6–7 for at most two high-impact steps per client (deep analysis, risk/opportunity synthesis).
  - Weekly cost control: stay within a combined cost_score envelope of ~24 across the workflow per client.

Quality Bar
- Every numeric claim must be checked against the exports (add a dedicated fact-check step).
- Use a compact evaluation rubric with weighted criteria: factual accuracy, relevance to client goals, clarity, actionability, and tone alignment.
- Keep auditability: save the prompt, sources used, and a short reasoning note per section.

Workflow Overview (desired)
1) Collect KPIs from exports (PII present) → local model assists with parsing/structuring only.
2) First narrative draft (no PII) → economical general model for a clean first pass.
3) Deeper risk/opportunity analysis (no PII) → escalate to a stronger reasoning model if trends are ambiguous or high-impact.
4) Chart text and headlines (no PII) → lightweight model.
5) Fact-check numbers (PII present) → local model verifies against exports.
6) Client email draft (no PII) → economical writing-focused model; final human pass.

Success Criteria
- Numbers correct and reconciled; trends interpreted with appropriate caution.
- Recommendations specific, measurable, and tied to observed drivers.
- Tone: concise, executive-friendly, and consistent with our brand voice.
- Delivery on time within budget envelope.
