Project: Desk Vent Controller v0.3 (Arduino Uno R3)

Goal:
- Read ambient temperature/humidity/pressure from a BME280 sensor over I2C
- Drive a small 5V DC motor (vent fan) via a transistor
- Position an SG90 micro servo to open/close a flap (two positions)
- Toggle modes with a single pushbutton
- Indicate status with an onboard LED
- Optional: send simple status over Bluetooth (HC-05) while also using the Serial Monitor for debugging

Current status (why I’m asking for help):
- The build is flaky. The Uno resets randomly, especially when the servo moves or when the fan spins up.
- The Serial Monitor shows garbage characters unless I keep changing the baud; sometimes it only shows gibberish at 115200.
- The BME280 sometimes isn’t detected by the I2C scanner; when it is detected, readings occasionally return NAN.
- The pushbutton input flickers (reads pressed when untouched) and is inconsistent.
- The LED once got very hot and died; I replaced it, but it’s extremely bright when on.
- Uploads sometimes fail unless I unplug the HC-05 from pins 0/1.
- The sketch feels unresponsive because of long pauses in the loop; I used delay() for timing.
- Touching or moving wires affects the readings and behavior.

Environment:
- Arduino Uno R3 powered via laptop USB
- Breadboard and ~30 cm male-male jumpers
- No shielding/enclosure yet
- Serial Monitor typically set to 115200 (I sometimes forget to match it to the sketch)

What I’m trying to figure out:
- A thorough diagnosis of the hardware and software problems
- A concrete fix plan (power, wiring, code)
- How to refactor the code to non-blocking timing and stabilize the inputs
