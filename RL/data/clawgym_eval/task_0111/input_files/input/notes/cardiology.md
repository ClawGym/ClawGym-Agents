Q: First-line pharmacologic therapy for stable angina?
A: Beta-blockers are first-line; nitrates for acute relief; add CCBs if persistent symptoms; risk factor modification.

Q: EKG changes consistent with STEMI?
A: New ST-segment elevation in two or more anatomically contiguous leads with reciprocal ST depression.

Q: Diagnostic murmur of aortic stenosis?
A: Harsh crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at right upper sternal border, radiates to carotids; delayed carotid upstroke.
