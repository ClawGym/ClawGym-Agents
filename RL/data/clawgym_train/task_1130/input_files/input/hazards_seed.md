Draft hazard notes for Fresh Fork Meals (ready-to-eat, mixed menu including poultry)

Process steps and hazards

Receiving
- Biological: Growth of pathogens if TCS foods delivered above 41°F; contaminated raw poultry (Salmonella, Campylobacter); Listeria in RTE dairy.
- Chemical: Residual pesticides on produce; allergen mislabeling from supplier substitutions; sanitizer residue on reusable totes.
- Physical: Foreign objects (stones in produce, broken pallet wood, metal fragments); damaged packaging.
- Notes: Check supplier temps; reject above limits. Verify COAs when available. Record receiving temps.

Storage (cold holding)
- Biological: Pathogen growth if cooler >41°F/5°C; cross-contamination between raw poultry and RTE items.
- Chemical: Allergen cross-contact due to shared storage without segregation; cleaning chemical storage near ingredients.
- Physical: Brittle plastic shards from cracked bins; glass breakage from jars.
- Notes: Store raw below RTE; color-coded bins; temperature logs for cold holding.

Prep
- Biological: Cross-contamination from raw to RTE surfaces; improper hand hygiene leading to Staph/Norovirus risk.
- Chemical: Sanitizer residues above label directions; mis-labeled ingredients leading to allergen exposure.
- Physical: Hard fragments from utensils, twist ties, broken scoops.
- Notes: Dedicated cutting boards; verify sanitizer concentration with test strips; strict allergen handling.

Cook
- Biological: Undercooking poultry (Salmonella), ground meats (E. coli O157:H7); uneven heating in batch cooking.
- Chemical: Excessive oil smoke residues; improper use of non-food-grade materials.
- Physical: Bone fragments in cooked proteins.
- Notes: Verify internal temps with calibrated thermometer, document cook temps.

Cool
- Biological: Spore germination/growth (Clostridium perfringens, Bacillus cereus) if cooling is too slow; Listeria risk post-cook.
- Chemical: Ice bath contamination if water not changed; incorrect sanitizer residue on pans.
- Physical: Pan foil fragments; broken container edges.
- Notes: Cool 135°F→70°F in 2 hours; 70°F→41°F in 4 hours; use shallow pans/ice bath; cover/lid after 41°F.

Package
- Biological: Post-lethality contamination (Listeria) if packaging area/surfaces not controlled.
- Chemical: Allergen label omissions; incorrect ingredient lists.
- Physical: Label backing or plastic fragments; hair, personal effects.
- Notes: Verify label templates; allergen bolding for Big 9; metal detection not in place (small scale) — emphasize visual checks.

Ship/Dispatch
- Biological: Temperature abuse during transport; inadequate cold packs leading to >41°F before handoff.
- Chemical: Allergen cross-contact in delivery totes.
- Physical: Cracked tote plastic; broken gel packs.
- Notes: Pre-chill totes; record dispatch temp; verify first-drop temp; keep under 41°F for cold items.

Potential CCPs and controls (draft)
- Cold holding ≤41°F/5°C (FDA Food Code 3-501.16).
- Cooking temps per product (e.g., poultry 165°F/74°C for 15 sec; Food Code 3-401.11).
- Cooling: 135°F→70°F in 2 hr; 70°F→41°F in 4 hr (Food Code 3-501.14).
- Receiving TCS foods at ≤41°F (Food Code 3-202.11).
- Monitoring: calibrated probe thermometer; ice-point checks; thermometer sanitation; logs kept daily.
- Corrective action (examples): reheat to safe temp, rapid cool, discard if limits grossly exceeded, document deviation.
