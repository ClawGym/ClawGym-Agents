# NSW Foreign Investor Residential Property Guide (local excerpt)

State: NSW

## Investor Categories
- Non-resident foreign individual: A person who is not ordinarily resident in Australia.
- Resident and temporary residents are regulated differently.

## FIRB Approval
- FIRB approval is required for non-resident individuals purchasing new residential property, including off-the-plan apartments.
- Established (second-hand) dwellings are generally not permitted for non-resident buyers.

## Foreign Buyer Surcharges (Rates)
- Foreign purchaser duty surcharge: 8% of the dutiable value for residential property, payable in addition to base transfer (stamp) duty.

## Land Tax Surcharge (Rates)
- Land tax surcharge for foreign persons: 2% annually on taxable land value (where applicable).

## Prohibited Property Types
- Established residential dwellings (second-hand) for non-resident individuals.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance
- May include civil penalties up to AUD 55,500 per breach and possible forced disposal of the property. Interest and additional charges may apply.

## Date-based Conditions
- Rates and thresholds are subject to change. The above rates are stated as at 2024-06-30. Confirm applicability based on the contract exchange date.
