Master Services Agreement — Excerpt (DocuFlux AI, Inc. / “Vendor”) — FluxExtract
Effective Date: 2026-02-01

SECTION 7 — CONFIDENTIALITY
7.1 Each party will protect Confidential Information using reasonable care. Confidentiality obligations survive for five (5) years after disclosure.

SECTION 8 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY; OUTPUTS
8.1 Customer Content and Outputs. As between the parties, Customer owns (a) all Customer Content, and (b) the structured data and documents generated by FluxExtract specifically for Customer (“Outputs”).
8.2 Vendor Technology. Vendor retains all rights, title, and interest in and to the Services, models, software, and any improvements, enhancements, and learnings thereto, including those resulting from use of the Services, provided that Vendor will not use Customer Content to train foundation models except as expressly permitted in the DPA. Vendor may use aggregated, de-identified information to improve the Services.

SECTION 9 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
9.1 Cap. EXCEPT FOR CUSTOMER’S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS, EACH PARTY’S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.
9.2 Exclusion of Damages. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, OR DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY.
9.3 Scope. THE CAP IN SECTION 9.1 APPLIES TO ALL CLAIMS, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION CLAIMS ARISING FROM BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, DATA SECURITY INCIDENTS, OR INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY INFRINGEMENT.

SECTION 10 — INDEMNIFICATION
10.1 Vendor Indemnity. Vendor will defend Customer against any third-party claim alleging that the Services, as provided by Vendor, infringe a third party’s intellectual property rights, and pay final judgments or settlements approved by Vendor. Vendor’s obligations exclude claims arising from (a) Customer Content, (b) combinations with items not provided by Vendor, (c) Customer’s use in violation of Documentation, or (d) open source components. Vendor’s obligations are Customer’s sole remedy for such claims.
10.2 Customer Indemnity. Customer will defend Vendor against third-party claims arising from Customer Content or use of the Services in violation of law.
10.3 Mitigation. In the event of an infringement claim, Vendor may (i) modify the Services, (ii) procure a license, or (iii) terminate the affected Services and refund any prepaid, unused fees. Customer must cease use of the affected Services upon notice.

SECTION 12 — TERM AND TERMINATION
12.1 Term. Initial term is twelve (12) months. Thereafter, this Agreement will automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the current term.
12.2 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement for material breach if the breach remains uncured thirty (30) days after written notice.
12.3 Convenience. No termination for convenience. Early termination only as permitted in Sections 10.3 and 12.2.

SECTION 13 — SERVICE LEVELS; CREDITS
13.1 Uptime Commitment. Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide 99.5% monthly uptime.
13.2 Credits. If uptime falls below the commitment, Customer is entitled to service credits per the Pricing & Terms schedule. SERVICE CREDITS ARE CUSTOMER’S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDY FOR ANY SERVICE LEVEL FAILURES.
13.3 Exclusions. Downtime due to scheduled maintenance and third-party cloud provider outages is excluded from uptime calculations.

SECTION 14 — DATA PROTECTION; DPA
14.1 DPA. The Data Processing Addendum (“DPA”) forms part of this Agreement and includes Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable. Vendor acts as processor; Customer acts as controller.
14.2 Deletion & Return. Upon termination or expiration, Vendor will delete Customer Content within thirty (30) days, with backups overwritten within sixty (60) days. Upon request within the Term, Customer may export Customer Content and Outputs via API or bulk export.
14.3 Audit. Customer’s audit rights are satisfied by Vendor’s provision of a SOC 2 report (or executive summary) and responses to a reasonable security questionnaire no more than once annually. No on-site audits without Vendor’s consent.
14.4 Sub-Processors. Vendor may engage sub-processors listed in its Sub-Processor List, subject to the DPA. Vendor will provide notice of material changes at least thirty (30) days in advance when required.

SECTION 15 — DISCLAIMERS
15.1 AI Output Disclaimer. AI-generated outputs may contain inaccuracies or hallucinations; Customer is responsible for verifying Outputs before reliance. Vendor disclaims liability for errors in Outputs except as limited by Section 9.

GOVERNING LAW: Delaware, USA. VENUE: State and Federal courts located in Delaware.
