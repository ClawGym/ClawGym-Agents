Community Programming Committee Brief
Program: Motorsports 101 – Watching Without the Jargon
Purpose: Provide non‑fans with a friendly framing for understanding what to watch for in any race, with emphasis on safety, fair play, and simple context.
Audience: Adults and teens (general public). Target reading level: ~6th–8th grade. Tone: neutral, plain language. Avoid brand marketing and insider jargon.
Desired resource types: Plain‑language glossaries, beginner guides, safety explainers, general “how to watch” pieces from official bodies, public media, museums, universities, or similar educational sources.
Non‑negotiables for selection: Must be free to access (no paywall/sign‑in), introductory in scope, and not focused on betting/gambling or brand promotion. Avoid highly technical/engineering deep dives.
Ranking criteria (weights):
- Authority (40%): Prefer official governing bodies, public broadcasters, museums, universities, or recognized educational organizations over fan blogs or brand marketing.
- Clarity (30%): Plain language, minimal jargon, concise explanations suitable for non‑fans.
- Relevance (20%): Broad overview or “how to watch” guidance applicable across series; not niche.
- Accessibility (10%): Free access, readable on desktop, minimal ads/popups.
Scoring: For each candidate, assign 1–5 for authority, clarity, relevance, accessibility using the above definitions.
Weighted score formula: 0.4*authority + 0.3*clarity + 0.2*relevance + 0.1*accessibility.
