Agenda:
- Establish framing pillars for the book: bio-abundance, equity, governance
- CRISPR literacy and community synthetic biology

Notes:
We explored how biotechnology can cultivate a shared utopia by expanding capability and care. The group agreed that our utopia must be grounded in equitable access and robust governance. CRISPR came up repeatedly as a tangible toolkit: cooperatives offering CRISPR-enabled screening and community education can demystify biotechnology. We also noted that synthetic biology, when open-source and community-led, can accelerate learning while keeping safety paramount.

The bioeconomy angle resonated: local biomanufacturing hubs can shorten supply chains and create bio-abundance. Longevity surfaced as a hopeful vector of wellbeing if equity is baked in from the start. We reiterated governance as not a brake but a steering wheel for utopia.

Decisions:
- Decision: Adopt the triad "bio-abundance, equity, governance" as the book's framing pillars.
- Decision: Open the first chapter with CRISPR-enabled public health cooperatives to ground the utopia in practical biotechnology.
- Decision: Gather case studies on community biomanufacturing as evidence of a flourishing bioeconomy.
