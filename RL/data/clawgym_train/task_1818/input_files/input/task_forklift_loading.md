# Task: Forklift Loading and Unloading at Docks

Scope: Movement of palletized goods between warehouse staging areas, trailers at shipping/receiving docks, and cold storage, using sit-down LP-powered forklifts.

Assumptions:
- Dock levelers and vehicle restraints are available but may not always be engaged.
- Aisles are shared (mixed traffic) in some zones near Shipping and Receiving.
- Operators perform pre-shift inspections and post-operation shutdowns.

Task Steps:
1. Pre-Shift Inspection and Start-Up
   - Conduct PIT pre-use checklist (brakes, horn, seatbelt, mast, forks, tires, LP tank leaks).
   - Install/verify seatbelt; start unit; check for leaks and alarms.
2. Approach Dock/Trailer and Prepare for Entry
   - Verify dock lock engaged and wheel chocks (if required).
   - Inspect trailer condition and floor integrity; open trailer doors if safe.
   - Lower forks, sound horn at intersections, and maintain pedestrian awareness.
3. Load/Unload Pallets
   - Enter trailer at walking speed; maintain three-points of contact when mounting/dismounting.
   - Pick/place pallet loads; maintain proper mast tilt and load height; avoid overstacking.
   - Use spotter if visibility obstructed.
4. Travel to Staging/Storage and Park
   - Travel with load lowered below axle height; obey aisle speed limits and stop signs.
   - Maintain clear distance from pedestrians; use designated aisles.
   - Park in designated area; lower forks to floor; neutralize controls; set parking brake; shut down and close LP tank.

Common Hazards Noted by Supervisors:
- Trailer creep when vehicle restraint not engaged.
- Mixed pedestrian/forklift traffic at dock intersections.
- Instability from high-stacked or poorly wrapped pallets.
- Reduced visibility in cold storage due to fog/condensation.
