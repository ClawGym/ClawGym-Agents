# SkillBazaar Policy & Business Model Overview

Last updated: 2026-03-31

This document summarizes SkillBazaar’s revenue model, discovery policies, publication workflow, and enforcement approach for third‑party agent skills.

## Revenue Model

- Skill monetization: Publishers may price skills as paid or free. For paid skills, SkillBazaar processes transactions and settles revenue to publishers weekly.
- Marketplace take rate: SkillBazaar earns a 15% per-download revenue share on paid skills. In addition, the platform collects a 3% processing fee on in‑skill purchases.
- Discovery monetization: Publishers may optionally purchase premium placement fees for increased visibility in category pages and search results. Sponsored placement is clearly labeled “Promoted,” and inventory is auctioned via pay-to-rank bidding windows that refresh daily.
- Refunds and chargebacks: Chargebacks are netted from publisher payouts.

## Discovery & Ranking

- Ranking signals: Recent engagement, rating velocity, install conversion, and policy compliance status. Promoted listings (via premium placement fees) are displayed in a dedicated carousel above organic results.
- Editorial spotlights: Periodic staff picks based on quality benchmarks and user satisfaction.

## Publication & Review

- Workflow: New submissions undergo automated static analysis, sandboxed functional checks, and human review for safety-sensitive capabilities.
- SLA targets: Standard queue aims for initial decision within 3–5 business days depending on workload.
- Fast-track option: Elite and Strategic partners have access to a fast-track review lane with 24–48 hour targets during business days. Priority review may be temporarily suspended during major incidents.
- Pre-publication requirements: Identity verification and a working demo are required for new publisher accounts.

## Enforcement Policy

- Policy framework: Content rules prohibit deceptive descriptions, undisclosed data exfiltration, and unsafe escalation paths.
- Tiered enforcement: Violations are categorized as Minor, Major, or Critical. Repeat offenses escalate consequences.
- Exemptions: For Elite and Strategic partners, first Minor violations within a rolling 90‑day window may qualify for corrective‑action warnings without immediate delisting if user harm was not substantiated.
- Appeals: Publishers may file an appeal within 14 days of enforcement actions for re‑review by a separate panel.

## Publisher Tiers

- Trusted publisher program: Publishers demonstrating sustained compliance and high user satisfaction may be invited to the Trusted Publisher track, unlocking reduced verification friction for routine updates.
- Revenue-tier benefits: Publishers exceeding annual revenue thresholds may be eligible for additional account support and fast-track scheduling during peak periods, subject to capacity.
- Expectations: All tiers must maintain accurate capability descriptions and disclose data access scopes.

## Transparency & Reporting

- Audit trail: Skill version history and change logs are retained. Public enforcement summaries are published for Major and Critical cases; Minor cases are summarized quarterly in aggregate.
- Data access: Anonymized marketplace statistics and reviewer backlog indicators may be shared in periodic trust reports.

By participating in SkillBazaar, publishers agree to the Business Terms and Policy Guidelines. Questions can be directed to compliance@skillbazaar.example.
