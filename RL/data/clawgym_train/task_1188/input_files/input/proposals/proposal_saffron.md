Title: Rivers of Story: Comparative Pathways in Ancient Near Eastern and Mediterranean Myths
Scholar: Dr. Arjun Saffron

Approach
Using comparative philology and historical diffusion models, this project traces the movement and transformation of mythic motifs across Mesopotamian, Egyptian, Levantine, and early Greek traditions. It emphasizes how ideas travel through contact, translation, and adaptation.

Corpus
Epic of Gilgamesh and related Mesopotamian materials (e.g., Enuma Elish), Egyptian cosmogonies, Ugaritic texts (Baal Cycle), and comparative analysis with Homeric hymns and early Greek sources.

Methods
Textual comparison across languages; motif cataloging; linguistic evidence for transmission; network analysis visualizing intertextual pathways and motif diffusion.

Deliverables
A 300-page monograph, 10 interpretive diagrams, and an online appendix providing an annotated motif dataset (CSV) for readers to explore cross-cultural links.

Timeline
12 months to deliver a polished manuscript and dataset suitable for production.

Notes
The breadth offers a strong cross-cultural perspective; philological detail increases scholarly rigor and may require careful framing for general readers.
