Title: Riders Are Voting With Their Feet
Author: RiderVoice
Date: January 20, 2025

When the city jacked up fares to $3.00 last July, riders felt the sting immediately. I spoke to neighbors who just stopped taking the bus because it “wasn’t worth it anymore.” The message is obvious: higher fares drive people away, and we saw ridership collapse in the second half of 2024.

Some officials point to complicated spreadsheets to claim things are fine. But common sense says otherwise. People live on tight budgets—raise the fare, lose the riders. It’s that simple. We should freeze any further fare increases until at least 2026 to stop the bleeding.

Don’t be fooled by the hype around “green” buses either. Once you include battery manufacturing, electric buses actually emit more CO2 than diesel overall. These shiny new vehicles are window dressing—expensive and not truly better for the planet.

The real solution is keeping transit affordable and practical. Cut the fares, keep the riders, and forget the flashy buses.
