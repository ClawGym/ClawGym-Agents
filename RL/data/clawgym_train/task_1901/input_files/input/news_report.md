Metrovale Times
City transit marks tenth straight month of growth, eyes greener fleet

Publication date: April 16, 2025
Reporter: Dana Kincaid

Metrovale’s public transit system posted robust numbers last year, with officials reporting 132 million trips in 2024—about a 10% increase from 2023’s 120 million. The momentum appears to be continuing, as preliminary figures show Q1 2025 ridership at roughly 34 million, up from 31.5 million in Q1 2024.

A mid-year fare adjustment raised the base adult fare to $2.75 on July 1, 2024. Discounted passes for low-income riders and seniors were unchanged. Despite concerns, transit leaders say usage continued to climb through late 2024, citing sustained monthly gains.

The city’s ongoing fleet transition also drew attention. Officials say the newer battery-electric buses have “roughly a one-third lower” lifecycle carbon footprint than the old diesel models when accounting for Metrovale’s electricity mix, consistent with independent reviews that generally find a 30–38% reduction under grids similar to Metrovale’s (about 45% renewable). Tailpipe emissions are eliminated on electric vehicles, though total lifecycle emissions include electricity generation and battery manufacturing.

Transit advocates remain divided on how fares affect ridership, but current data point to steady growth into 2025.
