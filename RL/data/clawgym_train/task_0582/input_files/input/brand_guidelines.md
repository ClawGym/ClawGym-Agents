PulseHub Brand Guidelines — Voice and Visuals

About PulseHub
- Product: Workflow automation for modern operations teams. Centralizes tasks, automations, and integrations to keep work moving.
- Audience: Ops leads, product managers, and ICs who value clarity and momentum.

Voice and Tone
- Core tone: Calm, confident, and helpful. Friendly without being cute.
- Style: Plain English, short sentences, zero blame. Empathetic and action-oriented.
- Personality: Pragmatic, reliable, reassuring. Lightly optimistic.
- Grammar:
  - Use contractions (“we’re”, “you’ll”) to sound human.
  - Prefer sentence case for body and links. Title Case for headlines.
  - Avoid exclamation marks and jargon.
- On errors (including 404s):
  - Acknowledge the issue.
  - Offer choices (search + popular destinations).
  - Keep copy concise and kind.
  - Never blame the user.

404 Messaging Principles
- Lead with a clear headline that doesn’t over-explain.
- Provide a short, empathetic message that invites action.
- Include:
  - Primary CTA to a high-success path (Home, Product, or Docs).
  - Secondary CTA to Support or Status (if relevant).
  - Search input with clear guidance.
  - A short list of popular pages.
- Avoid auto-redirects from a 404 page. Let users choose where to go.
- Optional: Show similar links if a typo is likely.

CTA and Copy Conventions
- Buttons: Title Case, clear verbs.
- Good examples:
  - “Explore Features”
  - “Visit Docs”
  - “View Pricing”
  - “Contact Support”
  - “Check System Status”
- Keep CTA text under 3 words when possible; avoid vague labels.
- Use “you” voice and frame outcomes (“Find what you need”).

Visual Identity
- Colors:
  - Primary: Midnight Navy #0B1F3B (headers, text on light backgrounds)
  - Accent: Electric Indigo #4F46E5 (primary actions)
  - Support: Mint #2DD4BF (success accents, subtle highlights)
  - Neutral: Slate Gray #64748B (secondary text)
  - Background: White #FFFFFF / Off-White #F8FAFC
- Typography:
  - Headings: Inter, semibold — clear, legible
  - Body: Inter regular
  - Code/mono snippets (docs): JetBrains Mono or Menlo
- Spacing and Layout:
  - Generous white space. Clear visual hierarchy.
  - Maintain site header and footer on the 404 so users feel “at home.”
- Buttons:
  - Primary: Electric Indigo background, white text, 8px radius, 16px vertical padding.
  - Secondary: Outline Indigo or text link style.
- Links:
  - Indigo color with underline on hover; clear focus outline for accessibility.

Accessibility
- WCAG AA minimum contrast. Keyboard focus visible on all interactive elements.
- ARIA roles and landmarks: header, main, nav, search, footer.
- Provide descriptive labels for search and CTAs.

Search Guidance (404)
- Placeholder: “Search docs, features, and help”
- Help text: “Try product, pricing, docs, or a specific integration.”

Brand Do/Don’t
- Do: keep copy short, empathize, provide clear next steps, keep site chrome consistent.
- Don’t: auto-redirect, overload with links, use blamey or jokey tone.

Recommended Recovery Destinations (typical)
- Home, Product, Features, Pricing, Docs (and API), Integrations, Support, Status, Blog, Changelog, Security.
Use the canonical URLs from the site structure.

Sample 404 Copy Snippets (for tone reference)
- Headline: “Page not found”
- Message: “We couldn’t find that page, but we can help you get back on track.”
- Primary CTA: “Explore Features”
- Secondary CTA: “Visit Docs”
