Night settled over the orchard as wind threaded the branches.
Casey reached the clearing first, placing stones in a rough circle.
Blake arrived carrying a coil of rope and a battered field guide.
The map's margin notes matched the trees, row by row.
In the hush, Casey confessed a fear of leaving the town behind.
Blake answered with a story of getting lost and choosing to keep walking.
Together they marked the path home and the place to begin again.
