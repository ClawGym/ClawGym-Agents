# Z-Pinch Basics in the Lab and in Space

A z-pinch uses a current-driven magnetic pressure to radially compress a plasma column. While z-pinches are famous in laboratory fusion research, the same physics gives intuition for plasma pinches in astrophysical environments. This short primer outlines key stability considerations and why plasma pinches can form thin, bright structures.

Hannes Alfven’s legacy permeates these concepts, reminding us to respect the electromagnetic nature of plasmas when interpreting both laboratory and space data.
