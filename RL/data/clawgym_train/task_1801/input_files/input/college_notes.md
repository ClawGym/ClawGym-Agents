# Academic Support
- Writing Center: One-on-one writing help for essays and research papers; good to book early near midterms.
- Peer Tutoring: Subject-specific tutoring; often evenings; book ahead.
- Office Hours: Professors expect students to come with questions; best way to clarify tricky topics.

# Wellness
- Counseling Services: Confidential counseling; short-term support and referrals; booking required.
- Student Health: Routine care and advice; good for non-emergency questions.
- Food Pantry: Free groceries and basic supplies for students who need extra help.

# Money Basics
- Track expenses weekly to avoid surprises; simple spreadsheet or notes app works.
- Build a small emergency cushion (even $10–$20/month adds up).
- Look for free campus events (movies, clubs, rec center) before spending.

# Routines
- Sunday 20-minute planning: look at classes, deadlines, work hours.
- Midweek check-in text with family (keep it short and supportive).
- Laundry schedule: pick one consistent day/time to avoid weekend rush.
- Daily 20-minute movement (walk, stretch, or gym) to boost energy.
- Eat one produce item and hydrate in the afternoon slump.
- Use a simple to-do list with 3 priorities per day.
