## hydration_note
Team, a friendly reminder about hydration because it’s a low-effort win that makes a real difference to performance and recovery. Aim for 2–3 litres per day, adjusting for the heat and your training load. On race day, 500 ml water between warm-up and first heat keeps things simple and effective without feeling heavy. Please avoid energy drinks on race day; they tend to spike and crash, and we’ve seen focus dip late in the session when they’re used. If you’re training or competing in the sun for more than 60 minutes, include electrolytes to replace what you lose in sweat. None of this is meant to be complicated—keep a bottle handy, sip through the day, and monitor urine colour as a basic check. Let’s stay on top of the small things.
