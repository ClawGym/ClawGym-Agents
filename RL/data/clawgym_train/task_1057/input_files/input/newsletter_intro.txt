Hello dear readers! I’m finally sitting down to gather a few thoughts from recent wanderings, and I realize this intro always runs long because I get sentimental about paths taken and friends made along the way. After fifteen years filing pieces for Lonely Planet, I’m enjoying the luxury of slower notes and unhurried mornings. This week’s drafts are still rough, but they carry the scent of pine, snow, and grilled butter. If you’re skimming over breakfast, I promise to keep things brief, but also I want to invite you to linger where you can. There’s a little Bursa chill, a little Aegean salt, and a reminder that travel can be as small as a walk to the ferry. Thanks for being here; your messages keep me writing and wandering, even when Istanbul’s traffic tries to have the last word.
