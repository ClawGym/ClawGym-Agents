Hello friends and the simply curious,

I have been thinking for years about where the world we live in begins and where the fictions we carry around with us end, and it keeps turning out that there is no hard line at all. This course is my attempt to invite you into that tangle. We will be reading short things that bend and blend, and we will also be walking, noticing, writing, and occasionally making small, playful disturbances in how we pay attention to what is real. I am excited (and admittedly a little overexcited) about the experiments, which will be gentle and low-stakes, but also bracing in the way a cold morning is bracing when you step outside and the air says, Wake up.

You will write weekly reflections that are less about getting the right answer and more about carrying a thread from observation into invention. We will talk about libraries that never end, cities that mirror themselves, and the ways ethics sneak into stories. If you have ever felt the world tilt a degree when you read a footnote or glance in a window after dark, this class is for you. Bring a notebook you don’t mind messing up, shoes for wandering, and a willingness to be honest about what you see and what you make up.
